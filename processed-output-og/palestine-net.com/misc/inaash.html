About In'aash: Our Goals

To improve the living environment in the Palestinian refugee camps. Tostimulate the development of women, children, and youth and motivate theirprogress. To raise the cultural, educational and economic standing of therefugees in the Palestinian Camps in Lebanon.

Since its founding in 1969, the Association has started several projects,the expenses of which have been covered partly by generous donations butmainly by the income generated from the marketing and selling of thebeautiful Palestinian hand embroidered products symbolized mainly by: TheCushion.

Our cushions are exquisitely embroidered with beautiful designs derivedfrom the specific patterns and motifs found on the different villagersdresses, associated with specific Palestinian villages. Just as thesedresses, still worn today have acquired a new significance as expressionsof a Palestinian National Identity, the cushion, in its turn, has becomeanother symbol of a nation's National Heritage.

In the context of a dispersed and fragmented society, in an era wheretradition and culture have become extremely precious, one means of savingsome of the values of a nation has become the preserving, reproducing andpromoting of the heritage of the Traditional Palestinian Embroidery.

The Association for the Development of Palestinian Camps is a Lebanesesocial and cultural association founded in 1969 by a group of Lebanese &Palestinian ladies. These ladies foresaw the necessity of preserving theheritage of traditional Palestinian embroidery which would also bringmeans of financial support to many needy families in the camps. Ourcurrent projects are administered by several dedicated employees under theguidance and advice of a group of volunteers who are usually members ofthe General Assembly of the elected Board of Directors.

In'aash Projects

The Embroidery Center :
Objectives:
Our main income generating project aiming towards promotingPalestinian embroidery by distributing items to women in differentcamps on a "pay by piece" basis.
Administration: Bikhaazi and Charif bldg
Manara, Ras Beirut
Locations:Tareek el Jadidah
Ain el Hilwi camp
Baalbeck camp
Rashidieh camp
Beneficiaries: 2600 women workers

The Kindergartens :
Objectives:
Provide preschool education for children between the ages3-5 years.
Locations:Near Al Jaleel Camp -Baalbeck
Borj El Barajneh Camp - Beirut
Mar Elias Camp - Beirut
Beneficiaries: 276 children

The Revolving Fund :
Objectives:
Helping small skilled artisans with small loans to start abusiness on a "pay back- basis".
Beneficiaries:250 skilled men and women

Mar Elias Cultural Center "
Objectives:
Providing a meeting place for the young generation of the camp toexercise different cultural and entertaining activities such assports, drawing, chess, acting, teaching illiterate people and others.Also included is a library and an evening school.
Location:Mar Elias Camp, Beirut.
Beneficiaries:450 young boys, girls and women.

Auto Mechanics Training Center :
Objectives:
Training young men between 18-25 years.
Location:Borj El Barajneh Camp.
Beneficiaries: 30 students every six months.

The Old Age Project
Objectives:
Giving monthly financial support to needy elderly peoplein the different camps.
Location: The project is administered from the Ras Beirut maincenter.
Beneficiaries: 55 old men and women above 60 years of age.
