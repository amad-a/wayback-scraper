The first twinning agreement between a Romanian and a Palestinian City

The Jericho Municipality signs a twinning agreement with the Municipality of Yache in Romania

The Mayor of Jericho Mr. Abdel-Karim Sider Concluded an official visit to Romania where he signed a twinning agreement together with the Municipality of Yache in Romania.

The agreement signing ceremony was carried out with the presence of The Ambassador of Palestine in Romania Mr. Fouad Al- Bitar, head of the Palestinian writer society Mrs. Hanan Awad, head of the Center of Jerusalem, and the delegation escorting the Mayor of Jericho, Dr. Ahmad Al Ju’ba City council member, and director of Public Relations in the Jericho Municipality Ms. Weam Erekat.

Mr. Sider declared that this agreement is considered a twinning between the two communities in Culture and history especially after the agreement included issues such as the cultural and commercial exchange. From the other hand, this agreement resulted in the procurement of 4 study grants for the students of Jericho in the field of medicine and engineering. Furthermore, the possibility of a delegation from Jericho to carry out a visit to Yache in order to find out the chances of making investments and conduct business deals.

The Mayor of Jericho gave a detailed illustration in the signing ceremony about the situation in Jericho and the tragic circumstances resulting from the unjustified Israeli measures.

On his part, Mr. Flaicher the Mayor of Yache, welcomed the Palestinian delegation and stressing on the importance of peace and brotherhood between both Jericho and Yache. he also expressed his warmest wishes to the Palestinian people to live in Security and stability denouncing the siege imposed on Chairman Yassir Arafat in his residency in Ramallah.

On the other hand, the president of the University of Medicine and Pharmacy in Yache Spoke about the importance of Peace and definitiveness of establishing the Palestinian State. During the delegation’s visit to the University he announced providing four study grants two in Medicine and two in Engineering.

On the other hand, the president of the Technology university in Romania received the Palestinian delegation and showed a will to support the Palestinian students and presented honoring certificates to the delegation, he also discussed the possibility to enable students to study by remote affiliation. The delegation also visited the chamber of commerce in Yache and agreed on conducting commercial exchange.

It is worth mentioning that the efforts made to establish this twinning agreement was exerted by the Arab students in Romania through the center of Jerusalem and the Palestinian embassy in Romania.

This agreement is the sixth of its kind whereas the Jericho Municipality established a lot of twinning agreements with many Municipalities around the world in order to benefit from the experiences of these cities in many development aspects.

The Opening Of CD Section In Jericho Municipal Library

In March 18 , 2003, and as part of the Municipal library in Jericho, a CD section in the Municipal library was officially opened under the disposal of the subscribers; the opening was attended by a number of concerned parties, school teachers, library subscribers, municipal council members, and municipality personnel. Deputy Mayor, Dr. Ali Husseini, has cut the strip declaring the opening of the CD section and all the guests took a tour around.

Project concept:
The idea came up as there was a rising need among the young people in the city of Jericho of using Hi- Tec and a necessity of facilitating information access for everyone. Especially to an important group and that is children and youth, the municipality desire was to be in a leading position in the Information, Technology, and Computer Field. For this reason the administration thought that adding a CD section in the municipal library is an essential step, as this kind of technology is distinguished from any other being the language of the world. During a visit of the head of the information technology section to the city of Nantes in France within a cooperation program with French municipalities, the idea was proposed and adopted by the Jerusalem- Gaza Loirre association. This association kindly donated a sum of 15000 French Franks for implementing this project.

 

Procedures for implementing this project:
As soon as the invoice for purchasing the CDs was issued and was sent to the Loirre association for approval, a set of steps were taken as soon as money was transferred, where there was an invitation for a bid. And under the supervision of The Mayor Mr.Abdel-Karim Sider and in cooperation with the IT and purchasing section a set of exclusive CDs was purchased from a high standard CD company. In addition to a CD writer and Empty CDs.
500 different CDs where purchased covering various topics and each one had a similar copy issued.
From the other hand, the Jericho Municipality contributed in paying a sum of 3000 NIS about 600$ US for designing and working out a CD cabinet, for exhibiting the CDs and facilitating the process of Choosing and borrowing. Two computers were put under the disposal of the library subscribers for the use of these CDs inside the library.


The Targeted Group:
The CDs where chosen to suit the users from the ages of 6 – and above.

Types of CDs:
1- Scientific – 25 CD
2- Cultural and recreational – 54 CD
3- Recreational for children – 15 CD
4- Children – oriented ( educational, games) -53 CD
5- Applied programs and photos – 27 CD
6- religious – 50 CD
7- Historical and Geographical 15 CD
8- Medical – 12
9- children And Fun – 8 CD

The borrowing procedures:
A special procedure for lending the CDs was used , it was adopted as to suit the municipal library policies, it involves the necessity of the borrower to be a member in the library.
The membership card will be claimed and the fact that this particular member intending to borrow a CD is listed in his membership card. In case this CD was damaged or lost the borrower should pay a fine of about 12 NIS (2.5 $ U.S) a copy of this damaged CD will be made in the Library, in the Future, the Municipality thinks of installing a digital lending system.

Classification of the CDs:
The CDs are classified according to the topic and programs. In accordance with a special classification system that was implemented by the Library personnel, a special colored sticker was fixed on the CD cover to indicate the topic it belongs to, which will help reaching the wanted CD.

This step has made the Jericho Municipality a pioneer as the municipal library will be the only one in the city that offers such services, this section will help in expanding the intellectual capacities of children, students, and adults and will help in communicating information.

Future plans:

As the Jericho Municipality has recently signed an agreement for the establishment of a new library with ANERA (Near east refugee Aid) project funded by USAID it is expected that such a CD section will be expanded more and will include more CD topics and specialized programs.

 A Delegation from the university of Rennes visits the Jericho Municipality to discuss developing and planning the city

Within the cooperation program implemented by the Jericho Municipality and French consulate general in Jerusalem, the Jericho Municipality hosted Professor Armel Huet Head of the Planning department in the University of Rennes in France together with a delegation composed of French academics. The delegation met the Mayor of Jericho Mr. Abdel- Karim Sider with participation of Eng. Basel Hijazi Director of the Engineering department in Jericho Municipality and Miss Weam Erekat Director of Public Relations department. In that meeting, both parties discussed the different ways of cooperation between the city of Jericho and the city and university of Rennes in order to make future planning for the city of Jericho. The delegation was briefed about the current situation in the city and the city planning maps and schemes. They also had an idea about the usage of residential, commercial, and agricultural areas in the city. Some major points were agreed on during this meeting such as the city development project on both the short and long term and the study grants that will be provided for the Jericho Municipality by the University of Rennes in the field of Urban Planning. From the other hand, the Municipality will start collecting data for conducting social studies according to which the Planning scheme will be based on.

After that, the delegation was escorted in a tour around the entrances to the city where they had a look at the nature of houses, extended areas, areas that had been annexed to be within the municipality boundaries, and locations of some future projects the municipality intends to execute.

In addition to that, the Municipality of Jericho organized a workshop in the Municipality hall; in that workshop Professor Huet gave a lecture about the importance of working to achieve planning and organization for the city. Many national and governmental institutions representatives attended this workshop. Professor Huet explained that planning will have a lot of economic, cultural, and social positive reflects on the residents in general. Dr. Huet gave examples about this referring to Brittany county and the city of Rennes. These areas as he said were considered underdeveloped comparing with other cities, but after organizing and planning took place they became one of the advanced areas in many aspects.

On the other hand Professor Huet expressed the importance of the availability of a vision emanating from the citizens about the future of their city and the aspects that could be highlighted in the development plan. He also stressed on the necessity of all the institutions to participate in the process of planning and also their support to such projects as they will not succeed unless there was an institutional and public cooperation for supporting them.

Dr. Huet concluded his lecture by recommending the position that the city of Jericho occupys on the international level as being the oldest city in the world thus a great responsibility lies on the residents for its not a national responsibility only but an international one that needs all the parties to cooperate for developing and organizing the city.
