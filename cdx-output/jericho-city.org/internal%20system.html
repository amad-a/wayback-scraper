Children's municipal council Bylaw


Article (1): definitions:

The following statements mentioned in this bylaw shall have the following definitions unless otherwise indicated.

Municipality: Jericho municipality.
Council: child municipal council.
Chairman: the elected chairman of the child council.
Member: a member in the elected child council.
Resident: the Palestinian child who is studying in a school within the limits of the Jericho municipality.
Bylaw: child municipal council rules of procedure.
Electoral law: the law by which the child municipal council was elected.

Article (2): The Municipality relation with the child municipal council:

According to these rules of procedures, the municipality shall take the following steps:

1- Drawing up a general policy regarding the child municipal council and activities related to it, supervise its functions and domains, in addition to working out balance sheets and managing administrative, financial, and judicial work. Also, following up the different procedures carried out during the formation of this council.
2- Establishing a standing order necessary to fulfill the duties, which are assigned to the members of the child council.
3- Employing all means and possibilities necessary for the outcome of a successful council inside the elementary schools, (elementary 7th, 8th, and 9th). This will result in the raise of an educated and responsible generation taking on its responsibilities towards its community and serving its country.
3- Contributing in the implementation of the resolutions and decisions that emanate from the child municipal council after deliberating them.

Article (3): The relation of the Ministry of Education with the Child Municipal Council:

1- Following up and supervising the duties and activities that the municipal council will be assigned to and engaged in, inside schools.
2- The Coordination with the child municipal council in conducting educational training courses and extracurricular activities in schools.
3- Initiating an open dialogue that will combine the members in the child municipal council and the ministry of education in order to obtain all the requirements necessary and needed by the members' schoolmates to improve the educational system.
4- Holding meetings all the time with the children of the council for consultation and guidance by exchanging ideas about the performance of the council.


Article (4): Relation of the Ministry of Education with the Municipality:

1- The ministry of education should hand over a full report in every academic year that will include the names of the 10 first class students in grade 6 in schools within the limits of the Jericho municipality.

2- The cooperation with the municipality regarding the special requirements for the extracurricular activities carried out in the schools within the limits of the Jericho municipality.

3- The cooperation with the municipality in explaining the idea of a child municipal council for the new students and stimulating them to participate in the annual elections for the council.


Article (5): The objectives of the Child Municipal Council:

1- Building up a sense of duty and responsibility among children towards their community.
2- Habituating children on relying on themselves and helping others and being up to the responsibilities.
3- Building up proudness in their country and learning to protect its interests and sovereignty.
4- Giving children a chance to express their needs through their representatives in the council.
5- Improving skills and leadership abilities.
6- Strengthening relationships and friendship between the Palestinian children and the Arab children in specific and the children of the world in general.

Article (6): The formation of the child municipal council:

The child municipal council consists of:
1- General assembly- this consists of all the students in the 7th, 8th, 9th grades in schools within the limits of the municipality
2- Consultative assembly- this consists of students who were elected during the internal elections in the 7th, 8th, 9th grades in each school within the limits of the municipality
3- Administrative board- this consists of the students who are members in the consultative assembly and are elected by the members of the general assembly.
On the condition that there will be at least one representative from each grade in the schools within the limits of the municipality, in addition to a number equal to 50% of the number of students representing the grades participating in the council.

Article (7): Nomination for Membership in the administrative board in the council:

The terms for the nomination for the administrative board are as follows:
1- The nominee will have to be a member in the general assembly.
2- The nominee will have to be a member in the consultative assembly.
3- The nominee will have to be of a good manners and adequate conduct.


Article (8): loss of membership in the consultative assembly:

The member looses his membership in the consultative assembly in the council for these reasons:
1- Not attending three consequent sessions and not presenting an acceptable legal excuse approved by the assembly.
2- Termination of his membership as the student had graduated from the 9th grade.
3- if he had taken any action or behavior that is inconsistent with the regulations, responsibilities, duties, and authorizations determined in this bylaw.
4- In case he had been voted of no confidence by third of the members in the consultative assembly.
5- In case he abandoned school or moved to schools outside the limits of the municipality.
6- In case he failed in his class or his academic attainments had been apparently decreased. This will accordingly lead for the possibility of the school principal to officially recommend his membership to be terminated.


Article (9): loss of membership in the administrative board:

A member looses his membership in the administrative board for these reasons:
1- In case he failed to attend three consequent sessions without a legitimate excuse accepted by the board.
2- The termination of the period of membership as the students had graduated from the 9th grade.
3- In case he had taken any kind of action or behavior inconsistent with regulations, duties, responsibilities, or authorizations determined in this bylaw.
4- In case he had been voted of no confidence by third of the consultative assembly.
5- In case he abandoned school or moved to a school outside the limits of the municipality.
6- In case he failed in his class or his academic attainments had been obviously decreased. This will accordingly lead for the school principal to officially recommend his membership to be terminated.


Article (10):

1- In case any student had lost his membership in the administrative board in the council, a substitute shall be appointed to fill his place from the members of the consultative assembly who hadn't been lucky to be members in the administrative board. on the condition that this will be done in terms of the highest votes for each member.
2- in case any student had lost his membership in the consultative assembly a substitute shall be appointed to fill his place from the members of the general assembly. who are candidates in the internal elections, and hadn't been lucky enough to be members in the consultative assembly, provided that priority will be to those with the highest votes. Also the substitute should be of the same class and school as the member who lost his membership, and in case there are no nominees, the school administration will recommend a name for nomination for the administrative board of the council for their verification.

Article (11): the administrative board members in the child municipal council:

The members in the administrative board of the council consists of one member from each participating class in the children council, 7th, 8th, 9th, grades in schools within the municipality limits + 50% of the number of these representatives and they are all elected by the general assembly.


Article (12):

After the elections for the administrative board, the Jericho municipality and after deliberating with the ministry of education will appoint two members in the administrative board. One representing children with special needs and one representing the run out students, provided that they will be of the same age as students in the council and they will fall under all the regulations in this bylaw. These two students will have the right for nomination and election for the position of the chairman and vice- chairman of the children’s municipal council.

Article (13): nomination for chairmanship and vice-chairman of the child municipal council:

After the announcement of the elections results determining the members of the administrative board of the council, the municipality declares in not less than a week the opening for the nomination for chairman and vice-chairman.
The nomination will last for one day only, and the names of the winners will be announced in an official meeting where all the members in the child council will be invited to attend.


Article (14): electing the chairman of the child municipal council.

The chairman will be elected in a direct, free, and public way run by the regulations of this bylaw, and by the elected administrative board in the council.
The winner will be the one who obtains maximum votes, the student who is elected as chairman should be more devoted than any other member, and the one who is not lucky to win the position of chairman will not have the right to nominate himself for vice-chairman.

Article (15): electing vice- chairman of the child council:

After electing the chairman, the members in the administrative board will run open elections to elect a vice-chairman, this will be the one who obtains the highest number of votes.

Article (16): duties assigned for the child council:

1- The chairman and the members of the administrative board will commence their duties and assignments starting the next day after the announcement of the election results.
2- The new substitute that will replace a member with an unoccupied position will commence the assigned duties during one week starting the date of vacancy of the position.

Article (17): child council sessions:

1- The council shall convene its ordinary sessions appointed by the chairman at least twice a week. These sessions will take place in the child council residence.
2- The chairman and members in the council (no less than 2/3) have the right to call an extraordinary session.
3- The members shall be informed about the agenda and time of each session one day before its convening, no subject irrelevant to the agenda shall be discussed unless 2/3 of the members of the council were present and agreed to deliberate an urgent matter.
4- The council has the authority to let anyone attend any of its sessions if this was decided by most of the members in a meeting. Also, all the resolutions are issued only in the presence of all the members.
5- Any member in the consultative assembly has the right to attend any of the council's ordinary sessions only for listening and will not have the right of discussion or voting.
6- The resolutions and developments concerning these sessions shall be recorded in a special paper fixed record and signed by the chairman and present members.
7- The quorum of the session consists of the majority of the members (half +1) if there was a lack of quorum in two successive sessions the third session is considered legal no matter what was the number of the attendants.
8- The resolutions of the council shall be issued as the majority of the members will vote on them. if there were an equal number of votes, with and against a resolution, the decision will be the preponderancy of the party for which the chairman has voted. and the election process shall be public.
9- The council can form committees consisting of members of the council in it, to handle any brought up issues. The recommendations of these committees shall not be valid until the administrative board confirms them.

Article (18): duration of the child council:

The duration of the council shall be one year starting the day it received its tasks.

Article (19): the chairman's authorities and responsibilities:

The chairman shall be entrusted to the following responsibilities and authorities:
1- Calling for a meeting for the council in a particular time, preparing the agenda of the meeting and notifying the members about them. Also, to be in charge of these sessions, this will include managing and keeping order in these sessions.
2- Representing the council in conferences, meetings and formal bodies.
3- To be committed to the councils resolutions and to take actions towards executing them.
4- The chairman has the right to entrust the vice-chairman or any other member with part of the assigned duties.


Article (20): Authorities entrusted to the vice-chairman:

The vice-chairman can practice the authorities and responsibilities of the chairman in case of his absence or if the position was vacant according to the principles of this bylaw.

Article (21): resignation:

1- The chairman of the child council can resign by submitting an official notification of that, his resignation shall not be considered final until it is approved by the members of the child council and the Jericho municipal council within a week from the resignation.
2- the majority of the members of the council( half their number +1) can resign all at the same time, if they submitted an official notification of that to the chairman, and the resignation shall not be considered valid unless it is approved by the Jericho municipal council within a week of the resignation.
3- A member in the council can resign by an official notification of that forwarded to the council and this resignation shall not be considered unless the council approves it. The Jericho municipal council should be informed of that within a week of the council's decision
4- In all the cases mentioned above, the council shall convene an emergency session within two days of the resignation for deliberation.
The resignation is considered final and valid if the Jericho municipal council didn’t give a reply within two weeks of the resignation.

Article (22): the vacancy of the chairman position:

1- The chairman position is considered vacant if it became unable to fulfill its duties and that is by loosing its legitimacy.
2- Resignation ,or death.
3- A new chairman is elected by the administrative board within two weeks of the vacancy of the position.
4- The vice-chairman shall occupy the position and handle the tasks of the chairman after the position is vacant until new elections are organized and a new chairman is elected.

Article (23): when the position of a member in the administrative board is vacant.

1- The position as a member shall be considered vacant if the member resigned, was fired, died or lost his legitimacy.
2- The council shall announce the vacancy of the position and the Jericho municipal council shall be informed which will in turn inform the involved bodies (ministry of education bureau and the elections committee), steps are taken towards appointing a substitute in accordance to this bylaw.
3- The vacancy shall be submitted to the next elected member according to the vote's assortments and as the official approved results will indicate.
4- The vacancies are occupied according the regulations in the previous paragraph not exceeding half the number of the members all at the same time.
5- If the positions of more than half of the members were vacant, new elections are organized within a period not exceeding one month from the time of the announcement of vacancy of position. This is to elect totally new members for the council, the date for these elections shall be determined by the Jericho municipal council.
6- In all cases the duration of membership is the period left for the councils term.
7- in case one member or more had resigned. and the number didn’t exceed half of the members and there were no substitutes according to the elections results, the child municipal council should recommend substitutes of whom have the right of nomination taking into consideration the school, class, branch, of which belongs the resigned members and shall forward the names to the Jericho municipal council for approval.

Article (24): Functions, Jurisdiction, and Authorities of the child municipal council:

Social and cultural activities:
1- Organizing celebrations in the important occasions ( child day, land day)
2- Organizing charity campaigns for poor kids in schools.
3- Organizing educational and social gatherings that will include all children in schools.
4- Encouraging children in the city to read and visit the public library.

Sport activities:
1- Inquiring about the requirements of schools concerning sport, this could be playgrounds, and athletic equipment.
2- Organizing sport training courses in schools.
3- Encouraging early morning sport in schools.
4- Coordination with sport institutions in the city, for improving the skills of talented students in the field of sport.

Gardens and beautification of schools:

1- Forestation and gardens care in schools.
2- Organizing competitions for the most beautiful garden in schools.
3- Supervising fence painting campaigns and walls decoration in schools.

Services connected to disabled children:
1- Taking steps towards following up the presence of special facilities for the disabled children in all the activities supervised by the council.
2- Organizing special activities connected to disabled children, which will guarantee their affiliation in the community.

Public relations:

1- Carrying out correspondences between the child municipal council and other child councils in other cities to reinforce friendships and good relations.
2- Organizing receptions for the visiting delegations.
3- Organizing campaigns to introduce the child council for the public.
4- Building up good relations with students in other classes in schools

Public health:

1- Inquiring and following up the cleanliness in schools.
2-Organizing reciprocal competitions connected to health and cleanliness in schools.
3- Organizing awareness campaigns against smoking and drugs and other social lesions.
4- Organizing visits for stationers and markets and encouraging their owners to follow public health procedures, in cooperation with the municipality staff.

Article (25):
This bylaw shall operate starting the day it becomes approved by the child municipal council and shall not be amended without the agreement of the child municipal council.

Annex illustrating the child municipal council Bylaw

As a result of the need for an amendment for the child municipal council bylaw in its 1999 term (it only included 6th grade, also the inability for the municipality to conduct elections in the year 2000 and 2001 due to Israeli reoccupation of the Palestinian territories).
The Jericho municipality in coordination with the UNICEF has amended the child municipal council bylaw taking into consideration the remarks that were sent to the Jericho municipality from other involved institutions. These remarks encompassed the necessity to involve students of less academic attainments.
In addition to that, there was an obvious necessity for the member to participate for more than one year. As a result, the Jericho municipality and the UNICEF have convened several meetings to discuss these suggested amendments. Finally, the bylaw was approved in its current form.
2- In order to pass the experience from the former members in the council to the new ones, the Jericho municipality found it essential to keep the old members in the council elected in the preceding term in 1999. These will become members in the new council without being elected which will consequently make them members in the general assembly. On the other hand, elections will be carried out to elect members from grades 7 and 8 for the consultative and administrative board. (Students in the 9th grade have been elected in the year 1999) and their duration will be for this year only.
3- in the coming years and in the beginning of each academic year, internal elections will be organized to elect students from the 7th grade only, where the old 7th grade students will replace the 8th grade in the council, and the 8th grade students will replace the 9th grade. These replaced 9th grade students will have their membership terminated.
4- after electing new members in the consultative assembly in the beginning of each year,( this will only include the students in the 7th grade), the students in the 7th grade in all schools and in general elections will elect one student from each school to represent them in the administrative board in the child council. These elected students will replace the students of the old 9th grade who will be terminated from the council's administrative board.
5- The members of the administrative board for this year will consist of:
a- members of the administrative board for the child municipal council elected in 1999 (students of the 9th grade).
b- One representative on behalf of each class (7th and 8th grades) from the participating schools in the administrative board.
c- in addition to 50% of the number of the represented students in section (b) in this paragraph where the elections shall include the sections (a and b) in this paragraph, for example if the number of the 7th and 8th grade students in all the participating schools was 9 classes there should be an additional 9 members representing all classes which will be submitted to general elections, In addition to 50% of the number 9, which is the number of students representing the classes.( in case of an odd number it will be approximated to the higher even number) for ex. 9+5 = 14 student from the consultative assembly will be elected to be appointed in the administrative board. When counting the votes, 50% will be offered to those with the highest results, and after that one representative for each class will be elected according to the highest votes he or she obtains.
6- Persistently and in the coming years the number of the administrative board members in the council will consist of:
a- The members that were elected previously in grades 7 and 8.
b- The members that will be elected at the beginning of each academic year. In the consultative assembly, in general elections organized for all the 7th grade students in all schools on the condition that there will be one representative for each school ( only for 7th grade) in addition to 50%, for ex. If the number of the 7th grade classes in Jericho schools was 10 the general elections will run to elect 10+5= 15 students to be members in the administrative board. This will continue for the next coming years.

7- concerning the internal elections in schools and in case there was more than one section for each class the elections will run so as to include all the sections altogether where all the students will participate as a whole and shall have the right of electing and nomination.

8- The number of sections for each class has no relation to the number of representatives in the internal elections for the consultative assembly or in the general elections for the administrative board (the same number of representatives for everybody).
