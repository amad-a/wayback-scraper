Ladies and Gentlemen,

As the Occupation Bulldozers are demolishing the houses and institutions in our cities, we decided together with some friends to establish this imposing structure. And as the same Bulldozers are pulling out the olive trees the symbol of the Palestinian endurance in our towns and villages, we decided to plant palm trees here, the symbol of Palestinian pride and sense of honor.

Beside it, we planted roses, love, and hope.

 

Ladies and gentlemen,

The tightened closure imposed on the city of Jericho will not be able to make it loose its brightness and power of attraction of the local and foreign tourism. If this glitter- ness fainted out, we will lighten it with our eagerness and determination, a light for the generations to be rightly guided with, perhaps this imposing structure is one of these guiding lights.

 

Ladies and gentlemen,

 Right From the moment we held the position of the Mayor, Exceptionality was our goal.

Investing in the city's infrastructure and citizen's wellbeing was our domain

The broader concept of the municipality role was our guidance.

The city of Jericho being distinguished in the field of child-care and developing and refining their talents has lead to the outcome of the Child municipal council in 1999. This council is considered the first and unique experience among all the Palestinian cities, and with help of the UNICEF the experience was a complete success. It developed and spread in other cities. This qualified the city of Jericho to move in solid steps to a larger project and a bigger experience and that is Jericho being a child friendly city.  Through this project, we will be able to rise up the sensibility of the decision-makers towards children's rights and needs.

Ladies and Gentlemen,

 

The idea of establishing the kids club came up many years ago, through these years this idea developed and turned into a technical study submitted to many parties for funding. In the last quarter of the year 2001, an agreement was signed with the British consulate for donating 53000$ for the construction of the first floor. This was just the beginning because another agreement was signed with the Japanese representative office for funding the purchases of the furniture with a total cost of 55000$. This was followed by a third agreement signed with the Norwegian representative office within the job creation program with a sum of money reached up to 60000$. and a fourth agreement signed with UNDP and funded by the Japanese government ,with a sum of 93000$ for the construction of the first and second floors and some external work. On the other hand, the Jericho Municipality contributed in funding a sum of 32000$ from its own budget. All this lead to the outcome of this club that combines four clubs (the arts club, the culture club, the performances and activities club, and computer club). And this meeting hall named (The WindMills) and the external garden.

This club will be supervised and managed by 8 Municipality Personnel and a number of volunteers in order to insure the participation of the local community in the process of development,

 To emphasize this, the Jericho municipal council in its session number 11/2003 dated March 8th 2003 decided to form a trustee board consisting of a number of parents and experts as to be part of this Club. as  the municipality personnel and volunteers will be receiving training conducted by specialists under the supervision and funding of UNICEF, the Members of the child Municipal Council will be getting training courses aiming on making them aware of their rights and duties and the rectification of their interests and behaviors towards the public interest, to be effective and productive.

Ladies and gentlemen,

 

This Imposing structure is one of the outcomes of our modest efforts that you strongly supported, hoping that God will bring Peace and blessing to our Adorable City.

 

Abdel-Karim Sider

Mayor of Jericho
