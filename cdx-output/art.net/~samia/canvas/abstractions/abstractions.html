Selection of Pictures for Article

by Samia A. Halaby

Ali,
These are all paintings which are part of the mainstream of my artistic expression. I have mostly a painter using oil as medium, but recently, I began to explore media. You will find a few paper mache sculptures and some stitched and folded works.
Samia


432 Marching, 1989. Acrylic on canvas, 84 x 186 inches.
This large painting was one of the final expressions of the project dealing with Canal Street. One day, I had gone out to photograph and perceived that the entire event at Canal was like a dance. Abtraction deals with the general and this entire experience was not unlike observing and participating in political demonstrations. For this reason the painting is titled Marching.


460 Branching, 1994. Oil on canvas, 36 x 36 inches.
Just prior to my involvement with olive trees, I had begun to explore branching as a process not only in trees but also in human behavior. I did several works in oil and then did an abstract kinetic painting on the subject. You will see them in the Kinetic section.


525 Density in Air, 1999. Oil on canvas, 28 x 20 inches.


532 Green Hay, 1999. Oil on canvas, 18 x 18 inches.
This painting is all about foliage in the wind.


536 Quiet Fire in Blue Sky, 1999. Oil on canvas, 36 x 48 inches.
This painting is also about foliage and its play in the wind and the similarity of these same principles of motion to all wave motion, even that of people waiting on line.


D1649 Praying mantis, 1999. Gouache on paper, 27.5 x 30 inches. (ps. Ali, there are many more works like this one. Let me know if I should add more.)


536 Olive Shading, 2000. Oil on canvas, 42 x 48 inches.
Olive Shading is about the beauty of sitting in the dappled shade under a tree in Palestine. The texture of foliage is cast upon the texture of plowed earth and over layed with a third distribution patter, that of the scattered stones and rocks of Palestine.

<
D1690 Textures of Ramallah, 2000. Acrylic on tyvek, 52 x 45 inches.
Here is a conscious attempt at contrasting the various textures of trees I've grown to love in Ramallah: Olive, along, fig, cypress, and many more.

Olive Orchard in My Studio, 2000. Acrylic on polyester, 72 x 175 inches.
After visiting the olive orchards of Salfit to be part of the film, Zatoonat, I felt a lasting sence of owe and satisfaction. It kept bringing to my mind a warm loaf of bred just removed from the oven. It is tragic that now Israelis make it impossible to be there anymore as they create barriers, confiscate land, and uproot trees, building their criminal stangulation wall.


538 Ulleiq, February 2000. Acrylic in papier mache, 24 x 16 x 12 inches
One of the many things that impressed my vision at Salfit was a weed called Ulleiq which hung from an almond tree and was growing thickly, tightly as though woven against incursion.


538 Ulleiq shown from another side in front of Orchard in My Studio


537 Balata, February 2000. Acrylic with paper mache, 11 x 17 x 6”


D1857 Semite Weaving the Wind, 2003. Silkscreen monotype. 22 x 30 inches.


551 Mountain Olives of Palestine, 2003. Acrylic and paper on canvas. approx. 70 x 60 inches
This painting is made up of cut and stitched canvas. Some parts have paper collaged in. It is one of my major works in response to my experience with painting olive trees. Especially significant was the impression of the huge old trunks of the olive trees of Palestine.


554 Mountain Air of alquds, 2003. Acrylic and paper on canvas, 84 x 48 inches.
A tribute to the beautiful location of our great city now sadly occupied by strangers. Here is a painting in dedication to its future liberation.


555 Three Dancers Flying Through the Trees Like a Strom, 2003. Acrylic and paper on canvas, 48 x 77 inches


556 Queenbee, 2003. Acrylic and paper on canvas, 25 x 12”


560 Purple Grapes, 2003. Acrylic and paper on canvas, 25 x 17.5”


572 Arbor, 2005. Acrylic and paper on canvas, 28 x 18.5”


576 Little Palestine, 2003. Acrylic on canvas, 46 x 27”


577 Demolish the Wall, Let Life Be Fertile, 2004. Acrylic and paper on linern, 56 x 59”


582 Her Garden in AlQuds, summer 2005. Acrylic on canvas stitched and glued, 51.5 x 31.5”
