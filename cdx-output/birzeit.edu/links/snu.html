Al-Quds University: http://www.alquds.edu - Al-Quds University was founded in the late 1970s when four separate faculties were founded in the Jerusalem area. In 1984 these faculties were unified under the umbrellas of Al-Quds University. At the present time, the website tells us, "the university stands out as an example of distinguished academic achievements". Brief information on the departments and programmes of this Palestinian university, presented in a simply-constructed website. Every time you visit, a new department seems to pop up. Review last updated: 21 December 1997. Top of page.


An-Najah University: http://www.palestine-net.com/ education/najah.html - A temporary website of this Nablus-based university, there is information on the university's history, academic facilities, institutes and centers, statistics, with contact numbers and addresses. Added to Complete Guide: 5 February 1997. Review last updated: 21 December 1997. Top of page.


Arab American University - Jenin: http://www.aauj.edu - A brand spanking new website for the most recent of Palestine's Universities. You can find an overview, history, a list of colleges and pictures of the planned campus. The latter is obviously not built yet and looks a little like Disneyland. The AAUJ's "full affiliation with the California State University System" and current advert for a president with American educational management experience seems out of place and the university-under-construction was greeted with mixed feelings by Palestinian educationalists. There are already 6 universities for the Palestinian West Bank population of 1.5 million and many feel the existing universities should be built up, particulary the two in Gaza. Gaza has over 1,500 students who wish to study in West Bank insititutions who have been prevented from doing so by Israel for the two and a half years. This seems to have escaped the public relations team that wrote the material for the website, who claim that "the location of the university makes it accessible to students from all over Palestine..." Why is Jenin any more accessible than anywhere else in this tiny country? Regardless of the politics, AAUJ's website is well put together and informative. Added to Complete Guide: 5 February 1997. Review last updated: 21 December 1997. Top of page.


Bethlehem University: http://www.bethlehem.edu - Launched on the Web on 17 May 1996, Bethlehem University's website offers brief information about the university, newsletters, and a staff directory with e-mail addresses are available. At the time of this review, the website had not been updated since 25 March, nine months ago. Review last updated: 21 December 1997. Top of page.


Birzeit University: http://www.birzeit.edu - Launched on the Web on 6 June 1996, Birzeit's is the largest website in Palestine. A good idea of where the website is going. Sections include:
  • Brief information about the university in English, French, German. Other European languages to follow.
  • Birzeit Archives - University Newsletter, Press Release, Human Rights & Photographic archives.
  • The Ramallah On-line Tourist Guide, to the area around the university (Ramallah, Al-Bireh, Birzeit, Jifna and Taybeh).
  • A guide to Events in Palestine.
  • Links to departments, research and community-outreach centres, and conference information are available on the homepage.
  • Birzeit News offers reports, including Real Audio, from the university and wider community. Other Palestine-related Web projects are also available off the homepage, in the "Featured Links" section.
Review last updated: 21 December 1997. Top of page.


Birzeit University, Continuing Education Department (CED): http://www.birzeit.edu/ced/ - Launched on the Web on 1 June 1997, the CED has taken its in-service courses for the private, public and NGO sector online. You will find information about courses in languages, computers, management training, community development and capacity building. Nicely designed website that is full of information. Added to Complete Guide: 1 June 1997. Review last updated: 21 December 1997. Top of page.


Ibrahimieh Community College: http://www.ibrahimieh.edu - A private, non-profit institute of higher education, the Ibrahimieh Community College website is still very much under construction. Islamic University of Gaza (IUG): http://www.teleport.com/ ~alquds/palestine/univ/iug.html - The IUG now has a brief introductory homepage on the Al-Quds University webserver. The IUG is located in Gaza City, close to the seashore and to the UNRWA's headquarters. It was established in 1978 "to serve the urgent education needs of about one million people living in Gaza Strip". Added to Complete Guide: 7 January 1997. Review last updated: 21 December 1997.
