NOTE: Palestinian Internet Services and Service Providers are not part of the Golden Olive Awards system. Primarily to enable the Birzeit Webmaster to avoid being met in dark alleys by disgruntled colleagues.


Assali Computer Services, Ltd.: http://www.assali.com/ - ACS Ltd. is a Jerusalem based computer services company focusing on: LAN & WAN design, installation and maintenance, Systems Development & Internet Services. They have been in the software development business for 3 years developing custom applications for the local market. All products are MS Windows bilingual (Arabic/English) and ACS is "currently developing using Borland's Delphi with extensive experience in Visual Basic, MS Access and MSC++". A nicely laid out site with demonstration adverts for clients. Review last updated: 19 December 1997. Top of page.



Baboun Computers and CDs: http://www.baboun.com/ - Baboun sells computers and software in the Bethlehem area. A plain one-page "contact us" website. Added to Complete Guide: 6 February 1997. Review last updated: 19 December 1997. Top of page.



Bailasan, Palestinian Internet Services Company: http://www.bailasan.com/ - Bailasan, is another web design company that has appeared in Palestine. Looks fresh, but what ideas it does seem to have are not well implemented. The "Our Jerusalem" section of the website, with a domain name of its own (www.our-jerusalem.org), is a nice idea, with historical, business and tourism sections. Yet the information is presented in a boring way, with broken links and unfinished work apparent everywhere. The limited information about the gates of Jerusalem, assuming that you were interested in them anyway, is what you would find in any guidebook. Same goes for the material on the different quarters of the Old City. The "Religious Affairs" section of Bailasan's website leads you to the "HomePage Of the Islamic Palestinian Scholar BASSAM JARRAR" [sic], where you can read two of his books online. Perhaps the saddest part of the website is the PINC, the Palestine Internet News Center, "your daily access to News, Reports and Investigations from Palestine". The "This Week" news section (bear in mind I am writing this in December) includes "Israeli bulldozers destroyed Christian Holy Sites in mount Abu Ghniem", "Head of the Knesset tries to prevent an Arab councilman from speaking in the memory of June War", and "Israelis run a begging network in cities of the West Bank". In other words, this week's "news" is six months old. Pictures from Palestine leads to a "File Not Found" (you'd think they might notice that a homepage button bar link doesn't work?) and "What's New?" (the final revelation) leads to an empty page "where we will be presenting sites that are done by Bailasan". In other words, How To Destroy Your Public Image Single-Handed 101. Added to Complete Guide: 6 February 1997. Review last updated: 19 December 1997. Top of page.



Baraka, the Palestinian NGO Network: http://www.baraka.org/ - Baraka, the Palestinian NGO service provider, launched its website in mid-1996. Information is provided about PNGO (the Palestinian NGO network), a few other NGOs, and links to other Palestinian sites. An e-mail address list for all its member organisations would be welcomed, as would homepages for all the NGOs that in the country. To be fair, Baraka has been through a difficult administrative period recently, and we are hoping that it will work out before all the NGOs defect to Palnet. Review last updated: 19 December 1997. Top of page.


Gamasoft Online: http://www.gamasoft.com/ - A new company with an Arabic-only website that has been in the same state since last reviewed in November 1996. They do need to sort out the many links off the homepage that lead to a "File not found - 404" message. Obviously a student from the Bailasan school of Web design. Haram, A shame. Added to Complete Guide: 27 August 1996 / Review last updated: 19 December 1997. Top of page.


Information Technology Special Interest Group (ITSIG): http://www.birzeit.edu/national/itsig/ - In its own words, the non-official body ITSIG aims, "to develop an atmosphere of coordination and cooperation between the various bodies and individuals involved in the development of IT in Palestine; to create an advisory body for IT projects; to influence policy makers in IT related issues and developments", etc., etc., The main purpose of this website seems to be to advertise ITSIG's mailing discussion list. Nothing really added in the last six months except an industry needs assessment on the state of the Internet and Internet industry in Palestine. Added to Complete Guide: 2 July 1997. Review last updated: 19 December 1997. Top of page.


Nigel Parry - website design and consultancy: http://www.birzeit.edu/nigelparry/work/ - A simple but attractive site with contact information, some basic advice for organisations wishing to launch a website with a link to a more detailed analysis, and intros and links to previously-designed websites. Prices begin at US$600 for a very basic site and rise to $1,300 for a more advanced design with 10 publications. All prices including one-year of Web hosting via the US-based PalGate. Added to Complete Guide: 29 May 1997. Review last updated: 19 December 1997. Top of page.



Palestine.On.Line: http://www.p-ol.com/ - Palestinian ISP and supposed content provider from the makers of Planet. The various searchable business and service directories are noticeably incomplete. The Palestine Internet Directory, "The most comprehensive Palestinian directory service on the net that helps you find individuals and institutions by name or by sector" isn't and doesn't. The "Attractions" section, with material on art, educational systems, etc., is almost without information. The "Palestine Information Highway" is the most outrageous, a Complete Guide to Palestine's Websites clone, down to many of the category titles and criminal use of a PhotoShop-altered Birzeit University logo! Clearly, no one has the time to actually make the clone a clone, as links to sites once you get inside the categories are noticably absent. Another Bailasan pupil. For anyone else planning on running a reviewed guide to Palestine's websites, note that this relaunch required one month's work on redesigning and implementing the design, a week's work re-reviewing the existing 88 sites, and another week finding the new sites. Add to that another two months work to produce and update the two previous versions and you have three and a half months of the time of a staff member to account for. By all means do it, just do it properly. Added to Complete Guide: 6 February 1997. Review last updated: 19 December 1997. Top of page.



Palestinian Academic Network (Planet): http://www.planet.edu - Planet's website offers links and space to a few Palestinian organisations, mostly with an academic/educational flavour. Planet launched a commercial on-line ISP business in the guise of Palestine.On.Line above and can only be considered to be the "Palestinian Academic Network" if putting up a single page on the Web with links to 20 news sites makes you CNN. Review last updated: 19 December 1997. Top of page.



PalGate: http://www.palgate.com - PalGate offers Web services to the non-NGO sector, including web page hosting and virtual domain names. Speed is assured by a US-based Web server connected to the Internet by a triple DS-3 connection (each 45 MB/s) and T-3 lines connected to Sprint, MCI, and UUnet. Palgate's network is monitored 24 hours a day to ensure that it is up and running. Added to Complete Guide: 17 November 1996 / Review last updated: 19 December 1997. Top of page.



Palnet: http://www.palnet.com - A private company, Palnet arrived on the scene offering e-mail and a Web connection to individuals and organisations for only $25 a month with unlimited access. PALNET's website has developed its links, although navigating through them can be a little boring, as only the titles of websites are offered with no reviews. The site also offers an interesting guide to Palestine's economy for those wanting to invest here, as well as government and business contact addresses. PALNET were at the Palestine International Festival 1996 and 1997, offering opportunities for visitors to browse the Web, and hopefully will make a reappearance this coming year. A dynamic company with 470 individual and institutional customers at the November 1996 count, rising to 852 by August 1997. Palnet offers a 128K leased line to Birzeit University and various Palestinian ministries, soon to be upgraded to at least double the speed. Review last updated: 19 December 1997. Top of page.



Palnet@K5M: http://www.palnet.com/k5m/ - The first Palestinian Internet Cafe, based in Ramallah, a joint project between the premiere Internet Service Provider - Palnet - and K5M, Ramallah's oldest restaurant-cafe. Four multimedia computers offer users the chance to find out what it's all about at cheap rates. The pizzas and food are great in this restaurant, with the Birzeit Webmaster's recommendation being the Venetian, allowing for the occasional defection to a Hawaiian. The homepage has both K5M restaurant and Internet price menus, photos of the cafe and a list of K5M's catering services. In case you were wondering, K5M's name comes from the 6 brothers that own the restaurant - 5 of them have names starting with M and one with K. Or is it the other way round? No matter, visit the homepage and then visit the cafe! Added to Complete Guide: 17 May 1997. Review last updated: 19 December 1997. Top of page.



United Nations Development Programme (Programme of Assistance to the Palestinian People): http://www.papp.undp.org - UNDP (PAPP) became interested in the idea of building a Palestinian research and academic network around 1994, sponsoring and training universities in how to provide e-mail to their faculty. The website is a good current, reference point for those interested in this work and the wider work of the United Nations. Formerly attached to the Internet via the Hebrew University, UNDP now offers its service through a connection with IBM. UNDP supplied Birzeit University's webserver's connection, and contributed substantially to the development of the Birzeit's World Wide Web Project through equipment donations, just a few examples of the outstanding service it has provided to the Palestinian educational sector. Review last updated: 19 December 1997. Top of page.


Zaytona Soft: http://www.zaytona.com - A private company offering computer consultancy and an Internet connection in Nablus, and some training. It's "Other Palestinian Links" have offered you the choice of two for over a year. Not particularly convincing. Added to Complete Guide: 17 November 1996 / Review last updated: 19 December 1997. Top of page.
