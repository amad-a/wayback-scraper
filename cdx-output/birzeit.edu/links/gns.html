Al-Ahli Hospital: http://www.ahli.org - A hospital in Hebron began in 1987 with 300 beds. It's a project of the Patient's Friends Society and this is a one page site with information about the hospital and how to donate to further expansion.Added to Complete Guide: 16 September 1999. Top of page.


Al-Bireh Municipality: http://www.al-bireh.org/ - The border between Al-Bireh and neighbouring Ramallah has, over the years, become a grey area for most of the residents of both towns. However, both towns have separate municipalities and two distinguishable characters. This website offers a City Profile, Historical View, The City Council, Public Services, Public Health, Economic & Trade Services, Social & Cultural Services and Sports, complete with photographs. Added to Complete Guide: 16 February 1998. Top of page.


Amro and Associates Law Office, Ramallah: http://www.law-amro.com/ - Established in Hebron 1961 by Yaser Hasan Amro. In 1968 the firm moved to Amman, Jordan and later in 1995 established offices in Ramallah, Palestine. Amro & Associates is a Law firm specilize in General Civil Practice, Corporate, International Trades, Agency and Distributorship, Construction, Foreign Investment, Taxation, Banking, Insurance, Securities, International Property Law, Labor Law, Litigation before all Courts and Arbitration. The firm has been assigned to prepare several draft laws and regulations. Information also provided on the firms partners and clients. Information is provided in both Arabic and English. Review updated September 2, 1999. Added to Complete Guide: 28 January 1998. Top of page.


Arabcare Medical Services: http://www.arabcare.com/ - This website has undegone a revamp and is looking promising. Quite a lot of this site is still under construction, such as the staff and publications lists and the photo gallery. But the sections on Medical Care, which has info about services provided, locations of Arabcare institutions throughout Palestine, and the technologies used in these institutions, - and Health Insurance, which contains limited information about Arabcare's nationwide insurance policies are worth looking through. The site has been modified recently, so keep checking back for more additions to it. Added to Complete Guide: 6 February 1997. Review last updated: 2 September 1999. Top of page.


Central Election Commission: http://www.planet.edu/~cec - This site, dedicated to "providing concise and up-to-date information.....about the Palestinian elections," seems to have been last updated in January 1996, but almost all of its information is still accurate. It features the whole text of the Israeli-Palestinian Interim Agreement on the West Bank and Gaza Strip, as well as the whole Palestine Election Law of 1995. It also contains fact sheets, which contain information about the election timetables, the 16 constituencies, the electoral register. Full of useful and relevant information. Review last updated: 2 September 1999. Top of page.


Department of Arab and International Affairs, PLO: http://www.dair.org/ - Oh please. One page with no links working. why?Added to Complete Guide: 20 September 1999. Top of page.


Gaza Community Mental Health Programme: http://www.gcmhp.net/ - The GCMHP site is packed full of information about its services and the programme itself - including its departments, events, activities, publications and research (all available to read online), and the annual report. We also find a picture gallery, guestbook, and press releases section and a link to the home page of GCMHP's founder and director, Dr. Eyad El-Sarraj where many of his articles in both Arabic and English are available online to read. This was the first "based-in-Gaza" homepage and it continues to provide useful unformation.Added to Complete Guide: 3 March 1998. Review updated 2 September 1999 Top of page.


Harb Heart Centre: http://doctor.medscape.com/NidalHarbMD - Harb Heart Center is a unique cardiac clinic in Ramallah which was estalished by Dr. Nidal Harb to provide a much needed service in Palestine. The clinic utilizes the most advanced technology and expert care in the country, including medical, non invasive diagnostic procedures, educational and supportive services in order to provide early detection, treatment and advice on prevention to help the fight against cardiac and vascular disease. Dr. Harb also specializes in non-invasive,invasive, interventional and nuclear cardiology.Added to Complete Guide: 17 September 1999 Top of page.


Halhul Municipality: http://www.fortunecity.com/roswell/gypsy/291/ - If you have the patience to wait for the gif-packed main page to download, you will be treated to the "Haggan Musical Symphony." Then, if you go to the main index page, you will see a display of blossoming flowers and flying butterflies all over your screen. If you can manage to wade through all this, you'll find links taking you to pages about the history, geography, and municipality of the town, which can be rather interesting if you haven't read anything about Halhul before. There is an "Arabic browsing" section on the site, which is still under construction. Added to Complete Guide: 17 March 1998. Review last updated 2 September 1999 Top of page.


Health, Development, Information and Policy Institute (HDIP): http://www.hdip.org/ - HDIP is an independent, non-profit organization devoted to policy research and planning concerning development issues and health care in Palestine. HDIP was established in 1989 by a group of researchers and health practitioners committed to improving the status of health care for all Palestinians in the West Bank and Gaza Strip. They recently relaunched their website and it really is quite impressive. Links to the activities of HDIP, their database and archives of publications some of which are summarized online. They also offer GIS (Geographical Information System) services and can produce maps for the public at the low cost of 50 shekels per theme. Examples of these maps are available online. This really is a useful site and well worth a visit. Added to Complete Guide: 9 February 1998. Review updated 2 September 1999. Top of page.


Jenin Municipality: http://www.zaytona.com/goj/titel.html - The website of this municipality offers information about Jenin's history, industry, agriculture and plans of the municipality. Added to Complete Guide: 21 June 1999. Top of page.


Jerusalem Water Undertaking (Ramallah District): http://www.jwu.org/ - The JWU offers its 1995 annual report, information about its installations, how to apply for employment and details of its services. A humerous graphic of a dancing water drop and a clear layout tells us its not all just sewers and pipes at JWU, on this well executed site. Unfortunately it's been over a year now since any new information has flowed from the JWU website.Review last updated: 2 September 1999. Top of page.


Nablus Municipality: http://www.nablus.org/ - An attractive and well-constructed site, it provides information about who's on the municipal council, the administrative departments of the council. There is a large section all about the city of Nablus - geography, arts and culture, history, agriculture, a photo gallery, and more. Parts of the site are still under construction, but the information that is already there is very interesting and well organised. Added to Complete Guide: 5 March 1998 Review last updated 2 September 1999. Top of page.


Negotiation Affairs Department, Palestinian Authority: http://www.pna.org/nad/ - Currently under construction. Judging from what this site used to look like previously, I'd say any change has to be a change for the better.Added to Complete Guide: 3 March 1998. Review last updated 2 September 1999 Top of page.


Palestine Medical Network: http://www.alrazy.net/ - An Arabic page with little information. Some links to Medical sites in the Middle East and a place to post your medical questions.Added to Complete Guide: 20 September 1999 Top of page.


Palestinian Airlines: http://www.palestinianair.com -The 10th of January 1997, was the date of the first Palestinian flight. Palestinian Airlines flight number (PF 141) took off on that day as the first trip carrying Palestinian pilgrims from Port Said Airport in Egypt to Jeddah in Saudi Arabia. Two Fokker 50 aircraft airlifted 325 pilgrims through seven return flights. An attractive website with details of flights to Egypt, Jordan, Dubai and Jeddah, information about the Palestinian Airline Fleet and links to other Palestinian sites. Added to Complete Guide: 5 May 1999 Top of page.


Palestinian Central Bureau of Statistics: http://www.pcbs.org/ - An English/Arabic website with a wide selection of current statistics from the Occupied Territories. Areas covered include Population, Climate, Labour & Wages, Balance of Payments, Agriculture, Education & Culture, Trade, Tourism, Living Conditions, Government, Finance, Environment, Health Service, Energy, Housing Conditions, Industry, Land Use, Victimization (i.e. crime), Construction, Gender & Children, Transport & Communication, Elections, and Prices. Pick your topic and seek knowledge! More websites like this please. The PCBS is also the organ responsible for conducting the Palestinian census - the results of which are now online. Review last updated: 14 December 1998. Top of page.


Palestinian Development Plan: http://www.palestine-pdp.org - For those seeking information about the Palestinian Development Plan. Quite a bit of information and downloadable PDF files of "The Plan", donor information and projects in Palestine. Added to Complete Guide: 16 September 1999 Top of page.


Palestinian Energy and Environment Research Centre: http://www.planet.edu/~pec - A simple page with background on this organization that is part of the President's Office and was established in 1993. "The Center has finished the plans and studies to provide electricity to the remote communities in Palestine, by using the Solar, Wind and Biomass energy."Added to Complete Guide: 14 January 2000. Top of page.


Palestine International Airport: http://www.gov.palestine.int/airport/ A good-looking site which promises much but unfortunately most of the links have not been added. Added to Complete Guide: 5 May 1999 Top of page.


Palestinian Legislative Council: http://www.pal-plc.org/ - An English/Arabic/French website - featuring the speaker's homepage, info on the PLC elections and structure, PLC reports for 1998, laws passed by the PLC. The layout is attractive but work is needed on the actual information on the site and its updating. Added to Complete Guide: 18 December 1997. Review updated 2 September 1999 Top of page.


Palestinian Ministry of Information: http://www.pna.org/ - The first PNA general information website but no longer the official one. It's import has decreased since the Ministry of Planning and International Cooperation website at www.pna.net came online. Features sections entitled "Basic Info about Palestine," "Palestinian Economy," "Who's Who at the PNA," "Key Issues," The site has articles and research available online, and several regularly updated sections such as the "Daily Press" section, which is a summary of the headlines in local Arabic and English newspapers. The presentation is a bit rough and needs some work, but all in all the site is very informative and up to date. Information available in both Arabic and English.Added to Complete Guide: 13 January 1997 / Review updated: 2 September 1999. Top of page.


Palestinian Ministry of Labour: http://www.pna.org/mol/ - This site contains basic information about the ministry, statistics and labour force surveys (from 1998), research papers, etc. There is a link labelled "ICE" and another labelled "PEP," which is rather confusing for the acronym-uninitiated amongt us. ICE is the International Conference on Employment in Palestine, and PEP is the Palestinian Employment Programme, and there is information available on both if you follow the PEP and ICE links. Added to Complete Guide: 18 December 1997. Review updated 2 September 1999 Top of page.


Palestinian National Authority Official Website: http://www.pna.net/ - The "official" PNA website from the Ministry of Planning and International Cooperation. One problem is the appearance of numerous non-official articles on this official site, including unsolicited material received on e-mail. Layout and information on this website improved a lot in 1997, and has continued to evolve. Rumour has it that this website - managed by a five-person team - was established by presidential decree after Yasser Arafat saw the equivalent Israeli Ministry of Foreign Affairs website online one day. The site contains lots of information - facts, articles, research, official letters and speeches, press reports, special reports, and much more. A lot of the links go back to pages in the Palestinian Ministry of Information's site (which used to be the official PNA site). Added to Complete Guide: 3 June 1997. Review last updated: 2 September 1999. Top of page.


Patient's Friends Society: http://www.planet.edu/~aburia/society.htm - Established in 1978 in Ramallah, the PFS runs clinics, a physiotherapy centre and in 1990 opened a rehabilitation centre. Added to Complete Guide: 18 September 1999 Top of page.


Population, Housing and Establishments Census 1997 website: http://www.pcbs.org/inside/inpbhou.htm - A huge amount of information on this page about the end-1997 national census that got the Israelis so uptight when it deemed to include Jerusalem. Information includes Legal Foundation, Historical Background, History of Censuses in Palestine, Census Stages, Justification, Census Contents, Outputs, Main Variables and Indicators, Confidentiality, Census Date Count Down, a message from the Offices of National Director, National Committee, Sub Committees, Supporting Committees, Acknowledgment For Donors, Donors Contribution, Census Plan, Dissemination and Publication Plan and Prelimintary Results, and an Appeal For All Palestinians to: fund it or make a technical contribution. If that wasn't enough, there is also the Census Progress in Pictures, Census Publications and...Other Activities. The budget for the census was US$7 million, supplied by the Palestinian Authority, UNFPA, the World Bank, UK and Norway. Added to Complete Guide: 18 December 1997. Top of page.


Ramallah Municipality: http://www.ramallah-city.org/ - Similar in many ways to the Al-Bireh Municipality website above, the Ramallah Municipality website basically offers more information, including a profile, history, details of the town council, projects, culture, and its role as a resort. The latter is perhaps a little misleading. Although Ramallah once enjoyed the reputation as the second most popular summer resort in the Arab World during the early 1960s, the Israeli occupation terminated this role completely through travel restrictions and de-development. Added to Complete Guide: 16 February 1998. Top of page.


Ramallah and Al Bireh Governates: http://www.ramgov.org/ - The website of the Ramallah and Al Bireh governates. The front page is attractive and there is lots of information about the infrastructure in the area, available services and other statistics. The only thing missing is photos!Added to Complete Guide: 14 January 2000. Top of page.


The Union of Palestinian Medical Relief Committees (UPMRC): http://www.upmrc.org/ - --"UPMRC is a grassroots, community-based Palestinian health organization. We were founded in 1979 by a group of Palestinian doctors and health professionals seeking to supplement the decayed and inadequate health infrastructure caused by years of Israeli military occupation." The siteoffers information about UPMRC - its missions and background, and a variety of published material in its "Health in Palestine" section. Also available is detailed information about its programs, health centres, and school, complete with pictures. Added to Complete Guide: 9 February 1998. Review updated 2 September 1999 Top of page.


United Nations, Office of the Special Coordinator in the Occupied Territories: http://www.arts.mcgill.ca/mepp /unsco/unfront.html - UNSCO is the Office of the United Nations Special Coordinator in the Occupied Territories, established in June 1994 by the Secretary-General of the United Nations. The site defines UNSCO's duties and objectives, and you will also find UNSCO/World Bank fact sheets about closure, quarterly reports, a directory of NGOs in Gaza, and links to other UN resources.Added to Complete Guide: 21 December 1997. Review updated 2 September 1999 Top of page.


Wafa, Palestine News Agency: http://www.wafa.pna.net/ - This is the official Palestinian News Agency. Unfortunately, the English version is under construction, and the Arabic version turns into gibberish on my screen, no matter what I try. It recommends Internet Explorer 4.x or above, which I don't happen to have, so you're on your own for this one. I read recently that WAFA was offering regular Real Audio broadcasts of their material from their website but I couldn't find that either. Added to Complete Guide: 17 March 1998. Review updated 2 September 1999 Top of page.


RECOMMENDED SITES OUTSIDE PALESTINE

Permanent Observer Mission of Palestine to the UN: http://WWW.Palestine-UN.Org/ - The website of the Permanent Observer Mission of Palestine to the UN. Can't quite remember the year of the General Assembly resolution condemning Zionism as racism? Not sure what UN resolution 181 said? Want the text of the Oslo Accords or the Wye Agreement? This is the place to go. An excellent site with tons of valuable information. Highly recommended.Added to Complete Guide: 13 May 1999. Top of page.



WEBSITES THAT ONCE WERE BUT ARE NO LONGER

Palestinian Ministry of Education: http://www.pna.org/moe/ This site disappeared sometime in mid-1999 but I understand a comeback is planned. Added to Complete Guide: 18 December 1997. Review updated 2 September 1999 Top of page.


Palestinian Ministry of Health (Nablus): http://www.palnet.com/archive/health/ - The Palestinian Ministry of Health (Nablus) surfaced on PALNET via two health-related reports that make interesting reading, concerned with the impact of the Israeli policy of closure on access to health services for Palestinians.Unfortunately the site never expanded and now no longer exists. Review last updated: 2 September 1999. Top of page.
