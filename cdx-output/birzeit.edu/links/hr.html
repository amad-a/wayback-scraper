A.I.C. - The Alternative Information Center: http://aic.netgate.net - AIC is a joint Israeli-Palestinian human rights group based in Jerusalem. The website has an attractive layout, a weekly news section compiled from the press releases of human rights and other organisations, an e-mail/Internet directory and lots of information to explore. Review last updated: 18 December 1997. Top of page.


B'Tselem, the Israeli Center for Human Rights in the Occupied Territories: http://www.btselem.org - B'Tselem may not at first glance seem to qualify for an inclusion in the "Complete Guide to Palestine's Websites". However, the organisation employs Palestinian field workers, works almost exclusively on human rights violations resulting from the military occupation and has consistently and courageously reported on the repression of the Birzeit University community. For these reasons, they are more than entitled to be part of this guide. Recent reports include August 1997's Sheer Brutality: The Beatings Continue, May 1997's Prisoners of Peace: Administrative Detention During the Oslo Process, and April 1997's The Quiet Deportation: Revocation of Residency of East Jerusalem Palestinians. The website is replete with downloadable reports, archived back to 1989, all presented in a crisp design. What more needs to be said? Added to Complete Guide: 12 November 1996 / Review last updated: 18 December 1997. Top of page.


Democracy and Workers' Rights Center: http://www.dwrc.org/ - Either under construction or neglected, all the links off the homepage lead to nowhere. Added to Complete Guide: 19 December 1997. Top of page.


Human Rights Action Project, Birzeit University: http://www.birzeit.edu/hrap - Birzeit's Human Rights Action Project, founded in 1977, has followed the case of every student, faculty and staff member detained by the Israeli military authorities, as well as "cases of restriction orders, house raids, deportation, and denial of freedom of movement and travel as it relates to university and academic activities". The website offers a link to the university's Human Rights and Press Release Archives, depositories of publications and press releases by the project and Birzeit's Public Relations Office, concerned with human rights issues. Monthly stats of students in Palestinian Authority and Israeli prisons are available. An excellent feature website on the problems of Gazan students studying in the West Bank, Academic Freedom First, can also be found here, offering a photo gallery, background information and an activists section. Review last updated: 18 December 1997. Top of page.


LAW - The Palestinian Society for the Protection of Human Rights and the Environment: http://www.birzeit.edu/lawe/ - LAW, a Palestinian Human Rights organization based in Jerusalem, was founded in 1990 by a group of Palestinian lawyers to promote human rights and further the principles of the rule of law, and to defend Palestinian rights in accordance with international human rights law and United Nations declarations. This site is easy to navigate and is a dream come true for all you fact addicts out there. It houses an invaluable directory of LAW press releases from 1994 until present, a comprehensive archive of LAW publications, reports concerning the Palestinian Authority, on-line reports covering environmental issues and human rights violations of the Israeli Occupation, as well as beginning to offer back issues of its very colorful and informative People's Rights magazine. The site is well worth the mouse clicks. A website about prisoners is its latest major addition. Added to Complete Guide: 13 August 1997/ Reviewed by Hanan Elmasu. Review last updated: 18 December 1997. Top of page.


The Mandela Institute: http://www.mandela.org - The Mandela Institute, formerly the Mandela Institute for Political Prisoners, has adjusted its mission since the coming of the Palestinian Authority (PA) to include human rights training for PA security personnel and rehabilitation for the prisoners released as a result of the Oslo Accords. The website is laid out clearly, but functions primarily as a virtual borchure for the organisation. An interesting link to some relevant UN documents is provided. A shame more information about the interesting work of Mandela is not available on the website. Added to Complete Guide: 19 December 1997. Top of page.


Palestinian Human Rights Monitoring Group: http://www.lebnet.com/phrmg/ - The Palestinian Human Rights Monitoring Group is a new human rights organization founded by Bassem Eid towards the end of last year. The site asserts that "Our conviction is that the building of a democratic society will not be accomplished by governments or leaders, but through the determination of the people themselves." The site has a simple layout, a special report on the death penalty in the West Bank and Gaza, and some editions of it's newsletter which document human rights abuses by both the Israeli government and the Palestinian Authority. Room for a lot of expansion. Added to Complete Guide: 7 May 1997. Review last updated: 18 December 1997 Top of page.


Palestinian Independent Commission for Citizen's Rights (PICCR): http://msanews.mynet.net/gateway/piccr/ - The Palestinian Independent Commission for Citizens' Rights (PICCR) was established in 1994, following a decree by Yasser Arafat, and was charged with monitoring and working toward the integration of human rights into Palestinian legislation and institutions. The fact that the president of the Palestinian Authority ordered it established seems to make its "independence" rather questionable and the fact that the same president ordered one former head of the PICCR - Eyad Sarraj - illegally detained makes you wonder exactly what the point of all this is. Having said that, the website, launched with assistance from the US-based Muslim Student Association in November 1997, offers quality information about the PICCR, press releases, urgent action requests, annual reports, and a position paper on "Occupation and the Building of Civil Society". You will find excellent and extensive material in these about both Israeli and Palestinian abuses of human rights. Added to Complete Guide: 7 January 1998. Top of page.


Women's Affairs Technical Committee (WATC): http://www.pal-watc.org/body.htm - WATC is essentially a human rights coalition, working together "for the realisation of the abolishment of all forms of discrimination against women in the preparation for the build up of a civic democratic society characterized by quality and social justice." Reads like that famous line in the first part of the Oslo Accord, doesn't it? Information available is split between a virtual brochure about the organisation, including its background, plans, members and accomplishments, and more meaty information, including newsletters, information about Palestinian women, a (suspiciously) undated "Message of the Week", and gender-related statistics about Gender and Human Development Indicators, Woman and Child Indicators, Total Fertility Rates by Selected Variables, and Maternal Mortality Rates by Age. A guestbook with intellegent comments left by Web surfers was also a bonus. Added to Complete Guide: 5 February 1997. Review last updated: 19 December 1997. Top of page.
