The International Palestinian Youth League report of Activities

February, 1997- April, 2000

The following are the activities that IPYL organized or participated in during the period mentioned above:

· "The Peace Chances in the World; Series of Seminars Lectures and Workshops"
Organized and financed by the Japanese Peace Boat Organization -Cruise, on boat meetings and discussions, it aimed to deliver a message of peace for places in conflict. The cruise toured 60 countries in 3 months, IPYL involvement was in the route; Rome-Athens-Jerusalem Feb. 1997.

"Short Term Youth Exchanges"
Seminar organized by The International Christian Youth Exchange (ICYE), it aimed to provide the participants with the necessary information, training and methodologies of how to fund raise a short term exchanges, as well as to develop their skills in the running and organizing such projects in future. Supported by The European Commission- DGXXII, took place in Yachranka/ Poland, 21-30/3/97.

"Coping with Violence & Terror"
Learning to Address the Grief of the "Other" in the Israeli Palestinian Conflict . Healing Workshop organized by The Harry Truman Research Institute for the advancement of Peace (Hebrew University) and the Palestinian Conflict Resolution Center (WI'AM). Bethlehem - 20-21/06/1997.

"First International Workcamps in Hebron"
Organized by the IPYL, in coordination with the Community work department at Birzeit University. The work included restorations in the old city of Hebron, building gardens at Hebron University and cleaning parts of the city. 5-8/08/1997, 100 local and international volunteers.

· "Young Leadership Training Seminar"
Organized by IPYL, supported by the Youth Programs of the Quakers. Issues covered; Management, Team work, Human Rights, Democracy, networking and Communications. Hebron, 14-17/08/1997.

· "International Youth Cultural Camp"
Organized by Mondotondo, supported by the European Commission, Turin/Italy, 20-25/08/1997 Preparatory meeting to organize future Youth Exchanges in the Mediterranean region, continuation to the conference organized by " Interkultural Centrum"/ Gaming/ Austria(1996).

· "Youth Leaders Meeting"
"Living together- joining hands with Gypsy communities striving for cultural diversity in Europe". Organized by the Youth Action for Peace (YAP), supported by the European Youth Foundation. Took place in Debrecen/ Hungary, 7-12/10/1997.

· "The First Palestinian Teens and Youth Conference"
To discuss the current and future challenges facing the Palestinian Youth; National Youth Policy. Organized by the Youth Program- Quakers, supported by the French Consulate, The Norwagean People Aid, UNDP, UNICEF and the Quakers. Took place in Ramallah, 23-24/10/1997.

· "The Alliance Conference"
Organized by the Alliance Organization, to discuss the International Work camps and Volunteers, the cooperation among the different organizations in the International Work camps. Took place in Mary le Roi / Paris/ France. 1-6/11/1997.

· "Voluntary Service & Conscientious Objection Study Session"
Study session to provide an exchange between conscientious objection and voluntary service organizations. Organized by EBCO, at the European Youth Center- Strasbourg, 16-23/11/1997. Supported by the Council of Europe.

· "Women Students in Arab Countries; Equal Participation in Higher Education Conference"
Awareness Raising activity organized by the National Unions of Students in Europe (ESIB). Took place in Lausanne/ Switzerland- Vienna/ Austria, 3-11/11/1997.

· "United we stand- Divided we fall- Seminar"
Study session organized by Service Civil International (SCI), International Christian Youth Exchange (ICYE) the Alliance, and the Youth Action for Peace (YAP) in cooperation with the European Youth Foundation. Aimed to strengthen the influence of the international voluntary service organizations in their work on European level by: Development of a coordinated, common strategy for external representations, the empowerment of representatives by providing different experiences and knowledge of structures and interactions. Took place in Strasbourg/ France, 11-18/01/1998.

· "International Youth Leaders Meeting"
Youth Perspectives 2000 - The role of youth exchanges and voluntary service in shaping a better future. Organized by Youth Action for Peace (YAP), aimed to define and update the needs and the motivations of the participants and youth leaders in international youth exchange and voluntary service activities. Supported by the European Youth Foundation, took place in Brussels/ Belgium, 17-22/02/1998.

"Mediterranean Network seminar"
The meeting was organized by the Coordination Committee for International Voluntary Service (CCIVS), it aimed to study the possibilities of more active cooperation among youth organizations in the Mediterranean region and the establishment of a network that could act as a coordinating body for future activities, to avoid duplications and repetition of activities as well as providing the different organizations with all the help and consultations needed to fund raise future activities. Took place in Santa Severa/ Italy, financed by CCIVS, 1-3/03/1998.

"A new Richness Seminar"
Seminar organized by Youth Action for Peace ( YAP- Italy Branch) Aimed to put the different experiences in the field of intercultural youth exchanges in contact among the participants in a wider cultural context respect to European one. Took place in Bolsena/ Italy, 21-29/03/1998

"Training Seminar for Youth Leaders"
Working for Peace and Tolerance with multicultural groups- methodology and contents of international youth work. This study session aimed to raise the awareness of young people about the cultural diversity on our planet and the need for tolerance. Organized by Youth Action for Peace (YAP) with the support of the European Youth Center- Budapest, 5-12/04/1998.

" Workcamps Leaders Training Seminar"
Four days training seminar aimed to provide the leaders of the international workcamps to be organized by IPYL the necessary skills to run, animate and organize the camps, the seminar was organized by the IPY and the Palestinian Conflict Resolution Center(WI'AM) , it covered issues in management and conflict resolutions. Financed by the Micro Projects - Arab Studies Society, took place in Bethlehem, 30/04-3/05/1998.

"Mediterranean Youth Forum"
Four days seminar organized and supported by the European Youth Forum to re- emphasize the importance of the Barcelona Declaration, concentrating in a particular way on the third chapter that concerns " The Partnership in Social, Cultural and Human Affairs: Developing Human Resources, Promoting understanding between cultures and exchanges between civil societies, as well as the launching of the Mediterranean Youth Forum. Took place in Valetta/ Malta, 13-17/05/1998.

"Electrical Communications"
Training seminar organized by YAP, to provide the participants with the necessary technological training, to be able to use the electronic communication in the daily life of the different branches of YAP, as well as to give them the necessary skills to be able to design the YAP/ and its branches web site. Took place in Frankfurt/ Germany, 2- 7/06/1998.

"Youth in Solidarity- A chance for Peace"
Seminar organized by YAP, the main goal of this seminar is to foster the links between Youth in the Middle East and Europe, to create a network to plan and implement multilateral projects. With the support of the European Commission DG XXII, Brussels/ Belgium 24-29/06/1998.

"Organizing International Workcamps"
Training seminar organized by the European Youth Council- Strasbourg, with the support of the Council of Europe. It aims to provide the participants with the necessary skills that will enable them to run, animate and organize an international Work camps. Strasbourg/ France, 21/06-4/07/1998.

"International Voluntary Work camps":

The IPYL planned and implemented a series of International Work camps throughout the reported time. The total number was 4 camps in different areas of Palestine. The dates of these work camps were as follows:
1. 5\7\98-25\7\98 ( Bethlehem City/ 18 foreigners/ 13 local)
2. 2\8\98-22\8\98 (Bethlehem City/ 23 foreigners/ 12 local)
3. 31\8\98-19\9\98 (Bethlehem/ Hebron/ Nablus/ 24 foreigners/ 12 local)
4. 18\12\98-3\1\99 (Bethlehem/ Beit-Jala/ 29 foreigners/ 9 locals)
Each Work camp consisted of two parts, the physical part and the study one. The volunteers worked in local community projects. The study part was rich and aimed at raising the awareness of the participants about the cultural life and the living conditions of the Palestinian people.

"Youth Exchange with YMCA Sweden":
During this activity, a number of Palestinian young men and women from the IPYL hosted 12 Swedish young men and women April 98 as the second part of the two years exchange between IPYL and YMCA Sweden. The visit to Sweden took place between 12-14 of August 98, where 12 Palestinian men and women visited the country. The two visits aimed to raise the awareness of the young people from both communities about the current concerns and problems facing the youth in both countries. IPYL and YMCA Sweden organized this exchange, the costs were covered by the last one.

"Kacholik School of Berlin":
Study trip organized by the IPYL for 35 students from this university, mainly from the Social Work Department, to introduce this collage to the Palestinian culture and the human rights situation in the region. The visit took place between 14-25/10/1998. The group visited all the cities in the West Bank and Gaza and met with young people from different backgrounds. As a result of this trip, IPYL, Bethlehem University and the Kacholik school of Berlin signed an agreement which enables the school to send interns from their college to do their practical training in Palestinian institutions. IPYL will select and contact the local NGOs to allocate the interns in as well as to help in the logistical and technical arrangements for them, while Bethlehem University will be in charge of the academic supervision for these inters. In February 99, IPYL organized places for two interns, one in the Palestinian Union for the handicapped and one in the General Union for the disabled sports.

"Preparation Team for: Our Contribution to the Human Beings":
This project was proposed by the Die Falken organization, IPYL was chosen to be in the preparation team for this activity which should take place in Summer 2000. The main theme of this project will be to expose young people to new methods, through workshops, study trips and lectures, about anti discrimination, anti fascism, anti militarization, and how the young generation should reject all forms of prejudice and discrimination. This first preparation meeting took place between 2-18/11/1998, in Potsdam/ Berlin/ Germany.

"Training Seminar for work camps Leaders":
Organized by IPYL in the city of Bethlehem between the period 13-20/11/1998. The main goal for this training was to provide members for the IPYL with the necessary skills to run an international work camp, in terms of management, finance, teamwork, democracy and conflict resolution. The training was financed and supported by ALERT/ Holland.

"International Congress for Voluntary Service":
IPYL participated in the general congress about the voluntary service in the world. The conference included workshops and study groups about different topics: EVS and Mediterranean Cooperation. It took place in Barcelona/ Catalonya/Spain between the period 9-14/12/1998. The congress was supported by the Autonomous Government of Catalonya and the European Commission DG XXII.

"Human Rights 1948-1998- 50 years of progress?"
Training seminar organized by the Norwegian Youth Council in Oslo/ Norway, between 5-16 December. The seminar aimed to educate young people from the Middle East about the Human Rights situation in the region and how to improve it. It was prepared, planned and hosted by LNU (Norwegian Youth Council).

"Preparation Team for the European Voluntary Service program":
IPYL was invited to participate in the preparation for the Youth Action for Peace seminar about the European Voluntary Service programs. The meeting took place in Brussels between 30/1-4/2/99.

"European Voluntary Service(EVS) Training Seminar":
Organized by Youth Action for Peace. The objective of this seminar is to train representatives of different youth organization to host EVS volunteers and to foster the program in the Middle East region. Took place in Bolsena\ Italy, between 17- 22/2/99. Sponsored by the European Commission.

"Alliance Technical Meeting":
IPYL participated in the annual meeting of the alliance for this year too. It took place in Turkey 5-10/3/1999. IPYL's work camps were advertised and discussed with all the members of the Alliance, who promised to send volunteers to our 1999 work camps.

"Committee of Directorate of the Youth Action for Peace":
During the YAP CD which took place in Italy in the period between 26-28/2/1999, IPYL was given the status of full branch of YAP. This new status, makes IPYL the first branch of this organization in Palestine, with full membership.

"National Coordinator for EVS Program in Palestine":
The IPYL is hosting 8 long-term European volunteers who are working with five different NGOs to develop programs and to be exposed to the Palestinian culture. This program lasts for six months starting March 1999. Sponsored by the European Commission/ European Voluntary Service(EVS).

"Implementing and Fostering EVS in the Mediterranean Countries" Seminar:
The aim is to train the different parties involved in the receiving of international EVS by providing an overview on EVS managing volunteer projects. ICYE European Association organized the seminar. It took place in Bouznika\ Morocco between the 17th and 23rd of April 99.


· "Working for peace and tolerance with multicultural groups"
The main goal of this training seminar was to provide the participants with the necessary skills on how to make an evaluation as an essential dimension of international youth work. Organized at the European Youth Council (EYC), Budapest, between April 5th - April 12th, 1999.

· "Preparatory Team Meeting for Women Issues":
The IPYL was invited to participate in this preparatory meeting by the Youth Action for Peace. The seminar will be held in Cluj/ Romania in October 99.It will cover different issues related to women and ways to improve it. The activity is supported by the European Commission.

· "Workcamps Leaders Training Seminar":
Organized by IPYL in cooperation with First Ramallah Group, in Ramallah. The aim was to train group of young leaders to lead the IPYL work camps for the year 1999. Issued covered in the seminar were, introduction about voluntary service, management, finances, conflict resolution, team work, cultural life inside the camp and distribution of work. The seminar was financed and prepared by the IPYL, it took place in Ramallah, 21/05/1999.

· "Youth Exchange Training Session":
Organized by the Norwegian Youth Council. The IPYL participated in this international activity in Amman/Jordan, with the aim of raising the youth awareness about the importance of youth exchanges in increasing their abilities. It was held between 7-18/7/99.

· "International Eco-peace seminar"
The IPYL participated in this activity which was organized by the International Eco-peace Village in Cyprus. The objective was to set up the bases for an Eco-peace village. The activity was carried on in larnaca/Cyprus between 31/7-8/8/1999.

· "International Voluntary Work camps":

IPYL organized a series of International Voluntary Work Camps as follows:
Jerusalem Old City (1-15/7/99) in cooperation with Al-Quds University, Islamic Waqf and the African Palestinian center. 15 international and 15 Palestinian volunteers participated in the camp. The work included the renovation of an old Turkish Bath, cleaning the Islamic cemetery and paining some walls inside the Aqsa Mosque yards.
Hebron Work Camp(18-31/7/1999) in cooperation with the Old City Renovation Committee and the Mennonite School. The work included cleaning, renovation and painting of some areas in the old city, and surrounding schools. 25 international and 20 Palestinian volunteers participated in the camp.
Ramallah Work Camp(3-18/8/1999) in partnership with the First Ramallah Group and the Municipality of Ramallah. The work included painting of sidewalks, renovation and cleaning. Also the program included visits to a variety of institutions in the Ramallah and other cities. We had about 100 participants, Palestinians and internationals.
Nablus Camp(9-22/8/1999) in cooperation with The Palestinian Red Crescent Society. The work included the renovation of a historical tourist site in the old city, painting schools and cleaning public parks. 17interational and 35 Palestinian participants volunteered at the camp.
Bethlehem Camp(6-25/9/1999) in cooperation with Artas Folklore Center. The work included renovation of old sites in the village, a tourists Path, painting and cleaning public schools in the village. We had 19 international volunteers and 10 Palestinians.

· "Training course on Euro-Med Cooperation":
The objective was to train young leaders on ways to fund raise for projects as part on the Euro-Med exchange programs. The session was held in Ankara/Turkey between 10-22/8/1999.

· "Study Trip to the Scandinavian Countries":
organized by Youth Action for Peace in Belgium, with the objective of a closer look on the Youth situation in these countries, and to look for possibilities for new partners from the youth organizations, to carry future joint projects in the future. This study trip included Denmark, Sweden, Norway and Finland and took place between 6-17/9/1999.

" Youth Information Centers Study Trip":
organized by BIJ and CIDJ in Belgium. The purpose is to train youth organizations leaders on mechanisms for collecting information, and introduce it to young people. The trip took place in Brussels and other cities, between 19-30/9/1999.

· "Women in Eastern and Western Societies Seminar"
The IPYL participated actively in this seminar, which covered women issues in different fields like education, health and socio-economical situation of women from both societies. Organized by Youth Action for Peace, took place in Cluj/ Romania, between the period 2-9/10/1999.

· "Committee of Directors for Youth Action for Peace".
This meeting is held twice a year, where the different branches of YAP meet to discuss different issues related to the organization as a whole. IPYL participated in this meeting and took a role in drawing the policies of YAP for the year 2000. The CD meeting was held in the city of Oradea/Romania in the period of 8-11/10/99.

· "Aqaba Seminar on Euro-Mediterranean Cooperation":
This seminar was organized by YAP (Youth Action for Peace), in cooperation with Hasan Youth Award and IPYL. The seminar dealt with way of future cooperation between Europe and the Middle East, and how to implement joint voluntary service activities between the two regions. The seminar took place in Aqaba/Jordan, between 12-21/11/99.

· "Meeting for New Partnerships" Seminar.
This activity was organized by the Spanish organization "Trebol De Cuatro Hijas". The objective was to establish new contacts and partnerships between the participating organizations and countries. IPYL participated actively in this seminar, and there will be future projects as an outcome of this activity. It took place in Leon/Spain, between 25-29/11/99.

· "EVS Training Seminar".
International Cultural Youth Exchange (ICYE) organized this activity, where people from Europe and the Middle East gathered to share ideas and experiences and way to develop joint projects in the framework of the European Voluntary Service (EVS) program, sponsored by the European Union. It took place in Cairo/Egypt, between 4-12/12/99.

· "Pedagogical tools in non-forma education and training in the Mediterranean area":
the main goal for this training seminar was to envisage new techniques and methodologies in training that targets the youth need in the Mediterranean region. More than 15 youth organizations participated in this activity. It took place in Bouznika, Morocco between the period 15-23 January, organized by International Cultural Youth Exchange (ICYE), financed by the EC.

· Study session " "Towards a United Strategy":
This study session was organized by YAP, ICYE, SCI. This study session aimed to put new strategy for youth work and cooperation between youth organizations. The session gave the participants first hand training in fundraising, communications, and lobbying. It took place at the European Youth Center (EYC) Strasbourg, France between the period, 23-29 /1/ 2000. Financed by the European Council.

· Preparatory meeting for the "Minorities Seminar"
Organized by Youth Action for Peace, to prepare for their seminar that should take place in April 2000. The first draft of the program and the methodology of the seminar was planned during this meeting. It took place at the European Youth Center, Strasbourg, France, between 30-31/1/2000. This meeting was financed by the European Council.

· Preparatory meeting for the Euro-Mediterranean cooperation Seminar. IPYL participated in the preparatory meeting that took place in Rome/Italy between 10-14/12/99. It was decided that the seminar would take place between9-14/2/2000. The seminar would discuss future projects between the two regions, and was organized by SCI, financed by the European Commission.

· "Training course for young leaders from Palestine"
Continuation for the training seminar organized by the French National Youth Council (CNAJEP). It aimed to give practical application to the theoretical part that took place in Ramallah last fall. The training included visits to different youth organizations working different fields, donor organizations as well as developed the skills of those leaders in fundraising and networking. Took place in Paris, France between 5-13/2/2000. Financed by the French ministry of Youth.

Preparatory meeting for " Water in the Desert Seminar"
Organized by Youth Action for Peace (YAP), to prepare for their forthcoming seminar that will take place in Egypt in April, 2000. The main goal for this meeting was to plan for the activity, in terms of methodology, finance, logistics and follow up. It took place in Paris, France between 13-15/2/2000. Financed by the European Commission.

· EVS Partner finding Seminar " Bridging the Gap"
Organized by Service Civil International (SCI). It aimed to help the participating organizations to find partners to cooperate on EVS projects, which included the criteria and the way of exchanging long term volunteers through the European Voluntary Service Program. It took place in Aix-En-Provence, France Between 19-24/2/2000, and financed by the European Commission.

· Committee Directorate of Youth Action for Peace
As an official branch for YAP. IPYL participated in the CD (General Assembly) meeting of YAP in Barcelona, Catalonya, between 18-20/2/2000. Financed by IPYL.

· "Conscienscious Objection seminar"
Organized by Youth Action for Peace (YAP), it aimed to study the possibilities of replacing the military service for the young people with civil service. The possible ways to do that, the options and the methods to do that, as well as, the potential approaches to lobby for this idea and encourage the governments to adopt it. The seminar took place in Barcelona, Spain, between 21-27/2/2000. The seminar was financed by the European Commission.

· "EVS Outgoing"
IPYL sent three long term volunteers to work in youth projects in France. Two men and one woman joint their projects in three regions in France: Le Creneau -Montcombroux, Beaumott Centre de Beaumotte in Beaumotte-Aubertrans and le Faï or Vaunières near Lyon. Their mission will last for 6 months from 1/3-1/9/2000. Organized by Solidarite Jeunesses, Paris, France.

· "Alliance Technical Meeting"
For the third year in arrow, IPYL participated in the technical meeting of the Alliance, which aims to disseminate the work camps of the largest 90 youth organizations in the world. Delegations from Europe, America, Asia and Africa. IPYL advertised its seven work camps for the season of 2000. Took place in Clermont-Ferrand, France between 9-13/3/2000. Financed by IPYL.

· "Study visit to Germany"
Organized by Kurve Wustrow in Germany. The study trip aimed to raise the awareness of young Palestinian Leaders on Youth issues, mainly in Conflict Resolution. It also included training on conflict resolutions techniques. The trip was planned since 15 months. A delegation of 7 Palestinians visited different youth organizations, the Green Peace, Amnesty International, anti nuclear prolifiration organizations as well as a visit to the German Parliament. The trip covered three cities in Germany; Wustrow , Humburg and Berlin. Took place between 1-14/4.2000. Financed by the European Commission.

· "Social Inclusion-Working with Minorities"
Training seminar organized by Youth Action for Peace. It aimed to give the participants a first hand training in how to deal with the minorities issues and rights. The seminar included training in developing the managerial skills of young leaders. It took place at the European Youth Center, Strasbourg, France, between the period 2-9/4/2000. Financed by the Council of Europe.

· " Conflict Resolutions Techniques"
organized by the Falkons. It aimed to give the participants with the necessary skills on how to deal with the conflict in a non-violent way. It took place at the European Youth Center in Strasbourg, France, between 10-13/4/2000. Financed by the Council of Europe and the Falkons.

· "In coming EVS"
IPYL received three long term volunteers as part of the European Voluntary Service program. The volunteers were placed to work at the IPYL main office, the Children's Art Center, and the Old city Rehabilitation Committee. The volunteers will spend the period between 4/4-4/10/2000 in these organizations. The project is financed by the European Commission and in cooperation with Youth Action for Peace.

· "Visiting groups"
The IPYL hosted between the period (February, 97- October, 99) forty visiting groups from different countries and different backgrounds, mainly from, France, Germany, Spain, Italy, Norway, Sweden, Denmark, USA, Switzerland, Belgium and the United Kingdom. The visits came to raise the awareness of the international community about the Youth situation in Palestine, as well as to discuss the possibilities of future cooperation and coordination in youth issues between the IPYL and these organizations.

· "Youth Information Center"
IPYL is currently seeking funds to establish a Youth Information Center, which should include departments for free basic training to the youth, to develop their working capacities and professional skills, as well as free access to the information library, and equipment.
