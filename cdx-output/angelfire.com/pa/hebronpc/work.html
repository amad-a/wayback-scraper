Some about us


VOLUNTARY WORK CAMPS


The International Palestinian Youth League (IPYL) considers the voluntary projects (work camps) as a way of embodying the respectful human virtues, through the serious voluntary work, which aims to serve the community, and explore those virtues through it’s work camps. Some of the main human virtues, which IPYL highly emphasizes on through it’s voluntary work are:- cooperation spirit, team spirit, and solidarity among the volunteers. Also it emphasizes on the continuation of connections between the volunteers from different cultures and backgrounds. Further cooperation is expected between the sending organizations and IPYL from one side and IPYL and local cooperated organizations from the other side. Those work camps include many activities, aiming to supply volunteers with some skills in leadership, group thinking, and decision making. Also it includes a number of social and cultural activities combined to some visits to Palestinian sights and projects, which enhances their knowledge towards Palestinian situation in general. Those activities are supposed to take in consideration the volunteers interests. IPYL works hardly on creating integration between both sides; the local community and international volunteers. At the end of each work camp the leadership and IPYL work hard to make sure that the continuation of connections between volunteers is established through distributing the addresses list to the participants.


Draft: The leadership of any work camp is given to the chosen leaders by the IPYL. After clarifying all the details to the leaders about their authorities and responsibilities in running the work camp program.


Leaders authorities:-


1-Setting the primary program for the work camp which includes:-

a- working days and times.


b- Free time.


c- Cultural activities.


d- Confirming appointments with associated organizations before the beginning of the work camp.


2-Running and proceeding the work camp which includes:-


a- dividing working teams if necessary .


b- setting working hours.


c- coordination with the associated organizations, the kind of work and it’s location.


d- Working on work camp’s problems and it’s solutions.


3-Carrying out the cultural program.


4-Determination of visiting times depending on the work camp circumstances.


5-agreeing on the presence of the local volunteers according to the nature of the work camp and it’s circumstances. The leaders have the right to hold of any local volunteer if the leaders have a good reason.


6-Spending money on the work camp out of it’s budget, according to the needs. And after discussing it with IPYL.


7-equating the work camp’s program according to it’s condition’s (the work camp).


8-leaders can’t banish any of the volunteers before having the agreement of IPYL.


NOTES:-


*Any announcements or regulations should be passed through the work camp conductor.


*Leaders of the Work camp are the only authorized part for giving instructions to the volunteers.


*The leaders are allowed to pass some of the authorities to volunteers according to the situations.


*Problems that could raise up in the work camp are supposed to solved by the work camp leader, and incase they were unable to deal with them they have to refer to IPYL. All of that is to be done away of the work camp atmosphere.


*In case of any disagreement between the leaders they have to solve it between them and IPYL, and without involving any other parts.


*Each volunteer is supposed to have a copy of the work camp’s program, instruction’s, and all needed information. Only official participants in the work camp are allowed to eat and/or sleep in the work camp.


THE CULTURAL ACTIVITIES includes:-


1- public activities :- such activities allow the participation of uninvolved members in the work camp such as members from the hosting association, members from the IPYL, or any others.


2- Private activities:- such activities don’t allow the participation of uninvolved members in the work camp.


Work camp leaders should take in consideration the following things:-


*sticking to the time table and following up with individuals, and associated organizations.


*taking in consideration the volunteers interests and needs.


*variety of activities, and avoiding the boring style eg. Intensive and consequent lectures.


*the cultural program should includes political, traditional and historical aspects, which give a clear image about Palestinian community.


*IPYL executive committee have the right to attend any of the work camp’s activities, and they represent legal references to the leader.
