Dutch University Gives Ph.D. to a Palestinian Researcher Studying Violence and Palestinian Children

Free Amsterdam University gave Dr. Samir Qouta, Director of Research at GCMHP, a Ph.D. for research conducted on violence and Palestinian children. The research centered on the effects of Israeli violence on Palestinian children on the cognitive and emotional capacities of children, and the relation between parents and children. The studies pointed to the importance of taking measured to care for children's mental well being as well as the family and social environment in which they live through social support to them and their families. The studies also indicated the importance of avoiding violence or watching violent scenes on television.

The university commended Dr. Qouta for these pioneer studies. Despite accusations by pro-Israel lobbyists that the studies are politically motivated and are propoganda, the university went ahead with the dissertation defense and praised the highly scientific nature of the studies despite their political implications.

Dr. Eyad El-Sarraj, Chairman of the GCMHP Board of Directors, was present at the defense. He stated that these studies are a clear indication of the progress of Palestinian scientific research, especially in the area of psychosocial sciences.
