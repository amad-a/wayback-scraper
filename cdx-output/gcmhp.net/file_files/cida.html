Cooperation Agreement between the Canadian Government and GCMHP

On 20 February, Mr. Jonathan Laine, Deputy Head of the Canadian Representative Office to the PNA, visited GCMHP and met Dr. Eyad El-Sarraj (Director-General), Christina Abdel Shafi (Project Officer), and Husam El-Nounou (PR Coordinator). The visit was for the purpose of signing a cooperation agreement between the Canadian Government and GCMHP to partially fund the Programme's Crisis Intervention Project.

The Project conducts therapeutic and counseling activities aimed at all sectors of Palestinian society subjected to the latest wave of Israeli violence. The Project also carries out training activities for professionals working with children and in the fields of education, as well as a public awareness campaign on how to deal with the psychological problem resulting from the Israeli aggression.

Mr. Laine explained that this initiative is part of a large Canadian project to support mental health in Palestine. This project consists of $US 500,000 in financial aid for Palestinian NGOs. "The reason we chose to support GCMHP is because we agree with the Programme's philosophy that is based on a community mental health approach." Mr. Laine stated. He also said that GCMHP's focus on preventive activities along side therapeutic ones is very effective, especially with regard to issues concerning children and women. Mr. Laine added that, "we work closely with the National Committee to Support Childhood; and GCMHP is a member of this Committee. GCMHP's Crisis Intervention Project falls within the parameters of the Palestinian plan to support childhood, and the Canadian strategy to support community mental health in Palestine."

Mr. Laine indicated that the Canadian Government "works with organizations that enjoy high levels of professionalism and have the capacity and skills to implement projects effectively." This is one of the reasons that led the Canadian Government to support GCMHP. The Canadian Ministry for International Cooperation is interested to fund projects that aim to help children in war-torn areas. Gaza is considered such an area. "Her Excellency Ms. Maria Mena (Minister for International Cooperation) approved the project to help Palestine since it goes along side the philosophy of Canadian strategy in achieving social development and well being, especially for children," he added.

On his opinion about future cooperation between the Canadian Government and GCMHP, Mr. Laine described this agreement as a stepping stone towards more cooperation over the next two years. He indicated that support will extend to the technical sphere in addition to financial aid. He stressed the desire of the Canadian Government to include PNA ministries (health, education, and social affairs) to work with civil society institutions to make services more accessible to the population, especially children.
