Gaza Community Mental Health Programme (GCMHP) Urgent Action Appeal

“I was on the floor, protecting my head- three men were beating me on my head and body. They sprayed gas on my face. I thought I was going to die. I could not breathe. One of the policemen started stomping his boot on my hand and I was bleeding… then they put me on the bed, tied my hands and legs- all the while beating me- and held my head and neck, choking me.”

This how Amneh Muna, a 25-year old Palestinian female political prisoner, described part of a beating episode at the hands (and feet) of Israeli prison wardens (in Abu Qbir prison) in the evening of 13 September 2001.

Mr. Issa Qaraqe’, General Director of the Palestinian Prisoners Club, stated, “Amneh was transferred to an isolated cell without any access to sun or proper ventilation.” He added, “She was brutally beaten. Even though her finger was broken, she did not receive any medical treatment.”

According to information obtained via the Women’s Affairs Technical Committee (WATC), that same evening witnessed the beating of another five Palestinian female political prisoners. They are Maha Al-Ekk(24 years), Abeer Amro (20 years), Su’ad Ghazal (18 years), Wujdan Bujeh (22 years), and Rabi’a Hamayel (15 years). The Israeli prison wardens followed the beatings by tying them to their beds in the same manner in which Amneh Muna was tied.

On the situation of female Palestinian political prisoners, Ms. Najah Duqmaq, a lawyer in the Mandela Institute for Human Rights, stressed that the conditions of female prisons have severely deteriorated. She stated, “In the last four months, the Palestinian female detainees have been attacked and beaten four time”. She also emphasized that the detainees are deprived of family visits and meeting with Palestinian lawyers from the West Bank or Gaza Strip. “Israeli authorities refuse to issue permits to Palestinian lawyers to travel into Israel in order to visit their clients incarcerated in Israeli facilities. Palestinian lawyers are also barred from representing Palestinian prisoners in front of Israeli courts”, she added.

According to WATC, in the past four months, numerous human rights organizations have reported that male and female wardens and riot police have brutally attacked and tortured Palestinian women detainees on several occasions, causing them severe injuries and lacerations on their bodies, not to mention sexually degrading them. Moreover, WATC notes that investigative reports have also revealed that nurses and medics in these detention centers have not sufficiently reported the abuse and torture inflicted on the detainees. Rather, they have participated in covering up the violence.

In light of the grave situation of female Palestinian prisoners, and the serious human rights violations they are subjected to at the hands of Israelis, GCMHP calls upon governments and nongovernmental organizations, human rights organizations and activists, and advocates of justice to condemn the barbaric treatment of female Palestinian prisoners in Israeli prisons.

Please send appeals and protests to any number of the following officials reminding them , among other things, that according to the United Nations Standard Minimum rules for the Treatment of Prisoners, Article 33, “Instruments of restraint, such as handcuffs and chains shall never be applied as a punishment.” Moreover, Article 53 states, “Women prisoners shall be attended and supervised by women officers.”
