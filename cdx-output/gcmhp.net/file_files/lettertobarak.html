Mr. Ehud Barak
Prime Minister
Office of the Prime Minister
Israel


Dear Prime Minister Barak,
I write you to express my concern over the death of an 18-year old boy
due to the explosion of military remnants left in one of the abandoned
IDF training sites in the West Bank. This brings to four the number of
people killed by unexploded ordnance (UXO) in the past two months alone.
Since 1997, UXO or landmines have killed 37 people.

This grave loss of life is caused by negligence that can be easily
avoided. Whoever and whatever caused the death of 37 people, the last of
which was 18-year old Massar Al-Kaabneh, should be held accountable to
the deed. It is unjustifiable and inexcusable for the IDF to leave
behind it dangerous materials that threaten people's lives. It saddens
me to know that if the first victim were someone other than a
Palestinian, measures would have been taken to solve this problem.


The next victim could easily be a tourist or even a settler. But this
alone should not move you to take action. The principle values of
humanity implore us to respect human life regardless of nationality or
religion. I urge you to take the necessary actions to ensure that IDF
training sites are combed and cleared of hazardous material.

Sincerely,

Dr. Eyad El-Sarraj
Chairman,
Board of Directors
