URGENT APPEAL 17th October 2000

Across Palestine: Patients Denied Medical Treatment as a Result of Closure Imposed by Israeli Occupation Forces

For the past 2 and a half weeks the West Bank and Gaza have been under 'closure' by the occupying Israeli army. Initially, the closure entailed sealing off the Occupied Palestinian Territories from occupied East Jerusalem and Israel. Permits allowing Palestinian to travel to or through these areas were revoked, leading, inter alia, to cutting off the West Bank and Gaza Strip. At a later stage, the closure was extended to sealing the individual Palestinian cities, towns and villages in the West Bank. All Palestinian locales are surrounded blockaded by Israeli troops, and some are physically blocked with large blocks of concrete placed on roads. Palestinians are thus being held prisoner in their respective cities, towns and villages across the entire West Bank, and are prevented from moving between them.

In addition to being terrifying, intimidating and economically suffocating, this imposed closure is a disaster for the health system. The ill and injured, especially in rural areas, are unable to reach hospitals, doctors' offices and pharmacies at a time when the need for medical attention is at its utmost. These closures do not make allowance for any exceptional cases, and many Palestinians have died or experienced avoidable deterioration in their condition due to the refusal of the Israeli troops to allow them to reach medical facilities. Just this morning a man died as a result of the occupying troops preventing him from leaving his village to receive dialysis treatment at the hospital in Nablus. These same troops routinely prevent ambulances from reaching the injured, often resulting in the death of the victim. Borders with Jordan and Egypt are closed, preventing patients who need special treatment from reaching hospitals in these countries. And these are only examples of hundreds, if not thousands, of cases of unnecessary suffering imposed on those in need of medical treatment.

From my personal experience as a Psychiatrist in the West Bank city of Ramallah, I can share the following:
My regular patients live throughout the West Bank. With the exception of those living in Ramallah city proper, none of them can currently reach my clinic, and I cannot reach them. These are patients in urgent need of medication and treatment and whose conditions are exacerbated by -- or who relapse as a result of -- the severe distress caused by the current security situation. Many of them are sealed off in villages that do not have a pharmacy, thus it is impossible for me even to phone in prescriptions as a stop-gap measure. In addition, in the areas that are equipped with pharmacies, there is less and less medication available due to the cut-off in supplies imposed by the closure. For my patients, the consequences are dire: In the cases of psychotic patients, without their medication and associated treatment, and particularly under the current stressful circumstances, they present a danger to themselves and those around them. Epileptics, who require antiepileptic drugs to prevent possibly life-threatening seizures, are in serious danger. Patients suffering from depression and anxiety disorders are likewise unable to obtain the medication and treatment that they desperately need. In these abysmal conditions, the risk of suicide is rising dramatically. And the list goes on.

My colleagues in the other various fields of medicine throughout the West Bank and Gaza have hundreds or thousands of disastrous tales of their own.

During this period, my offices receive at least 100 phone calls per day from panicked patients and family members, desperate to obtain treatment for themselves and/or their loved-one(s). I am helpless to assist them under the conditions of the closure. We are all trapped and vulnerable. No patient and no doctor should EVER be placed in this position of absolute helplessness if it is in any way avoidable. In this case, it is entirely avoidable. The total closure serves no purpose to the occupying forces except as a form of terror and intimidation and a show of power. Even if the mythical excuse of security were to have some merit, the indiscriminate and collective suffering caused to innocent civilians is entirely disproportionate.

In addition to being profoundly morally reprehensible, deliberately causing suffering to the innocent ill and injured is a violation of a vast array of international human rights norms. It effects everything from the right to life, to the right to be free from cruel, inhuman and degrading treatment and punishment, to the prohibition on collective punishment, to the right to health. In its all-pervasive cruelty, closure can be considered one of the most heinous instances of ethnically-based mass persecution.

We appeal to the international community, and in particular to the international medical community, to speak out actively and loudly against this travesty. The Israeli medical community is under a special obligation to act, as it is best placed to influence policy makers and public opinion (letters/calls should be directed to, for example, the Israeli Medical Association, the Israeli Ministry of Health, the Israeli Prime Minister's office. Contact information is below).
