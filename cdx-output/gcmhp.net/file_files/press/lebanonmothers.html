Urgent Action Appeal

Mothers of 19 Lebanese prisoners held in Israeli jails are on a hunger strike to protest the continued detention of their sons despite Israeli withdrawal from Southern Lebanon.
The mothers started the strike on 7 August opposite the UN headquarters in Beirut.

Following the Israeli withdrawal and the disintegration of its proxy militia, Lebanese civilians stormed Khiam detention center in Southern Lebanon and freed the prisoners held in it.
However, these 19 prisoners are being held in jails inside Israel.
The detention of these Lebanese prisoners constitutes a violation of the 4th Geneva Convention, to which Israel is a state party.

Some of this group have already ended the sentences issued by Israeli courts but remain imprisoned.
Others have not been tried despite years of detention. Israel is holding these Lebanese as “hostages.”

We call upon you to condemn this action and call for the immediate and unconditional release of these hostages by writing to the Israeli Prime Minister and the Minister of Justice at the addresses below.

AMAN-IRCT (Network of Centers of Rehabilitation of Victims of Violence and Torture in the Middle East and North Africa) is a regional network consisting of nine centers in the Middle East and North Africa working in the field of rehabilitating victims of violence and torture.
The main goal of this network is to encourage the participation of research and workers in the fields of social and health sciences in defining and investigating problems related to violence and torture.
AMAN-IRCT aims to contribute to solving these problems, support and encourage initiatives and activities in these fields at the local and regional levels including the establishment of specialized centers for the care and rehabilitation of victims of violence and torture, cooperate with local and international bodies within the framework of serving the network goals.
