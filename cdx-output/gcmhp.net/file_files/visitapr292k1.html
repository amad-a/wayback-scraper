The Government of the Netherlands Supports the Establishment of a Regional Community Mental Health Institute in Gaza

On 29 April 2001, GCMHP and the Government of the Netherlands signed a cooperation agreement, under which the Kingdom of the Netherlands funds the Program's Training and Education Department. Mr. Ron Muyzert, Head of the Netherlands Representative Office to the PNA, signed the agreement on behalf of the Government of the Netherlands; and Dr. Eyad El-Sarraj, GCMHP Director General, signed it on behalf of the Programme.

On this occasion, Dr. El-Sarraj stated, "the relation between GCMHP and the people of the Netherlands has been strong since the establishment of the Programme in 1990, when the first international fund to GCMHP came from the Netherlands. I am proud of the unique ties between the Programme and the Netherlands representatives in Palestine and Dutch Ministers of International Development and Foreign Affairs."

It should be noted that GCMHP hosted the Dutch Minister for International Development in the Programme on two occasions, in 1996 and 1998. Mr. Abdel Hamid Afana, GCMHP Deputy Director for Administrative and Financial Affairs and Director of Training and Education, stated that this agreement emphasizes the strong ties between the Programme and the Dutch people and government. He also said, "the Netherlands has always supported the Palestinian people and democratic civil society institutions. It has also granted substantial support to the PNA in the areas of infrastructure, judiciary, planning, and the seaport.

In its part, the Programme has also maintained strong ties with Dutch academic institutions such as Utrecht University, who is represented by Professor Wolters in the Academic Board of GCMHP's postgraduate diploma in community mental health and human rights. In addition, Dr. Samir Qouta, GCMHP Director of Research, acquired his Ph.D. with honors from Amsterdam Free University a few months ago. Also, the Government of the Netherlands and other Dutch organizations have supported GCMHP's work in human rights and rehabilitation of torture survivors for years."

Ms. Birgitta Tazelaar, the Desk Officer for Development Cooperation at the Netherlands Representative Office to the PA, stated that, "the Netherlands representative Office decided to support the Gaza Community Mental Health Programme for a period of three years, starting from April 1, 2001. The Netherlands have gained experience with the GCMHP since 1995 through different projects and believes that the work the center does is unique and has proven of great value to the Palestinian people.

The Netherlands attaches great importance to Human Rights and gladly supports organizations that promote human rights in every aspect. GCMHP does so through its services and training in "Mental health and Human Rights." Especially in these times, when so many children, men, and women are suffering from distress because of the occupation and the violence, the work of the center is needed even more than ever. The Netherlands Government attaches great importance to the position and well being of women, who have been denied so much. We value the work the center does to empower Palestinian women so that they can participate on an equal basis in the development of their society.

Human rights (including women's rights), participation, dignity and security are indispensable aspects for the well being of each individual. These values are currently being denied to the Palestinian population. We count on the GCMHP to continue with all its efforts and expertise, to contribute to the development of these important universal values."

Concerning the agreement, Mr. Afana said that the most important item in the agreement is the assistance GCMHP would receive to upgrade its personnel and administrative systems in preparation to establishing a regional institute of community mental health and human rights in Gaza. The institute would attract students and teachers from the whole region. GCMHP's unique experience in the field of training and education will enable it to supervise the institute. In this regard, the Programme has been implementing the postgraduate diploma in community mental health and human rights since 1997.

The diploma is a 2-year course with the first one for theoretical study and the second for practical clinical and community training. Trainees are exposed to the basis of psychiatry, psychology, and counseling through a culturally sensitive curriculum adapted to Palestinian culture and society. Students are educated to attain a better understanding of mental health and human rights in Palestine in order to work towards resolving psychosocial problems while utilizing cultural components such as the family. This understanding is applicable to similar Arab societies.

The topics taught to students include: clinical psychiatry, health education, field work, cognitive therapy, scientific research, child development, culture and mental health, human rights, and democracy.

Upon graduation, students receive accredited diplomas from the Islamic University and the Palestinian Ministry of Education."

In addition to the diploma, the Programme conducts training courses to professionals in Palestinian governmental and non-governmental organizations, including physicians, nurses, psychologists, social workers, security personnel, teachers, and counselors.

At the end of the signing ceremony, Dr. Eyad El-Sarraj thanked Mr. Ron Muyzert on behalf of the Programme. He also extended his thanks to the people and government of the Netherlands.
