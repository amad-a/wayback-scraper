Public Relations Department

The nature of the work of the GCMHP requires constant contact - academic, political, and social - with many other public organisations and governmental institutions. Publicity, in terms of raising the international profile of mental health issues in Gaza, is also an important part of the work of many of the departments. The Public Relations Department therefore plays a central role in the Programme.

The Public Relations department is responsible for creating and maintaining relations with international organisations, governments, and individuals, as well as with local NGOs. The GCMHP is a founding member of the Palestinian Non-Governmental Organisations Network (PNGO), a committee established to coordinate between NGOs on one hand, and legislate between NGOs and the Palestinian National Authority on the other.

The biannual international conferences are one of the highlights of the work of the GCMHP. The PR department organises the logistics and details of these events. The aim of the conferences are to encourage scientific exchange and cooperation which leads to opportunities for education, research, and funding.

The department has organized 5 international conferences since the establishemen of the Programme. The last two have been in 1998 and 1999. In December 1998, the Programme hosted the 6th International Research and Advisory Panel (IRAP) Conference on Forced Migration. Despite a last-minute change of venue from Gaza to Jerusalem due to President Clinton's visit to Gaza, the conference was a success, with 60 speakers and attended by 180 professionals from all over the world in the field of migration.

Between 21-23 November 1999, the Programme held its fourth international conference, on "Women in Palestine". In the struggle for freedom, many women's issues have been left undiscussed. This conference themes included gender and development, women in Islam, women and human rights, and the role of women in the developing community. About 800 participants attended, including about 150 international visitors.

As well as international links, the Public Relations department emphasises the place of the GCMHP within the local community. In May 1998, the magazine "Amwaj" (Waves) was launched. This is a pioneer project in raising local awareness and combating stigma as it is the only magazine devoted to mental health issues in Palestine. Edited by Husam El-Nounou and Eyad El Hassani, it comes out once every two months. Most articles are by GCMHP staff, about a range of issues concerned with mental health in the community. "Amwaj" ends with a discussion of a specific concern, such as suicide or violence against women. The magazine is aimed at the local community: it is distributed to governmental institutions and NGOs, as well as professionals working in fields related to mental health in Gaza.

The department compiles, edits, and publishes the GCMHP monthly report and annual report that cover the different activities. The last annual report is posted on the web site. The department issues press releases and statements upon recent political developments. It updates the web site with material on various GCMHP activities.

Another important aspect of the PR department's work is hosting visitors. Local, regional, and international visitors come to GCMHP; and the department facilitates their visits. The facilitation includes providing the necessary information about the Programme and the general situation, field visits to get acquainted with the living conditions and culture of the Palestinian people, and logistic arrangements if necessary.
