Special Report: Intifada Update (No. 8)

Palestinian Human and Material Losses Inflicted by Israel during the Intifada (Uprising) September 28th, 2000 until May 18th, 2001

Number of Palestinians killed by Israeli security forces and settlers:
Palestinians in the West Bank, the Gaza Strip, and east Jerusalem: 489
Palestinians in Israel: 13
Palestinians in southern Lebanon (killed in clashes on Israeli northern borders): 2
Total (including >147 deaths inflicted among children aged 18 and below): 504

Number of Palestinians injured by Israeli security forces and settlers:
Live ammunition: 2,770
Rubber bullets: 4,925
Tear gas: 4,324
Miscellaneous: 2,005
Total (including 6,000 injuries inflicted among children below the age of 18): 14,024
Permanent Disabilities: (including 25 cases of complete blindness) 1,500

Palestinians arrested by Israeli authorities for political reasons:
Within Israeli territories: 1,189
Within Palestinian territories: (1000 have been released) 1,387
Total: 2,576

Residential Palestinian buildings completely destroyed by Israeli attacks:
Gaza Strip: 226
West Bank: 333
Total: 559
Total of residential buildings shelled: 3,669

Number of olive trees uprooted form Palestinian land: 25,000
Area of Palestinian cultivated land destroyed: 3,669,000 m²
Palestinian homes demolished by Israeli authorities since 28/9/2001: 116

Since 1967, Israel has confiscated almost 750,000 acres of land from the 1.5 million acres comprising the West Bank and Gaza Strip. Since 1967, almost 200,000 tress have been uprooted by Israeli forces from Palestinian land. Since 1987 only, more than 2,500 homes have been demolished on the basis that they were not supported by the required construction permits.

Israeli attacks against doctors and ambulance drivers:
Number of ambulance drivers killed: 3
Number of doctors killed: 1
Number of doctors and ambulance drivers injured: >160
Number of ambulances hit: 109

Number of Palestinian schools shut down due to Israeli siege: 174
Number of Palestinian students deprived from attending school: 90,000

Impact of Israeli closures on Palestinian economic life:
Number of Palestinians unemployed due to the closures: 257,000
Unemployment rate in the West Bank and the Gaza Strip: 47%
Total income losses for Palestinian workers previously employed inside Israel: $ 3.6 million/day
Actual losses: Shortfall in GNP between Sept.-Mar. $ 1.5 billion
Decrease in per capita income: 47%
Percentage of Palestinians living below poverty line: 53%
Estimated loss if closures continue in 2001: $ 1.7 billion
