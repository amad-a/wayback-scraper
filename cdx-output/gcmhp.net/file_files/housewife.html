Injured woman dies at an Israeli roadblock

On 28 February 2001, Nada Abdel Jabbar, a 45-year old housewife with 8 children from the village of Beit Fourik fell while working at home and started to bleed severely. Her family called an ambulance; but Israeli soldiers at the checkpoint prevented it from entering the village. So, Nada had to be transferred to the checkpoint in a private car. Her family approached the soldiers in order to get permission to carry her by foot to the ambulance waiting on the other side. Despite Nada's critical condition, the soldiers refused to let her and her family pass-not even on foot.

Her family was then forced to take a dirt back road through the neighboring village of Salem. Again they were stopped by Israeli soldiers and denied access into Nablus where the main hospital is.

In a third attempt, the family took yet another dirt road and finally managed to reach the hospital after 45 minutes. Without the Israeli imposed siege, the trip from Beit Fourik to Nablus takes about 10 minutes. By the time Nada reached the hospital, she had already lost a lot of blood. Despite all efforts to save her life, she died two hours later.

This is yet another crime committed by the Israeli army against civilian Palestinians. Nada and her family were not terrorists and did not pose any security threat to Israel. They were trying to go to a Palestinian town for medical treatment.

We call upon all the advocates of human rights and all those that value human life to call upon the Israeli government to respect international law and the provisions of the 4th Geneva convention regarding civilian population in war zones.

Sincerely,

Dr. Eyad El-Sarraj
Director-General
