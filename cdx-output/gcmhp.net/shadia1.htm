“FROM THE GRAVE..A CALL FOR JUSTICE”
by: Shadia El Sarraj
Women’s Empowerment Project
 
 
Reem, a 13 year-old girl, was killed by her father in cold blood. One night, he asked her to join him for a walk. After killing her, he returned home looking for her and asking everybody: “Have you seen Reem?” After a week of searching, her body was found by the seashore. 
By chance one of his sons followed him that night and watched his sister killed and buried. His school teacher noticed a change in his temper and behavior. The teacher called the police, who asked him about what happened. The young brother told them everything. “That night my father asked Reem to join him for a walk. I felt jealous and I wanted to join them. By the seashore they stopped. My father dug a hole and asked Reem to jump in, then he broke her neck, buried her and returned back home asking: ‘Where is Reem?’”

The obvious question is why a young girl would be killed by her father? In Reem’s case, police investigations found that her father suspected that Reem had illegal sexual relations!!

Reem’s parents were divorced when she was only few months old. After their separation and remarriage to other people, she joined her grandparents. At the age of 11, her father started to force her to come back to him. According to the Personal Status Law, the father has the right of female custody at the age of 9. The reason he wanted to bring her back was to serve his new wife who has many children. Reem became the house-maid and was taken out of school.

When Reem’s body was examined, she was found to be a virgin. Her body was full of scars and bruises and her hair was shaved. Also, there was a scar of about 25 inches on her head.

The father is now in prison, but according to the Personal Status Law this crime will be considered an honor killing, not a murder. This means that he will be sentenced to a maximum of 2-3 years.
