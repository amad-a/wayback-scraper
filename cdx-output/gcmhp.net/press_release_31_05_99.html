Press Release
( 31.05.99 )
(Public statment on the death of SHADI SAQER in
an Israeli prison )

Shadi Saker Abu Dahrouj (21 - from Jabalia) died in his holding cell in the Beir Sheva prison, better known as the Negev or Ansar III prison.  What was the crime that Shadi committed to merit this punishment?  Did he kill the Israeli PM? Did he plan to blow up a bomb in Tel Aviv? Was he a member of a terrorist organization? Was he a ticking "time bomb" as the Israeli security establishment classifies "dangerous" prisoners? The answer simply is that Shadi was working in Israel, and was arrested by the Israeli security.

Shadi was suffering from a heart condition.  Where were the doctors of the prison? How was he locked up in a cell without notice of his physical ailment? Are doctors in prisons only to evaluate how much damage prisoners can take?

This shocking incident comes at a time when our hope was rekindled in breaking the deadlock in the peace process.  Yet, it comes as a reminder to us that peace cannot be achieved without all sides unconditionally agreeing to the respect of human rights.  The respect of human rights should be a key component to any political settlement in the region.

In order to prevent the occurrence of this incidence we should:

1- Request that international human rights organizations intervene to guarantee Israeli adherence to international declarations on human rights.
2- Request that an independent, international investigating committee visits Israeli prisons to evaluate the conditions of Palestinian prisoners.
3- Requesting the international community to pressure Israel to stop torture and abolish all regulations allowing the use of torture.
4- Requesting the international community to pressure Israel to release all Palestinian prisoners?  If they are terrorists, what is the classifications of all the "free" Israelis walking the streets and Knesset halls with Palestinian blood on their hands.
5- Requesting the sponsors of the peace process and the donor countries to include the respect of human rights as a key component in any political settlement, and a precondition for economic aid.
 

Dr. Eyad El-Sarraj
