"Surprise…Surprise!”
    By Dr. Eyad El-Sarraj

    Nothing in this strange world surprises us anymore. That is why people smiled in mockery as they listened to the representative of the Attorney General testifying that, after 50 days of the arrest, the AG knew nothing about of Dr. Abdel Aziz El-Rantisi’s detention (refer to the press release of the Palestinian Center for Human Rights, 27 May 1998).

    Mr. Fayez Abu Rahma did not surprise us when he resigned from the post of AG.  We expected him to resign earlier because he is a man of the law who upholds the principles of justice, and will try to implement his beliefs.  In addition, we knew that he was chasing a mirage.

    We were not surprised at the arrest of some detainees one hour after releasing them.  Forcing Chief Justice Kosay El-Abadlah into retirement a few weeks ago did not surprise us, nor did compelling High Court Judge Abdel Fattah of Ramallah to retire after deciding to release some Birzeit students.

    However, what surprises us is the simplicity with which the representative of the AG testifies bitter truth about how we live in a time lacking respecting the judicial system and its decisions.  This testimony was like making a factual statement about a normal thing of everyday life to which the people have become accustomed.

    In reality, the testimony presented by the representative of the AG is a rebellion against the Arab norms of total submission to the establishment as in the famous Chinese proverb, “no hear, no listen, no speak”.  In this case, the person spoke the untold truth.

    This testimony expresses the utter humiliation felt by people working in the judicial system.  This representative is testifying that the AG is like the wronged husband, the last to know.  He cannot defend people because he does not know why they were detained.  He does not know why they are prevented visitation rights, why they are not put in front of a judge within 24 hours, etc…

    In the end, it seems that the last to know is not the wronged husband or the Attorney General, but the Law itself…
