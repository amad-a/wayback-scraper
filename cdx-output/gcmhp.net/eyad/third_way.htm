“The Third Way”
    By Dr. Eyad El-Sarraj
    Scientists have proven that anxiety and depression can be causes of death. They also confirmed that life expectancy in developed countries is higher than in developing countries. For example, life expectancy in Sweden is nearly eighty years, whereas it is much lower in this part of the world.
     
    Some primary causes of increased life expectancy include a balanced system of nutrition, availability and high quality of medical services, adequate exercise and a non-smoking environment. In addition, it has become obvious that effectively coping with stress is another fundamental aspect of this equation. As a result, interest in psychological problems and family relations has increased, and mental health clinics have spread all over the world. It is now normal in the United States, for example, to go to one’s psychiatrist/therapist regularly, just as one would go to the dentist or sports club.
     
    In our country, however, people still feel ashamed to talk about their problems to a psychiatrist or other mental health professional; and people continue to believe in spirits, possession and magic. I have seen the long lines in front of the dens of fortune-tellers, amulet-makers and exorcists. People turn to these individuals in times of national crisis, when feelings of helplessness increase. However, the danger inherent in this phenomenon is not difficult to discern: I saw with my own eyes the effects of barbaric beatings at the hands of exorcists, which led to the death of an epileptic girl and that of a girl suffering from hysterical paraplegia.
     
    Nonetheless, given the current atmosphere of helplessness, of feelings of surrender to fate in the face of Israel, of loss of faith in the Palestinian Authority and in Arab regimes, it is not surprising that the number of fortune-tellers and magicians in our midst is on the increase. Likewise, the tribal mentality has taken hold of people, leading to a splintering of society and leaving pessimism and anger as the only common denominators.
     
    Thus, at a time when scientists are discovering the complexity of the human brain and determining the different chemical processes that govern human behavior, people in this part of the world are trying to avoid reality and deal with their feelings of hopelessness and despair by turning to voodoo and magic.
     
    Modern science has proven that depression sometimes has chemical causes due to the malfunctioning of certain chemicals secreted by the brain. This malfunctioning leads to a disturbance in the electrical activity in the brain, which in turn leads to deterioration in some cognitive and physical functions. Another interesting discovery is that continuous stimulation of the brain leads to the activation of idle brain cells and rejuvenates brain activity.
     
    This finding encourages me to say that our stagnant political atmosphere needs to be revived with new ideas. New blood needs to be pumped in so as to stimulate the cells and brain.
     
    The Palestinian political picture has two main colors: The Palestinian Authority (PA) and the Islamists. While the PA is an authoritarian regime holding the reigns of power and controlling the military and security machines, the Islamists remain on the sidelines, out of their objection to Oslo and its by-products. The Islamists have also suffered tremendous blows as a result of the suicide operations, which were perceived as a declaration of war on all parties. Thus, the PA has dominated the political arena and has deprived it of any real debate between the political factions, at a time when the Palestinian people most need such a debate.
     
    The void on the political stage can be explained by many factors, one of which is the communal state of shock felt at the collapse of the Palestinian dream. This has led some to stay in the “trenches” and grasp the last thread of fading hope. However, this vacuum in the political arena has become dangerous, indicating a serious lack of popular influence in the decision-making process.
     
    To overcome this problem, the Palestinian political environment requires overwhelming changes bordering on a Cultural Revolution. Such a revolution should be headed by a group of thinkers to bring to light a third way and to reach out to the masses in Palestine and the Diaspora with a message of civilization and development. We need a group of men and women who will preserve the rights of the Palestinian people and who will offer a program designed to stop the deterioration of our situation. We need a party that will lead the people to a victory on the political level, will build a democratic, civil society that respects the Law, and will strive to give the Palestinian people freedom and dignity.
