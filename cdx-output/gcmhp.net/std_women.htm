A Study Day on
“Suicide among Palestinian Women”
 

On 20 June, 1998, the Women’s Empowerment Project (WEP) held a study day on “Suicide among Palestinian Women” in the training hall at the Gaza Community Mental Health Program.  Representatives from the Program, Ministry of Social Affairs, Ministry of Wakf and Religious Affairs, Ministry of Health, and Governmental Psychiatric Hospital attended the study day, which aimed to coming up with therapeutic and preventive mechanisms of action to this problem.

Psychologist Azza Muheisin of the WEP presented a paper on “Suicide among Palestinian Women” in which she pointed to the deteriorating political, economic, and social conditions that resulted in increased social violence against women and children.  She also presented the preliminary results of a study on suicide and university students’ opinions on this problem and the suicide statistics from January 1996 to June 1998.  These statistics showed a significant increase in the number of women who attempt suicide and the number of women who are killed.  In addition, Ms. Muheisin presented the results of a study on 25 women who attempted suicide and highlighted their age, educational and economic background, and the reasons that drove them to attempt suicide.

Ms. Abier Jabir, Legal Counselor at the WEP, talked about the legal aspects of suicide.  She pointed to the existence of various laws regarding this issue, and pointed to the fact that these laws are not enforced.  Ms. Jabir presented a number of cases at the Women’s Empowerment Project as examples of the lack of enforcing the laws pertaining to suicide.

After discussing the topic at hand, the participants agreed on preventive and therapeutic mechanisms of action between their respective organizations.
