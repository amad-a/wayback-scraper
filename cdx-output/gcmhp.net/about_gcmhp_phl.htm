Philosophy And Strategy

Gaza Community Mental Health Programme was established in April 1990, to meet the immediate needs of the population of Gaza Strip for mental health care during the upheaval of the Intifada and the systematic state organized violence. Much of our activities were to help the traumatised children and the victims of torture. The most immediate task was to train the workers who would deliver such a service when only few had any experience in Mental Health . In a culture of conservatism, ancient strong traditions and resistance to change, our endeavour was a challenge. Taboos and ignorance deform growth. Believing in the devil possession of the "mad" and the resort to exorcism, the presentation of the body to express the emotional suffering , the resistance to acknowledge "weakness" are to be challenged .

The first four years were then spent on training a selected number of workers and to engage ourselves in the community education and train community workers, doctors, teachers, nurses and parents . In the same time we established a wide network of friends, supporters and professionals in Palestine and elsewhere . We invited and hosted hundreds of experts friends, many conducted courses, seminars or workshops . We campaigned for human rights joining our friends in Palestine, Israel, and the rest of the world .

With the signing of the declaration of principles between the PLO and Israel, the Palestinian community entered a new era with immense challenges and opportunities . The national strategy should be aimed at strengthening the human resources and democracy, rebuilding the social fabric and infrastructure, and starting intensive and dynamic debate between all sectors of the Palestinian community on the future of political. economic, environmental, educational and human strategies . In many ways we are a nation coming out from the rubble with the determination to survive and with the new spirit of building. We have to acknowledge our losses, our pain and our trauma . Thousands of our children and many more of our adults were exposed to violence and torture. They lost their education and their dreams . Many grieve in silence and some will never forget . An immediate task of the GCMHP will be to treat and rehabilitate the victims of both Israeli torture and communal structural violence. In the meantime preventive community work and education should be started with an emphasis on children, family and mental health .
Due to a long history of living under colonial, oppressive rule, the Palestinian community has always felt alienated from the authority of the land which has affected attitudes towards public and state property and affairs. In the spirit of building the country, community awareness campaigning should start with sensitivity in order to build a strong identification between the individual and the community based on respect for human rights and democracy. Taboos such as the abuse of women and children should be challenged and exposed .

In primary health care sector, most doctors are poorly grounded in the areas of mental health and psychiatry . This ignorance has serious effects given that almost all psychiatric cases are presented in somatic forms and complaints. The education of all workers in the health sector should include the mental health component .

Children should be the central focus of educators, planners, and community leaders . Parents and teachers should be encouraged to communicate with children and be sensitised to children's perception and behaviour.

Women have traditionally been denied fundamental rights. In a traditional patriarchal society like ours, man's authority is dominant. Women should be encouraged to take equal place in building the society alongside men .

GCMHP will work in co-operation and co-ordination with all Palestinian institutes whether governmental or non-governmental . GCMHP will continue to function as an independent non-governmental organisation. Over the period of five to seven years GCMHP aims to become a focal point for education, learning and training with academic accreditation. The need for strengthening our resources have been never greater, and the support of our friends is vital.

           
    Dr. Eyad El-Sarraj President
