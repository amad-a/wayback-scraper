PRESS RELEASE
Gaza Community Mental Health Program
Fourth International Conference
"WOMEN IN PALESTINE"
21 November 1999

 
 

The Gaza Community Mental Health Programme's fourth international conference began today in the Rashad El-Shawa Cultural Center in Gaza City, Palestine. The theme of this year's November 21-23 conference is Women in Palestine. The conference brings together over 800 professionals, researchers, academics, and activists from throughout the world to discuss the status of women and their problems, aspirations, and future role in development. It includes about 80 presentations in 24 workshops.
The conference features a photo exhibit by GCMHP’s Women’s Empowerment Project.  There is also an exhibition of handicraft products of different women’s organizations in Gaza.

Ms. Sama Aweidah of Women’s Studies Center, Palestine, delivered the opening speech: Palestinian Women's Rights from a Palestinian Feminist Perspective. Ms. Aweidah argued that despite all the sacrifices Palestinian women have offered for building the society, they still have a low status in the society. She pointed out various reasons for this like the exclusion of women from decision-making positions, the discrimination of women in the law, as well as social stereotypes of women as week creatures who can not be trusted, nor take responsibility. Ms. Aweidah further pointed that as low status of women is an obstacle to development of the society, it needs to be upgraded. We can not ignore the requirement for women when building the Palestinian State. The development process begins with the human being and it ends with the human being. Therefore, she hopes that the issue of women will soon be placed in its right order.
 

Dr. Amin Mekki Medani, chief technical advisor of the UN High Commissioner for Human Rights, stressed that women’s rights are an integral and inalienable part of human rights. He further stressed the importance of a symbiotic partnership that law, legislators, civil society and the State share in the matter of the rights of women. He claimed a special responsibility rests upon civil society and the community to support law enforcement and prosecution with the needed demand for their response. He expressed hope to see more coordination between communities and law enforcement to eliminate crimes typically carried out against women. In conclusion he said that the UN High Commissioner for Human Rights is available to support and offer technical assistance on the ground and through access to treaty bodies and the UN system at large.

The plenary session was followed by the Awards’ Ceremony for some Pioneer Palestinian Women, where Dr. Eyad El-Sarraj, Director of the Gaza Community Mental Health Programme presented awards to Ms. Najla Yassin (Um Nasser), Head of the General Union of Palestinian Women, Her Excellency Ms. Intisar El-Wazir, Minister of Social Affairs, Dr. Hanan Ashrawi¸ Secretary General of Muftah, The late Dr. Firyal El-Bannah,  the late Ms. Samieha Khaliel, The Women’s Affairs Center and finally to Um Jabir Wishah

Three concurrent workshop sessions were held in the morning. In the first one on Women and Human Rights, Dr. Abdallah Abu Eid of Najah University, Nablus, spoke on Palestinian Women’s Struggle for Equality. He stressed the irrationality of asking for equity at the present time but rather stressed stepwise fusion. Also he suggested collaboration rather than opposition of men in their request.
Ms. Turid Smith Polfus of University of Oslo, Norway, delivered a presentation on Are Human Rights Anti Women?  She stressed that even international human rights charters did not perserve women’s rights as they were all with androcentic focus. She also made comparison about domestic violence and violence at hands of prison officials where she fund strikingly similarities on methods and impacts on the victims.
Ms. Areen Hawari of the Arab Association for Human Rights, Nazareth, spoke on Palestinian Women in Israel. Ms. Hawari claimed that Palestinian women in Israel are abused both as Palestinians as well as women. She also described the discrimination between Arabs and Jews in many items of Israeli Law.

The second workshop was on Women in Islam.  Professor Raja Bahlul of Birzeit University and Dr. Mustafa Abu Sway of Al-Quds University were prevented from giving their presentations on Islamic Perspectives on Women and Feminism and Women in Islam: The Cultural vs. the Religious as they were not given the permission by the Israelis to enter Gaza.
Dr. Violette Dagguerre of the Arab Commission for Human Rights in France spoke on Hidjab, Excision, and Marriage in Islam. Dr. Dagguerre’s claim that hidjab is underestimating the women’s position in community led to a discussion on her point of view on the hidjab and islam.
Dr. Omaima Abu Bakr of Cairo University spoke on The Role of Men in Islam. Dr. Abu Bakr referred to contemporary interpretation of the Quaran, where responsibility lies on both men and women to take care of the family.

The morning's third workshop was on Women and Labor. Ms. Laila Al-Atshan of East Jerusalem YMCA Rehabilitation Program spoke on Handicapped Women and Employment Opportunities. She said that the work opportunities for women in general are few, however they are even less for handicapped women. Further, handicapped women suffer from lack of opportunities of education and training.
Ms. Zeinab El-Ghoneimi of Ministry of Labor spoke on Palestinian Women and Labor. She argued that the low rate of women in the work force is due to high rates of unemployment in general, weakness of economic structures, and the correlation between Palestinian and Israeli economy.
Mr. Luay Shabaneh of the Palestinian Central Bureau of Statistics spoke on Women and Labor in the Palestinian Areas. He outlined basic indicators related to work marketing and women’s participation. He argued that women’s participation in the work force is determined by the women’s education level, culture and social status.

During the lunch break, the film Children of Shatila was screened.

In the afternoon, three workshops were held. In the first session, which was on GCMHP's Women's Empowerment Project, Hana Wali from the WEP spoke on The Philosophy and Approach of the Women’s Empowerment Project. She outlined the main idea of the project, which is based on empowering violated women. Ms. Ayat Abu Jayyab, also from the WEP, spoke on the Project’s Achievements and Obstacles. Ms. Rana El-Farra, WEP, spoke on Violence against Women in the Gaza Strip. She asserted that violence against women is prominent in male-dominated societies where democracy and Good Governance and social awareness are absent.
Dr. Samir Qouta of the GCMHP presented a paper on Scientific Deciplinary Variables and Mental Health Problems of Women. His paper focused on the depressive personality traits being influenced by social and cultural pressures, including spouse and family relations.

The second workshop was on Arab Women and Social Challenges.
Ms. Monica A. Awad of UNICEF delivered a presentation on Gender Audit. She discussed how gender is taken into consideration in projects implemented by UN organizations in Palestine. She pointed to some of the gender related mistakes and errors made in the professional setting as well.
Dr. Hadeel Qazzaz, (an independent) researcher from Palestine spoke on Palestinian Women and the Feminization of Poverty. Dr. Qazzaz identified the concept of feminizing poverty, excluding women in development on the basis of discrimination. Then talked about the caused for this phenomena and the methods of correcting it.
Ms. Alia Arasoughly, a Palestinian producer and director presented a paper on Double Worlds – Video Clip Women and Palestinian Teenage Girls. She talked about the importance of media in affecting people's attitudes. She discussed video clips as expensive, short TV productions. She analyzed some video clips and their effects of the viewers.

The third afternoon workshop was on Women and Development.
Mr. Omar Shaban Ismail gave a presentation on Palestinian Women and Entrepreneurship. He discussed the reasons why Palestinian women start their business and the ways of spending their earnings.
Ms. Shanti Arulampalam of (Survivors Associated, Sri Lanka) spoke on
The Role of Women in the Developing Society of Sri Lanka. Ms. Arulampalam compared between the status of women in Palestine and Sri Lanka.
Ms. Khitam Shweideh of El-Khalas National Islamic Party, Palestine, spoke on The Role of Women in Community Development. She discussed the role of women in Islam starting with a historical description and ending with a contemporary view on the role of women in Palestine.
Dr. Randa Nasser of Al-Quds University spoke on Empowerment of Palestinian Women Managers and Political Leaders. Dr. Nasser presented a qualitative research involving 5 women about the definition of empowerment.
 

In the evening the First Graduation Ceremony for the Post-graduate Diploma in Community Mental Health was held at the Rashad El-Shawa Cultural Center. Mr. Abdelhamid Afana, Director of Education and Training, GCMHP, Dr. Eyad El-Sarraj, Director of the Gaza Community Mental Health Programme, Dr. Riyad Zanoun, Palestinian Minister of Health, Dr. Ihsan Al-Agha, Dean of Post-graduate studies at the Islamic University, and Mr. Geir O. Pedersen, Head of the Norwegian Representative Office to the PNA gave speeches. At the end of the ceremony, the diplomas were distributed to the students.
