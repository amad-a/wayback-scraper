A Study Day on Refugees’ Mental Health
 
 

 The “Rehabilitating Survivors of Human Rights Violations Project” organized a study day on “Refugees’ Mental Health”. The study day was on the different aspects of the psychosocial problems refugees suffer from as a result of expulsion from the homeland and moving to a new living environment.

Dr. Samir Qouta, Research Officer, initiated the day with a lecture on “The Consequences of Uprooting: Palestinian Refugees” in which he presented some Palestinian and international studies on refugees. Dr. Qouta talked about some psychological phenomena of migration such as increased levels of anxiety as well as some psychosomatic effects.

Anwar Wadi, social worker, followed with a presentation on “Refugees’ Psychological and Social Balance” which pointed out that, despite the large number of refugees from different places and cultures, similar factors affect their psychosocial equilibrium. He also talked about the different stages of psychosocial balance and the difficulties refugees face in a new environment.

Dr. Abdel Aziz Thabit, Project Director followed Mr. Wadi by giving a paper on “Mental Problems of Refugees and Non-refugees and the Cultural Factors that Affect Coping”. He pointed to the problem exile and the effects of losing family and possession on life in a new environment. Dr. Thabit also talked about the Palestinian experience citing that wide scale depression appeared in the first few years after the uprooting of 1948. He also stated that Palestinian refugees still have inner feelings of victimization that in turn lead to the continuation of depression and anxiety. Dr. Thabit then explained the cultural differences between refugees and original inhabitants.

Following the lectures, the floor was opened for discussion on the various issues discussed, and it was agreed that more workshops should be organized to discuss ways of solving refugees’ psychological problems.

Note: GCMHP will host the 6th IRAP Conference on Forced Migration on 13-16 December 1998 in Gaza. This conference will feature numerous research studies on various refugee issues.
