PRESS RELEASE
 Commission's lawyers prohibited from visiting political prisoners
During their hunger strike
 
The Palestinian Independent Commission for Citizens' Rights (PICCR) is following with great anxiety the matter of the political detainees held in the detention centres of the Palestinian National Authority (PNA), who have been protesting their long periods of detention without charges, without being presented before a judge, and who are deprived of the due process of law. On the human side, there have been dire consequences as well, in terms of loss of employment or their being prevented from completing their studies. Several among them have also suffered from untreated illnesses, and all of them have felt strongly the denial of rights to which they gave been subjected. As a result they have declared an open hunger strike.
From the time, one week ago, when the political detainees in Jneid military prison announced they were going on an open hunger strike, the Commission has been getting in constant touch with the Palestinian Authority at all levels, demanding that these cases be examined and that they be released immediately, particularly as we are approaching the 'aid al'adha' (Feast of the Sacrifice).

The Commission has likewise made various attempts to visit the hunger strikers so as to gain more information as to their present condition. Unfortunately, the administration of Jneid military prison, which is not trained or qualified to run a prison which includes civilians, has refused to co-operate with the Commission and has not allowed its lawyers or field researchers to visit these detainees, or to have any contacts with them whatsoever. It should be noted that, in contract to all other prison administrations of the Palestinian Authority, Jneid prison has to the present date, never shown any willingness to co-operate with the Commission.

We have sent a letter to General Hajj Ismael Jaber, Head of the National Security Forces in the West Bank, In which we demanded that the lawyers of our Commission be permitted to visit and talk to the prisoners. Despite our repeated daily contacts with his office, we were constantly given excuses and delays by way of answers to our requests.

The Palestinian Independent Commission for Citizens' Rights protests in the strongest terms the continued detention of these citizens without cause, and considers this a clear violation of the most elementary of human rights, contradicting international conventions and agreements as well as domestic legal norms. We likewise strongly protest the refusal of the office of General Hajj Ismael to grant the Commission's lawyers permission to visit the prisoners, and declare this to be unprecedented. We fear that the reason for this refusal is the decision to conceal the violations of prisoners' rights being perpetrated within the prison.

We therefore call upon the leadership of the Palestinian National Authority to adopt a clear and courageous stance, and put an end to the suffering of dozens of political prisoners and their families, who are entirely innocent of any crimes. We likewise demand that the PICCR's lawyers and representatives of all human rights organisations be granted immediate permission to visit the prisoners so as to find out what they are actually living through in the military prison.
