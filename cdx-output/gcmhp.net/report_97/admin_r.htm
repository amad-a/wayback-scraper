1996-1997 Report / Administration
 
Administration
In 1990, the GCMHP began work as a small operation intending to meet the mental health needs of the Gaza community. Today the permanent staff numbers 69 highly trained professionals. There are three clinics, three women’s centers, numerous outreach programs, a significantly expanding training program, new departments, and new ideas. By maintaining an open policy of democracy, transparency, and accountability in the administration of the Programme, the GCMHP helps to spread these ideals throughout Palestinian society.
 
The GCMHP is a non-governmental, non-profit organization. Its day to day operations are carried out by teams in the different centers, departments, and projects of the GCMHP. These operations are organized through coordination between the heads of these teams, Deputy Director Ahmad Abu Tawahina, and Director General Dr. Eyad El-Sarraj. A seven-member Board of Directors oversees the operations of the GCMHP and establishes overall policies. Chaired by Dr. El-Sarraj, this Board meets regularly to discuss the development and direction of the Programme. In addition, a twenty-two member Board of Advisors provides consultation and support for administration of the Programme.
While the GCMHP charges minimal fees to its clients, this is done to generate a feeling of involvement, to help the clients feel that they are receivservices in a relationship of exchange rather than as charity. The fees charged are not substantial, however, and patients that cannot afford the fees pay nothing. This means that the GCMHP depends very highly on the donations of international governmental and non-governmental organizations. Without the support of these international donors, the GCMHP could not continue providing its essential services to the Gaza community. In conducting and accounting for these financial transactions, the GCMHP maintains its strict policy of transparency and accountability.

The GCMHP continues to grow in order to meet the lasting and developing mental health needs of the Gaza community. While serving an ever-increasing number of patients in its clinics, the GCMHP reaches thousands of others every year through its community outreach and prevention programs. In 1998, the GCMHP plans to open three new departments and a fourth women’s center, to expand the Research Department and the different committees, to hold another international conference, and to continue preparations for the establishment of the Arab Institute for Community Mental Health. Reaffirming its commitment and its dedication to the people of Gaza, the GCMHP looks forward to a future of progress and service.
