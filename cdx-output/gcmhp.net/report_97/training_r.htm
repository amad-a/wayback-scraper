The philosophy of the Training and Education Department is to develop mental health workers who are concerned with mobilizing the social services in the community to assist individuals in feeling that they are members of the community and not merely members in the community. They are not merely providers of care but facilitators of care; thus, enabling people to solve their problems.
One of the major limitations to the provision of effective mental health care in Palestine has been the lack of adequately trained mental health professionals. Working to overcome this deficiency, the GCMHP has always considered its training programs an essential part of its mission.
 
GCMHP training activities began with an ambitious program that brought trainers from prestigious institutes around the world to enhance the knowledge of the GCMHP staff. Over time, the GCMHP also began training its staff as trainers so that they could teach others the theories and methods of community mental health. During the last two years, the Training Department has provided numerous training opportunities for the GCMHP staff: nine staff members participated in an intensive course in computer skills; ten staff members completed a course for ‘training of trainers;’ eight members of the staff attended a weekly course in psychotherapy at Tel Aviv University; ten staff members continued work on a five-year course in group psychotherapy taught by Norwegian group psychotherapist Dr. Freddy Reddy; and three staff members traveled to England for a ten-day course in forensic psychiatry. In addition, the Training Department continuously provides the staff with quality training in the form of guest trainers, workshops, seminars, study days, case conferences, and opportunities to participate in national and international conferences and workshops.
 
As the staff members gained expertise through training and experience, the GCMHP began to receive requests from other health institutions in Gaza to train their staff. In response, the Training Department began in 1993 to initiate short courses and programs to train health professionals in the principles of community mental health. Since that time, the Training Department has increasingly provided out-service training for doctors, nurses, teachers, and social workers in the Gaza community. In 1996 and 1997, twenty-four women undertook a four-month course in social worker assistance; twelve social workers, psychologists, nurses, and doctors participated in a one-year course in community mental health; fifteen nurses participated in a six-month course in community mental health nursing; thirty-seven kindergarten teachers participated in two three-month courses in child development and well-being; twelve women completed a six-month course in community women leadership; and twelve women completed a twelve-month course in community women leadership. The Training Department will continue designing and conducting training courses that produce qualified and competent mental health workers in Palestine.
The long-term goal of the Training Department is to establish the Arab Institute for Community Mental Health to assist in meeting the mental health needs of Palestine and the Arab world. Over the last two years, the Training Department has taken a significant step towards achieving that goal. In partnership with the Islamic University of Gaza and Oslo University, and with the participation of Edinburgh University-UK, Flinders University-Australia, Oslo University-Norway, University of California at San Francisco-USA, Tel Aviv University-Israel, Tunis University-Tunisia, and Utrecht University-the Netherlands, the GCMHP held international curriculum development workshops throughout 1996 in preparation for the 1997 establishment of the Post Graduate Diploma in Community Mental Health – a two-year course designed to help professionals develop theoretical and practical skills in the field of community mental health. This course is the only one of its kind in the Middle East – it represents important progress in the field of mental health training not only for Palestine, but for the entire region.
The Training Department is playing an increasingly important role in providing mental health care services to the Palestinian population in the Gaza Strip. The progress that the GCMHP has made so far towards actualizing the Arab Institute for Community Mental Health is exciting. By the year 2002, the GCMHP hopes to have its local trainers ready to begin teaching courses in the Institute.
