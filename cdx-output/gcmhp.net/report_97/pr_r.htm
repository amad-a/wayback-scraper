1996-1997 Report / Public Relations Department
 
Public Relations Department

The Public Relations Department at the GCMHP carries out the fundamental tasks of distributing information about the goals and activities of the GCMHP and maintaining a network of links with the Gaza community and the world. During 1996 and 1997, the Public Relations Department prepared brochures, conducted interviews, received guests, published the proceedings of an international conference, and planned, organized, and held the third GCMHP international conference.

The Department arranges interviews and tours of the Programme and facilitates visits to Gaza for a constant stream of international delegations, visiting professors, students, journalists, photographers, donors, and other guests. In November 1997 alone, the Public Relations Department received over 90 visitors.

In addition, the Department conducts outreach work. It plays a role in coordination between Palestinian NGOs. It also writes appeals and press releases for the GCMHP. Three times in 1996, the Public Relations Department took part in the campaign to secure the release of Programme Director Dr. Eyad El-Sarraj after his detention by Palestinian police. In 1998, the Department plans to begin publishing an Arabic newsletter – Amwaj (Waves) - for the local community.

One of the major activities of the Public Relations department is organizing international conferences. In 1996, the GCMHP compiled, edited, and published the papers delivered at its 1995 Second International Conference, “Palestinians in Transition: Rehabilitation and Community Development.” The Department then spent most of 1997 preparing for, holding, and following up on the Third International Conference, “Health and Human Rights,” held from 13-15 October 1997. The conference, which brought together over 400 scholars, activists, and professionals to discuss the importance and relationship of concepts such as mental health, human rights, and democracy, was considered a huge success.

During 1998, the Public Relations Department will publish the proceedings of the Third International Conference. In addition, it has agreed to coordinate with the International Refugee Advisory Panel (IRAP) to hold the Sixth IRAP Conference on Forced Migration in Gaza. Addressing issues pertaining to refugees around the world, this conference will take place in Gaza in December 1998.

The continued services of the GCMHP depend on maintaining a good image and keeping an ever-expanding list of friends and supporters informed about the numerous activities of the Programme. By building a strong network of support within the community and around the world, the Public Relations Department constitutes an essential function in sustaining the provision and development of mental health services in Gaza.
