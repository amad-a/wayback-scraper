Parenting Style and Children's Activity, Resources, and Psychological Well-Being in Traumatic Conditions" (Samir Qouta, Raija-Leena Punamaki, and Eyad El-Sarraj)
The relationships between traumatic experiences, parenting styles, children's political activity, resources and psychological adjustment were examined among 108 Palestinian boys and girls of 11-12 years of age. Results showed that children exposed to a high level of traumatic experiences perceive their mothers and fathers more disciplining, punishing and rejecting than children exposed to low level of trauma. Also, the more traumatic experiences children had, the more hostile they perceived their mother's parenting style. Boys perceived both parents more disciplining, punishing and rejecting, and their mothers more hostile than girls. Parenting had both mediating and moderating role in the relationship between traumatic experiences and children's adjustment. Exposure to traumatic experiences increased both directly and through poor parenting children's psychological adjustment problems. Good parenting moderated the negative impact of traumatic experiences on children's psychological adjustment: trauma significantly increased adjustment problems only among children whose parents were punishing, rejecting, hostile, and non-loving.
