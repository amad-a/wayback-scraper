A Seminar on
“The Tunisian Legislative Experience during the Transitional Period”

 
The Gaza Community Mental Health Programme (GCMHP) and the Democratic Forum organized a lecture on “The Tunisian Legislative Experience during the Transitional Period” in the meeting hall at GCMHP.

 
Husam El-Nounou, PR Coordinator, started the meeting by welcoming the guests and introducting the speakers: 

Dr. Ali El-Khaldi, Professor of Civil Law at Tunis University 

 
Dr. Mohammad El-Munsif El-Shaf’ie, Professor of International Law at Tunis University
 
 
 Dr. Mohammad El-Munsif El-Shaf’ie followed the introduction with a summary of modern Tunisian history and the occupation by France, and the steps it took to control the government in Tunis. He then talked about the Tunisian legislation in the period of transition, which was from June 1955 to March 1956, that was followed by the total independence of Tunis from the French occupation. Dr. El-Shaf’ie highlighted the fact that legislation during that period was concentrated in the areas pertaining to the Basic Law, and aimed to strengthen the Tunisian national status. 


 
 
 
Dr. Ali El-Khaldi talked about the changes that took place in the different laws in the transitional period with reference to the general and criminal laws. In addition, he explained the changes that took place in the Tunisian judicial system before, during, and after the French occupation.

Following the talks, the floor was opened for discussion. The speakers and participants that included local judges, lawyers, and intellectuals discussed the differences and similarities between the Tunisian and Palestinian legislative experience in the transitional period. In addition, they discussed the legislative process and laws in the two countries.

During their stay in Gaza, the Tunisian guests visited the Palestinian Legislative Council and met members of the Legal Committee and the Human Rights and Monitoring Committee. They also met the staff of the Legislation Department at the Ministry of Justice.

On 22 March, the delegation headed to the West Bank to hold a seminar on the same topic in “Mowatin Center” in Ramallah.
