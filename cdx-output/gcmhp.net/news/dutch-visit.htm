Dutch Minister for International Cooperation visits
the Gaza Community Mental Health Programme

 

His Excellency Mr. Jan Van Pronk, the Dutch Minister for International Cooperation, visited the Gaza Community Mental Health Programme (GCMHP) on 10 March 1998. A delegation from the Ministry of International Cooperation and the Representative Office of the Netherlands accompanied Minister Pronk on this visit in which he met Dr. Eyad El-Sarraj, the Director of GCMHP and the Commissioner-General of the Palestinian Independent Commission for Citizens’ Rights, and the GCMHP departments heads.
 
 

Dr. El-Sarraj welcomed Minister Pronk emphasizing that this special visit is an indication of the commitment of the Government of the Netherlands towards supporting peace and justice in Palestine. He then gave the guests a detailed description of the living conditions of the Palestinian people and the human rights situation in Palestine. Dr. El-Sarraj pointed out that, during the past year, there have been tangible improvements in the Palestinian Authority’s human rights record. Nonetheless, he made it clear that there still remain human rights violations such as using torture, detention without trial, and violating judicial rulings perpetrated by the security apparatus.

Moreover, Dr. El-Sarraj gave the visiting delegation an overview of the possible scenarios of the peace process, stressing the fact that, regardless of these scenarios, building the democratic process in Palestine is a long and difficult task, which is nonetheless vital. Dr. El-Sarraj expressed his optimism in achieving this goal with the efforts of the Palestinian democratic forces and international backing.

Dr. El-Sarraj also briefed the guests on the need for local and international backing to enhance the role of the Palestinian Legislative Council in improving the human rights situation and democracy in Palestine. He also emphasized the importance of the work of the different branches of the civil police, and the need for continuous technical and professional support from the Dutch Government in this regard. Dr. El-Sarraj also noted the need to ensure the freedom of the press in the process of democratic building in Palestine.

Following the description of the situation in Palestine, Dr. El-Sarraj explained the different activities of GCMHP and the services it provides to the community in the Gaza Strip. He gave special notice to the post-graduate diploma in community mental health that started in January 1997 as the first of its kind in the Middle East, and emphasizes mental health and human rights. Dr. El-Sarraj pointed out that there are efforts to open channels with other Arab countries that could benefit from the Palestinian experience in community mental health. Finally, Dr. El-Sarraj expressed the desire of the Palestinian people in cooperating with Europe, and particularly the Netherlands, in preserving their rights; and in expanding cultural and educational cooperation between them.

Minister Pronk informed the hosts that the Netherlands is interested in developing its relations with the Palestinians, noting that this is the second visit he makes to GCMHP.

It is duly noted that the Government of the Netherlands gives financial and technical support to certain projects within GCMHP, notably the “Rehabilitation of Ex-political Prisoners Project” and the “Training and Education Department”.
