Stop Torture Omnibus Program (STOP) visits GCMHP
 
On 27, 28 June, the Stop Torture Omnibus Program (STOP) visited Gaza as part of its three-year world tour. Mr. Niels Zeeberg, Head of the Program and former Project Coordinator at IRCT, explained that the main purpose of their visit is to “call upon the Israeli State and the Palestinian Authority to eradicate torture in their respective countries”. The visiting group participated in a workshop on “Prevention of Torture” on 27 June on the UN Day for the support of victims of torture, and held a press conference at GCMHP headquarters in Gaza on 28 June.
 
Mr. Zeeberg also called upon Palestinians to pursue Israel in International courts for its systematic policy of torture sanctioned by the highest judicial authority, Israeli High Court. In addition, STOP appealed to the Palestinian people, victims of torture and violence for decades, to stop using all forms of violence and torture in order to preserve their strong ties with the supporters of their just cause worldwide.
 
STOP started one year ago and has already visited 33 countries. The Program is scheduled to run for two more years in which they will visit 116 centers for rehabilitating torture survivors in more than 100 countries.
