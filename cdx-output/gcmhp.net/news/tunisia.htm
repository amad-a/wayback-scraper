Kholoud Ben Mohamed 
&
Monia Lahlaoui
 
 
 
Monia Lahlaoui

Kholoud Ben Mohamed 
Faculty of Social Science & Humanities
Tunis University
 
 
 
 
 
My visit to Gaza was, in a way, special. It 
introduced me to a community mental health program that offers services to all sectors of a 
population that has been suffering the effects of war and occupation for generations. In addition, I got acquainted with the situation in this country and witnessed the extent of the problems facing the people. 
In the Gaza atmosphere, I was impressed by the activities of GCMHP, especially the Women’s Empowerment Project that conducts public awareness and rehabilitation activities for Gaza’s women in order to give them their due share in the community building process in this country. 
I thank the staff of the Programme introducing me the different activities they carry out. 
 
Kholoud Ben Mohamed 
First of all, I would like to thank the Programme for giving me this opportunity to visit Palestine. My stay has given me a clear picture of the suffering of most of the people, especially in the impoverished refugee camps. Living in Tunis and having seen Palestinians before, I had never imagined the magnitude of the Palestinian dilemma until now. 
I salute GCMHP for its efforts in alleviating the suffering of the people through the different activities. I single out the public awareness and 
community work aspects that are necessary in this respect.
