Dr. Mustafa El-Masri's Visit to Nepal
(2-18 March 1998)



The visit took place in the context of the project with TPO ( Transcultural Psychological Organization-Holland). There were many aims for the visit:
  • To present the initial results of the Epidemiological Study conducted in Gaza in collaboration with TPO.
  • To participate in designing another multi-site study proposal on Psycho-social interventions, in which GCMHP will participate.
  • To design a book on the TPO projects worldwide. Dr. Elmasri was chosen to be the first author on the Gaza project. The book is expected to be ready in final form for printing next year.
Dr. Joop De Jong, director of TPO-Holland, chaired the meeting. He is a Professor in the Free University of Amsterdam, and is well known for his work in transcultural Psychiatry. He worked as a psychiatrist in different countries in the third world, and initiated community mental health projects.
Dr. Robert Giel, also a well known psychiatrist advised and commented on the interventions. He is very well known in Gaza since the early days of GCMHP in 1990, and has assisted in training the GCMHP professionals.
The participants were project directors and administrators of the TPO projects in Nepal, Uganda, Ethiopia, Congo, Cambodia, and two invited mental health professionals from Algeria, who were interested in starting a Mental health project in their country. Project directors presented the latest developments in their countries and projects. A general direction towards more advanced and standardized intervention programs was evident from the presentations.
In Nepal, Dr. El Masri presented the latest statistics on the work in GCMHP, including the work done in the areas of community and clinical interventions, children, and rehabilitation. He also presented a lecture on the rational of outreach preventive interventions in the area of Community Mental Health. In the context of the meeting, the standard clinical procedures and documentation of the GCMHP were presented for the other projects. The audience found the presentations very informative and requested further detailed information on the Programme activities. Many of the participants found the work in GCMHP as a model to be followed, and expressed their wish to visit the Programme.
