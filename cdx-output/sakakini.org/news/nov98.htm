Other News of Palestinian Culture

November, 1998:
 
 
 

Poster of the Carmen-Palestine production

 
 
 

* Special Events:

-The second annual Palestine Awards for the arts and humanities were announced this month by the head of the awards committee, poet Mahmoud Darwish. The committee was composed also of poet Fadwa Tuqan, and a number of last year’s winners. The Humanities Award went to poet/translator Dr. Salma Khadra Jayyusi for her pioneering efforts at translating and publishing Arabic & Palestinian literature. The special Jerusalem Award went to Dr. Walid Khalidi, founder and director of the Institute for Palestine Studies, & author of notably “All that Remains”, documenting Palestinian villages demolished during the Nakba, and “Before their Diaspora”, a visual history of Palestine & the Palestinians from 1876 to 1948.

The poetry award went to Ahmad Dahbour, music went to young classical pianist Salim Abboud, visual arts went to Slimane Mansour, the novel award went exceptionally to Lebanese author Elias Khoury for his novel on the Nakba Bab el-Shams (Gate of the Sun),  the press writing award went for the second year in a row to a writer from the Al-Ayyam daily: columnist and former magazine editor Hassan el Batal, cinema to director Rachid Masharawi, theater to actor/director George Ibrahim of the Jerusalem Qassaba Theater, and the folklore award went to writer/researcher Dr. Abdellatif Barghouti. The awards will be presented in a special ceremony chaired by President Arafat at Najah’s Nablus university in December.
 

-The Spanish consulate in Jerusalem organized a series of activities in cooperation with local cultural institutions, as part of Spanish culture week, in El-Bireh, Nablus, & Jerusalem. The activities focused on flamenco classes for children, bilingual readings of Lorca poems by actress Iman ‘Aoun,  lectures on the Prado museum, and an exhibit of reproductions of Prado famous paintings.
 
 

* Visual Arts:

- Visual artist Sharif Waked held his new  exhibit at the Ramallah Ziryab Café & gallery. The works are a visual reflection on the Nakba.

- Ceramist Dina Ghazal held her second solo  exhibit at the Ramallah Matall gallery. Her works consist mainly of Islamic art-style potteries and ceramic murals.

- Young painter Abdel-Hadi Yaïsh held his second exhibit of symbolist paintings at Ramallah’s French cultural center.
 

* Film:

- The Ramallah Goethe Institute organized special screenings of Fritz Lang's Metropolis, accompanied by a 4-member Jazz ensemble: The Ensemble Metropolis Project. The film was shown in El-Bireh and Gaza City.

-The French Cultural centers network screened a number of new French films at their centers in Nablus, Ramallah, Jerusalem Gaza, and  Bethlehem
 

* Literature:

- Issue # 57 of the literary quarterly edited out of the Sakakini by poet Mahmoud Darwish is out. It features a comprehensive article by critic Faycal Darraj on Khalil Sakakini, a new poem by Izzedine Manacerah, articles on the fall of Safad & Deir Yassin by Mohammed Barout &  Dr. Salma K. Jayyusi, a study by Fawwaz Trabulsi on Palestine in Fayruz's music, an article on Marwan Qassab Bashi's works, etc.. In addition to book reviews & regular features.
 

* Music:

- The French cultural centers network of Palestine organized a series of exceptional concerts of the opera Carmen in Gaza City, Jerusalem and Birzeit. The recitals featured 4 French opera singers (among them Nathalie Espalier, Caroline Dumas & Christian Lara), performing excerpts from the opera accompanied by two local children’s choirs (the Sindbad Children’s choir of Gaza City & the National Conservatory’s children choir), and by the -adult- Jerusalem Choir. The singers were accompanied by two pianists, & the show had a Palestinian narrator: Actor/director George Ibrahim, who told the story from a children's point of view. The shows were a great success and drew over-capacity crowds.

- Lebanese-Swiss classic guitarist Jad Ezzkoul gave a series of recitals in Bethlehem, El-Bireh and Jerusalem of Villa Lobos, Piazzola, etc..

- The Ramallah Goethe Institute organized a Jerusalem recital by a German trio composed of French Horn player Radovan Vklatkovic, pianist  Nina Tichman , and  violinist Ida Bieler. The trio performed a program  of Beethoven, Brahms, etc..
