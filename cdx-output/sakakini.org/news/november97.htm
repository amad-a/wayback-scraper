Other News of Palestinian Culture

November, 1997:


The father of critic Fakhri Saleh receiving his son’s award from President Arafat & Culture minister Yasser Abed Rabbo. Saleh , who lives in Jordan, was denied entry into Palestine by the Israelis


The most important news of this month, was the award of the first annual Palestine Prizes. The awards were announced in October, and were presented by President Arafat to the winners at Najah University in Nablus. For more information, see the October News Section.

Theatre:
Representations of the Palestinian adaptation of Death & the Maiden, by the Qassaba Theater Company, continued in Jerusalem.

Literature:
- The second annual Palestinian book fair was held in Bethlehem. The fair was attended by a great number of prestigious Arab publishing houses. The only foreign country invited to participate was France. A number of activities were held on the margins of the fair, such as lectures, roundtables, readings, etc.. Poet Mureed Barghouti attended the fair, & gave a reading there, and in El Bireh.

- Poet Ali El Khalil published a new collection of essays, under the title of "Al Nass al Mouareb", (Texts Ajar). The collection was published by Hebron’s Dar El Moustaqbal, and is a reflection on representations of the Palestinian Question in Arab intellectual discourse.
