Other News of Palestinian Culture

October, 1998:


Poster of the third annual Palestine book fair. Ibrahim El-Muzayyen design.

* Visual Arts:

- Visual artist Nisreen Sawalmah held her 1st  exhibit at the Ramallah Matall gallery.

- Visual artist Ashraf Fawakhry held an exhibit of his new works “ I am a Donkey” at Jerusalem's Wasiti gallery, his commentary on the status of Palestinian-Israelis in Israel. The show featured prints of a donkey reproduced on differing backgrounds.

- French photographer Jean-Pierre Gilson held an exhibit of night views of the French French northern port cities of Dunkerque and Le Havre, titled Rivages Noirs (black shores) at Gaza’s French cultural center.
 

* Theater:

- Theater Day Productions premiered at Jerusalem’s Qassaba theater their production of the monodrama “Walideh” starring Raeda Ghazaleh, directed by Jan Willems, written by Jackie Lubeck. The play tells the story of a girl who has lot all memory ..but..”I am repairing my life..I am preparing my life”.

- The German classic “Nathan the Wise” by Gotthold Lessing was performed by the Heillborn theater company in El-Bireh’s Friends School and at the Najah University in Nablus, to unanimous and numerous negative reviews.

* Film:

- Director Subhi Zubeidi premiered  his second documentray “Women in the Sun”, a 57’ film on the situation of Palestinian women, and the women’s movement post-Oslo.

- The Matall Gallery screened a number of new Palestinian short subjects, among them Rachid Mashhrawi’s Tawattur (tension) a documentray on the present situation in Palestine, and Mohammed Bakri’s directorial debut 1948, featuring notably interviews with former Israeli soldiers who killed and deported Palestinians during the Nakba.

-The French Cultural centers network screened a number of new French films at their centers in Nablus, Ramallah, Jerusalem Gaza, and  Bethlehem
 

* Literature:

- The third annual Palestine book fair was held in Gaza, attracting  over 400,000 visitors, and displaying over 60,000 titles, from publishers in Morocco, Libya, Egypt, Syria, Lebanon, Iraq, Jordan ,France, and the Gulf.
 

* Music:

French Jazz cellist performed in Jerusalem, Nablus, Ramallah and Gaza a number of excerpts of French classical pieces in a jazz mode; accompanied by a string, wind and drums quartet.
