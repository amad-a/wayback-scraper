Jamal Badran


His work:
Badran was the most prominent Palestinian artist working in Islamic art. His reputation grew after he was commissioned by the Islamic Waqf to renovate the burned pulpit (Minbar) of the Aqsa mosque, destroyed by an Israeli arsonist in 1969. His major lifework was his work at the Aqsa and Dome of the Rock mosques in Jerusalem, which included design of ceramics, mosaics, wood and plaster carvings. Badran also worked for two years decorating the Jordan University mosque. He was also famous for the individual pieces he created, from illumination, to leather, to lampshades, and glass.
