A Brief History 


1918   Founded as "An-Najah Nablusi School".
1941   Developed to be "An-Najah College".
1963   Upgraded to a two year community college.
1977   Became "An-Najah National Uni-versity. "Two faculties were 
established: Science and Arts.
       Joined the Association of Arab Universities as a full member.
1978   Economics & Administrative Sciences faculty established.
1981   Joined the International Union of Universities.
1982   Construction of Auditoriums, library,and student center complex 
started.
1983   Constructions stopped by military order.
1985   New Departments established: 
       Agricultural Sciences, Fine Arts, Medical Technology.
       In the same year graduate studies (M.A) in Chemistry, Islamic Studies
       and Education started.
1986   Permission given to continue the construction of the 
Administration building.
1988   Campus is declared (by Israeli Authorities) a closed military area.
1991   Reopened by military order.
1992   July 14-17, the University  under military siege by Israeli soldiers.
1993   Agricultural Sciences, Fine Arts, and Islamic Studies developed to 
Faculty
       of Agriculture, Faculty of Fine Arts,and Faculty of Shari'a 
(Islamic Law)
1994   New faculty and centers established:
       Faculty of Pharmacy, Academic Program for the Study of Involuntary
       Migration, Water and Environmental Studies Center, Center for Studies
       Consultation & Technical Services, Public Administration 
Center.

Other Institutes and Centers

Scientific Research And Graduate Studies

       In addition to training graduate studies,the Deanship publishes
An-Najah Journal of Research in both Natural Sciences and Humanities.

Documentation, Manuscript & Publication Center:

       The Center was established in 1982. It is concerned with 
preserving 
the culture and heritage of the Palestinian nation.The Center also publishes
seminar proceedings, academic studies, and old Arabic manuscripts.

Rural Research Center

       The Center was established in 1981 to act as a link between the 
University and the local community. The Center concentrates on:
       * Assessing the economics and technical facilities of 
developmental projects.
       * Analyzing various agricultural problems and suggesting solutions.
       * Collecting statistical data related to agriculture and the 
economy and
         making them available to students and researchers.

Academic Programme For The Study Of Involuntary Migration:
       
	Established in 1994, the program has three main objectives: research
activities, teaching courses on forced migration and collecting documents.
       The introduction of a multi-disciplinary research program on 
Involuntary 
Migration is an opportunity to stimulate the overall intellectuaL growth 
of the students. 

Water And Environmental Studies Center:

       The main aim of the Center is providing communal services in the 
field of water and environment and in the following forms:
       * Interactive programs to determine utilities needs (urban and rural),
         reviewing their master plans and projects, training their staff, and
         helping them in monitoring water and environmental quality 
within their
         service area. 
       * Conducting special studies concerning water and environmental issues
         affecting peoples health and welfare as the extent of lead pollution
         in humans, the effect of raw waste water irrigation on water, soil,
         and plants, the role of religion in water and 
environmental,protection 
         and conservation, and others.
       * Design and conduct a public awareness programs concerning water and
         environmental issues and elements (resources, activities, and 
their 
         impacts and effects on the community) to get peoples support, 
         cooperation, and participation in communal projects and plans.

COMPUTER CENTER

       Established in 1992, the center intends to copmutarize all University
administrative departments through all stages: System analysis and design,
Programming and testing, training staff for system implementation, 
computer 
assistance for University researchers. As well as helping community 
through 
computerized services for institutions and through continuous educational 
courses.

Center Studies, Consultancies, & Technical Services 

       The center aims at accomplishing the University goals in serving the
Arab Society and the Palestinian society in particular. This will be 
achieved 
through the following services.
       * Offering studies, consultancy and technical services for 
Institutions
         in the public and private sectors inside and outside Palestine. 
It 
         will supervise the organization of these services and their 
management.
         It will also follow-up the implementation of these services in
         return for charges.
       * Developing the University potentials and abilities in offering these
         services, as well as providing moral and material incentives for
         University employees and encouraging them to contribute to Arab and
         palestinian Societies' development plans.
       * Sponsoring and holding seminars,conferences, symposia and training
         courses with the aim of developing expertise, abilities, scientific
         & professional skills of participants to enable them to contribute
         at a time the palestinian society is passing through a 
construction stage.

Public Administration Institute:
       Established in 1994, the Institute aims at implementing the following:
       * Fostering the concepts of modern administration principles to 
         transfer it to different Palestinians institutions.
       * Upgrading and improving performance of government and private 
sector.
       * Organizing symposia and conferences in different areas of 
         administration areas such as finance, marketing and production.
       * Making assessment needs to develop different training courses 
for 
         different administration level in all institutions.
       * Conducting research and studies in different administration areas
         for the purpose of developing on the job performance for local 
         institutions

Services, Facilities, Admissions and Activities

Employees Union

       An-elected body representing employees and staff members, the 
Employees
Union defends the legal rights and interests of all employees; it works to
raise their academic levels; it also provides other services: health 
insurance,
a nursery and a kindergarten for the employees' children.

Students Activities: Student Union

       Elected annually, it represents and serves the cultural and social
needs of the student body. They have cooperation agreements with other 
student 
unions abroad. An-Najah students participate in local and national events.
They engage in voluntarily activities for their university and for the 
community as well.

Scholarships:

       Financial aid, partial or total, is offered to needy and 
meritorious 
students. Special cases, including family problems or long periods of 
political 
imprisonment, also receive appropriate preferential treatment. Scholarships
may include tuition waivers, financial help, or pocket allowances.

Community Service

       Every student  must carry out at least 32 credit hours of voluntary
work in service to the university or the Palestinian community in the 
Occupied Territories.


Library

       An-Najah University Library houses 130,000 volumes. 80,000 in English
and 50,000 in Arabic. It subscribes to 150 periodicals in
both languages. It offers research guidance, reference assistance, and inter-
library loans.

Admissions:

       Acceptance is competitive and is based on merit with provisions made
for special hardship cases. Students with high scores in the "Tawjihi", 
or 
the "General Secondary Exam" may apply. The Admissions Office screens the
applications and notifies students with acceptable scores to register for 
the following semester.
Campus Buildings & Lands
Current Campus: 
       The University has a campus of 30 dunums. The University buildings 
are 
as follows: Faculties' Buildings, The Library, The University 
Administration, 
The Student Center (under construction), The Auditoriums (under 
construction),
& New Library Building (under construction)

Future Campus:

       The University also has 120 dunums which will be stated for 120 
dunums, 
to provide Engineering and Science Faculties. To date no construction has 
begun due to the refusal of the Israeli authorities to issue a building 
permit, (submitted 1980). On April 22, 1985, the Israeli Military 
Authorities 
declared the land a military zone, and confiscated it for military purposes.
