Decisions of the Arab Information
Ministers' Council

Session 32
Cairo
16-17 June, 1999

First item:

  • Supporting Jerusalem and protecting its Arab character.

    • The Council of Arab Information Ministers;

    • After hearing the report presented by the head of the State of Palestine delegation,
    • Reviewing its decisions in this respect.
    • Studying the report of the second meeting of the media team authorized to lay out an Arab plan to cover the issue of Jerusalem,
    • And observing the recommendations of the Permanent Committee,

    decides on the following:

  1. Endorsing the recommendations issued by the Permanent Committee in its 63 meeting and working on translating these recommendations virtually.

  2. Launching an Arab media campaign calling to terminate all Jewish settlement projects in Arab Jerusalem, particularly at its eastern entrance, Jabal Abu Ghneim and Ras el-Amud.
  3. Supporting and highlighting the EU's position calling to stop the Israeli settlement activity, considering this activity illegal and Arab Jerusalem as an occupied city.

  4. Calling on Arab media means to concentrate on rejecting the US Congress decision calling to move the American Embassy from Tel-Aviv to Jerusalem, on the basis of the illegality of this decision and its contradiction with the International Legitimacy resolutions and the Peace Agreements.

  5. Approving the Arabic list of names related to the city of Jerusalem presented by the Palestinian delegation and not to use the Israeli names.

  6. Asserting the position of the previous Arab Information Ministers' Council which supports the initiative of the United Arab Emirates, calling to organize "Jerusalem Day", allocated to support Jerusalem and its resistance against the most vicious settlement attacks, and the formation of an Arab media committee to make this function come to a success, and urging the Arab satellite TV stations to contribute in this day.

The Second Item:

  • Supporting the Palestinian Intifada and resistance against the Israeli colonial occupation in Palestine, the Golan Heights and South Lebanon.

    • The Council of Arab Information Ministers;

    • After hearing the report presented by the head of the state of Palestinian delegation on the subject,
    • and observing the recommendation of the Arab media permanent committee,

    decides the following:

  1. Confirming the previous decisions and recommendations.

  2. Concentrating Arab media means on the need to end the Israeli occupation and Jewish settlements expansion in Palestine and the rest of the occupied territories, raise the slogan "No Peace With Settlements" and to intensify Arab and international pressures on the Israeli authorities to honor and implement the International Resolutions and Laws, and the signed agreements.

  3. Adhering to the principle "Land For Peace" which constitutes the core of the UN Secretary Council Resolutions 242 and 338, aiming at achieving the full Israeli withdrawal from the occupied territories including Arab Jerusalem, from the Syrian Golan Heights and from South Lebanon according to Security Council's Resolution 425.

  4. Preparing the Arab and International Public Opinion for materializing the right of the Palestinian people for self-determination, on top of which the establishment of its Independent State on its land during 1999.

  5. Enhancing the role of the International Criminal Tribune to look into war crimes and presenting the necessary recommendations regarding the Israeli occupation's war crimes in Palestine, the Golan Heights and Lebanon, and follow up its procedures in this respect.

  6. Launching a media campaign against using internationally banned methods with Arab and Palestinian prisoners, and demanding an immediate release of all political prisoners, particularly those who suffer from chronic diseases.

The Third Item:

  • Supporting the cultural and media work in the occupied Arab territories and the PNA's territories.

    • The Arab Information Ministers Council,

    • After hearing the report presented by the head of the Palestinian delegation on the mater,
    • and reviewing the recommendation of the Permanent Committee,

    decides the following:

    1. Asserting all the previous decisions and recommendations in this respect, particularly resolutions 75, issued by the Arab Information Ministers' Council in its 30th Session, regarding laying out a plan for establishing a Journalistic and Media Training Institute in Palestine.

    2. Urging the member states to train Palestinian media people inside and outside Palestine through direct contacts between the Palestinian government and governments of the member states.

    3. Praising the initiative of Saudi Arabia Kingdom regarding its readiness to send media specialists to Palestine to organize training courses and receive Palestinian media people in the Kingdom to train them.

    4. Calling on Arab media means to contribute in covering celebrations of Bethlehem 2000, and participate in its cultural and artistic activities, to confirm the spiritual and cultural importance of Palestine and confront the Israeli attempts aimed at effacing the cultural and religious sights.

Palestine Ministry of Information
June 23,1999
