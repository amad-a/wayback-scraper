Women in Politics
 
   Palestinian women played a major role in the resistance against the Israeli occupation on both a political and non-political level.  Today Palestinian women are still well organized in women's committees, but they rarely take part in the political-decision making process.  In fact, there remains a gap between the political leadership and the women's committees.  Furthermore, the women's committees are facing a lack of support from their constituency.  Many women have left these organizations leading to a decrease in their activities and the beginning of a collapse of some committees. 

   Panorama believes that the presence of women in the political decision-making process in all levels of society 1) is crucial to the development of a democratic society, and 2) that it lays the foundation for a more widespread assertion of women's rights in Palestinian society. 

  Therefore, since June 1996 Panorama, in cooperation with the Women's Studies Center at Birzeit University, has been conducting a study on the Palestinian Women's Committees. The aim of this study is to understand the reasons for the recent demobilization of these committees.  The study is based  on a comprehensive questionnaire conducted on a sample of 270 respondents living in the West Bank.  These questionnaires have been filled out by members and former members of five Women's Committees: 

  • The Union of Palestinian Women Committee
  • The Union of Women Social Work Committee
  • The Union of Palestinian Working Women Committee
  • The Women Action Association
  • The Union of Women's Action Committee
   Panorama organized a regional workshop in Ramallah using the facilities of the Women's Affairs Technical Committee on January 25, 1997 in order to present the preliminary findings of the questionnaires. 
 
   A summary of the findings was presented and important discussions took place on issues such as 
  • the impact of the Women's Committee on promoting women's action
  • the relations between the Women's Committees and the political parties they are linked to
  • the future of the Women's Committee
   In order to reach out to all the Palestinian regions, Panorama will organize other workshops and a more detailed report will be presented at the end of the project.   A national conference will be held at Birzeit University in order to foster a national debate on this issue and to raise further awareness on this topic.
