Pluralism in Action
 
     About fifty percent of Palestinians are under the age of nineteen, and many of them are at school, college or university.  It is Panorama's belief that disseminating democratic principles through education, and by extension two of the most central and fundamental Palestinian institutions--  family and the school, is the best way of working towards a democratic future for our society.  The central aim of the Pluralism in Action Project is to change the individual oriented and monopoly-like decision making processes in these institutions. 

    Since 1995, Panorama, with the generous support of the National Endowment Fund for Democracy, has conducted workshops throughout the West Bank and Gaza Strip.  During these workshops, youth between the ages of 17-22 participate in activities that encourage them to think about issues of accountability, transparency, democracy, rule of law, citizenship, and respect for human rights.  Some of these activities involve serious discussions, but most involve role plays where the youth learn experientially the difference between governance systems that are inclusive and representative and those that are not.  From 1995-7, approximately 7,000 Palestinian youth participated in these workshops.  This past July, Panorama completed the third phase of its Pluralism in Action Project. 

    The results were once again encouraging.  From October 1997 through to July 1998 Panorama made over 168 workshops that reached just under 6,000 people.  In addition, Panorama conducted many of these workshops in rural areas that were previously not reached.  Another important accomplishment of the Third Phase was the initiation of ‘vertical courses’, more in depth and specialized courses that simultaneously help Panorama to identify new trainers.  These courses were held in Tulkarm and Jerusalem. 

   Based on the success of these vertical courses, Panorama in the next phase of Pluralism in Action, will increase its focus on offering them.  Two sets of these courses will be held in the West Bank, and a third will be held in the Jerusalem Area.  In addition, Panorama will continue to offer workshops to schools and clubs throughout the West Bank and Gaza with the assistance of trainers trained in the Third phase
