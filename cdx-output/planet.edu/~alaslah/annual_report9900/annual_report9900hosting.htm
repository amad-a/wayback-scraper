Annual Report 1999 - 2000

Hosting Groups

"Establishing lasting peace is the work of education; all politics can do is keep us out of war" (Maria Montessori)

Wi'am recognizes that there are no borders to conflicts and no borders to peace. The doors of the Center are open for visitors to come and learn about the work of Wi'am and the conditions Palestinians live under. Through learning at the Center they are able to understand a little better about the people and issues than listening or reading the news. Many visiting groups from abroad ask to meet with the staff of Wi'am. Over the last year, the Wi'am staff met with over 20 medium to large groups, and over 10 small delegations. Talking with the groups is an important project for the Center, to be able to share experiences and tell family stories.

Along with opening our doors to share, Wi'am has also been involved with setting up group itineraries when asked. Because of Wi'am's work in the community, we have been able to develop many contacts in the local community as well as networks with other organizations throughout Palestine. This allows the staff to set meetings and trips for groups who are interested in learning more about the Palestinian situation. Throughout the year Wi'am hosted two groups in this fashion.
