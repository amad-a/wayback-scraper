Anti-Incitement project
Although Wi'am mainly focuses on Palestinians in the Bethlehem area, we have also participated and partnered with Israeli groups who try to promote peace and understanding through dialogue and interaction. Our newest project is an Interfaith project called "Track II & III Anti-Incitement through violence prevention by Emerging Leadership". For this project, we have partnered with the Catholic Relief Services and the Harry S. Truman Institute for Peace (Hebrew University).

Reflection written by Lucy Thaleigh, Women's Coordinator, about her experience at EMU.

I became more aware and familiar with the basic literature available in the field of conflict resolution, understanding conflict, definitions, and dynamics of communication and the negotiation process. The course I took are used to promote accurate international awareness regarding the identity, presence and witness, while encouraging groups from around the world to work for justice, comprehensive, and enduring peace, informed by truth and empowered by prayer and action.

People to People Workshop
During the ongoing dialogue between Israelis and Palestinians, it has been decided to hold joint workshops. In general, these workshops are designated for Israelis who place themselves in the center of the political spectrum, especially new immigrants, moderate religious groups, and residents of development towns and neighborhoods with certain 


leadership abilities or potential. The Palestinians are from different domains (students and working youngsters), so as to discuss the Israeli - Palestinian conflict and dialogue on one hand, and on the other, to promote maximum interchange, interaction, peace values and mutual understanding between the participants. Concentration is placed on final status issues: Jerusalem, refugees, and settlements.

Wi'am has also conducted in-service training for those interested in Sulha. In-service training is provided so individuals can have a hands-on, practical learning experience without feeling as though they have been thrown into the lion's den all alone. Individuals are asked to be a part of Sulha by observing what is said and done. After the Sulha meeting concludes, Wi'am staff sits down with the trainees to evaluate the Sulha process. 


Conflict Resolution Training Seminars

Goals:

  • Encouraging police members to take their role in building a democratic and civic community seriously, based on respecting the human beings as well as enhancing the rule of law.
  • Providing police members with conflict resolution skills, in order to let them think more objectively with potential conflicts and how to solve them professionally.
  • Raising their awareness by educating them to be the voice of justice by addressing the injustice within the community.
  • Providing the police members with a safe environment to share their experience and approaches in dealing with the masses.
  • Building up Palestinian civil society and to move on with the social transformation.

 

Capacity Building in Institutional Development Seminars

Goals:

  • Equipping the Palestinians with skills and techniques to cope with challenges in cross-cultural activities.
  • Providing the NGOs with knowledge how to trigger their building up capacities in different fields.
  • Helping individuals to be aware of sorting out conflicts emerging in the intra-society and inter-society especially in this season of conflict.
  • Realizing the effectiveness and enhancing the efficiency of our work in carrying out various activities in crystallizing the social transformation.
