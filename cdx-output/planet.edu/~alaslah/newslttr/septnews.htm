  • Conflict Resolution:

    The continuation of the hot Summer, frustrating economic times, and the various Jewish holidays have led to a busy month for the staff in addressing conflicts. In reflecting back over the month's effort, it is difficult to see that much has been accomplished, because upon resolving one case- another walks through the door. Current conditions have festered discouraging attitudes that lead to people easily taking out their frustrations and rage on neighbors and families. In a small community as Bethlehem, such attitudes and emotions spread quickly. Over the last month, the Center has successfully worked through the following conflicts:

  • Preserving Bethlehem

    Over the last couple of weeks Wi'am has joined efforts with other organizations and concerned individuals to look at the future of Bethlehem. Most of the focus in the past about Bethlehem has dealt with the new Millennium and the possible onslaught of tourists. Our concern is about the future health of Bethlehem and Bethlehemites well after the year 2000, in light of the newly discovered plans by Israel to erect a more permanent checkpoint to Bethlehem, that would separate Palestinians from visiting tourists and Jews.

    Such an implementation would for all purposes: close off Bethlehem from Jerusalem (killing the lifeline of Bethlehem), prevent many workers passage to their jobs, allow Israel to take more land and place more facts on the ground, and create an apartheid system of separation in the West Bank.

    As we move forward into the next couple of months, we look to encouraging times and positive signs that will support our work of improving the relationships in this community during these difficult days.


    Wi'am ('Cordial Relationships' - in Arabic) began its formal service to the local community between 1994 and 1995.  The main focus is resolving conflicts by using the traditional Arab method - Sulha- integrated with Western techniques.  Along with Sulha, Wi'am works to prevent conflicts through education of such topics of: Human Rights, Democracy, Media, Gender Issues.

    The times in Bethlehem remain to be unstable as the occupation continues.  Many of the internal conflicts stem from the checkpoint and the inability to move freely back and forth to Jerusalem.  For this reason, Wi'am also supports individual's and family needs.  Throughout the year we try to raise money to help with building projects, sending children to school, helping with needed operations, finding jobs for people, offering trips for the youth, and providing gifts for children during the Christmas time. (We welcome any support that others are able to offer in these matters.)

    As a grass-roots organization, Wi'am strives alongside other democratic forces present in the larger Bethlehem community to build a democratic and just society.  The Center aims to improve the quality of relationships by: addressing injustices rather than avenging them; dignifying persons on both sides of the conflict; promoting human rights and advocating for peace among all people.
