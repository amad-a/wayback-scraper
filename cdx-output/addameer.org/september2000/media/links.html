Other Media Sources

Links take you to Middle East news sections of local, commercial, and alternative websites. For websites similar to this one but from a different time period, please see the general links section on this site.


PALESTINIAN SOURCES (English)

  • Palestinian Authority - hourly updates in English and Arabic are available.

  • Arab Media Internet Network - AMIN - Offers the main news stories, plus an archive full of older articles and stories which are all available online, organised into sections such as "Eye on Jerusalem," "Eye on Amman," "Israeli Affairs," etc. Articles are mostly in Arabic, but there is also a smaller section for English articles.

  • Jerusalem Media and Communication Centre - JMCC was established in 1988 by a group of Palestinian journalists and researchers to provide information on events in the West Bank (including East Jerusalem) and the Gaza Strip." Their website, a great source of information, publishes special reports, its occasional document series (reprints of resolutions, agreements, correspondence and other documents related to the peace process and Palestinian legislation in Arabic and English), and their Palestine Report online.



PALESTINIAN SOURCES (Arabic)

  • Palestinian Authority - hourly updates in English and Arabic are available.

  • Amwaj Radio (Ramallah, Palestine) - Live RealAudio radio broadcasts in Arabic

  • Al-Quds Online - Al-Quds is the Palestinian newspaper with the largest circulatation, numbering around 25,000. Al-Quds Online's material, fresh each day, includes a selection of news, comment and cartoons.

  • Al-Ayyam Newspaper - Al-Ayyam's circulation is around 5,000 and it's website offers the day's news in a clean layout and presentation.

  • Al-Hayat Al-Jadedah Online - Al-Hayat is another local Palestinian newspaper online, said to be the official organ of the Palestinian Authority.

  • Assenara - Arabic newspaper based in Nazareth, their URL changed sometime in early 1999.

  • Al-Esteqlal - An Arabic language newspaper which is moving to daily publication and is based in Gaza.

  • Kul Al-Arab - An Arabic newspaper based in Nazareth. Regularly updated.

  • Al Sabar - An Arabic language bi-weekly newspaper published by the Organization for Democratic Action.

  • Arab Media Internet Network - AMIN - Offers the main news stories, plus an archive full of older articles and stories which are all available online, organised into sections such as "Eye on Jerusalem," "Eye on Amman," "Israeli Affairs," etc. Articles are mostly in Arabic, but there is also a smaller section for English articles.
