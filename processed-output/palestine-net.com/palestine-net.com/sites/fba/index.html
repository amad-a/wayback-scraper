The Friends of the Blind Association is a charitable institution concerned with the various aspect of life of the blind. It was founded in the city of Al-Bireh/Ramallah, Palestine in 1976. Its activities cover all parts of Palestine. FBA is authorized by the social welfare office in the district of Ramallah and is a member of the Union of Charitable Associations in West Bank and Jordan.

FBA Goals:

The Friends of the Blind Association aims at:

  • Safeguarding all interests of the blind on all levels
  • Working toward the improvement of the conditions of the blind, be they social, economic, educational or vocational
  • Raising the public awareness as to the situation, needs and conditions of the blind people and promoting the public’s understanding and response to the problems and needs facing the blind people of the Palestinian population.

Achievements:

Education: FBA established The National School for Blind Girls in 1978 in a building rented from the department of Islamic Waqf. In 1992, the school acquired a Thermoform Braillion Duplicator and a supply of special Braille paper . Students’ desks were renewed as well. The association also purchased a central electronic Braille machine (Everest D). To encourage education, the FBA offers scholarships for its outstanding students to help them continue their education.

Health: Admitted students undergo a medical examination and those classified as treatable get due attention by the association to help them medically. Most medical care is provided by St. Johns Hospital in Jerusalem (Alquds) and a doctor visits the school once a week.

Nutrition: FBA provides the students with all food necessities.

Administration and Logistics:

In addition to the school, the FBA has a rented flat for the accommodation of teachers and for blind girls working in factories Ramallah. The school obtained a computer and a fax machine in 1992 to facilitate administrative duties.

Future Plans:

FBA is planning to improve and extend its current services. Planned activities include:

  • Expanding the school and increasing the number of employees
  • Purchasing laundry equipment and a cleaner
  • Obtaining a computer and special related equipment/devices to help the students learn in an efficient way
  • Getting books, equipment and furniture for the school library as well as a public library for the blind

If you can help:

Despite the lack of a stable income, FBA managed to support its current activities thanks to miscellaneous financial help/donations from kind and concerned people. However, help from local sources is insufficient for our future plans.

The Friends of the Blind Associations will be very thankful to those who’d like to extend their generous hand and help us achieve our goals.
