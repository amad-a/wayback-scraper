Computerized 3D graphics has played a big part in bridging the gap between reality and the imagination. With today's 3D tools becoming more enhanced and affordable, it would be safe to say that computerized 3D graphics and animation is here to stay, and will be available to a wider range of users with time.


With the use of computerised 3D, graphic artists and non-professionals alike can produce imagery and animations that can be applied to a wide variety of industries and professions. With use of such software, users are no longer bound by convention, but only by the limitations of their own imagination.

Try taking a look at Pacific Data Images , a California based animation and production firm that uses the latest technology in computerised 3D Graphics and Video for top film production.

 


These pages cover the basic concepts, methods and techniques that go into Computerized 3D Graphics and Animation as well as looking at how Computerised 3D can be applied. In addition there are a series of online tutorials on how to use trueSpace2 by Caligari Corp , which will demonstrate some 3D techniques and methods. As well as giving you basic practical and creative experience with a 3D animation software package, this will also provide you with a stepping stone into the world of computerized animation.

N.B
These pages are mainly aimed at a PC windows based"desktop" level of computing rather than high end workstation production. However, a lot of the concepts and methods that are covered do apply to a broad spectrum of platforms and software ranging from Intel, Apple and SGI based systems.

One of the underlying messages I am trying to get across is that users can attain excellent results when using cheaper lower end 3D applications such as trueSpace by Caligari. The features of this package are outstanding and available at a cost of well under $NZD1000, which 2 years ago would of cost a buyer well in excess of $NZD$5000.
