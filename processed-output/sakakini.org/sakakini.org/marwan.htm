Marwan Qassab- Bashi

The Centre hosted the  exhibit of Syrian artist Marwan Qassab Bashi.Marwan is one of the most important Arab artists working today, and enjoysgreat fame in Europe. This exhibit,  organized in cooperation withthe Ramallah Goethe Institut, was held as a result of  Marwan’s wishto donate  to  “the children of Palestine” a collection of 75etchings of great value on the occasion of the 50th anniversary of theNakba. These were distributed between the Sakakini and Bir Zeit University.

Professor Jorn Merkert, director of the Berlin Museum of Modern Artand chief expert on Marwan's work, attended the opening, commented a shortdocumentary film on Marwan after the opening, and gave a lecture and slideshow at the Bir Zeit opening.

This important gestures underscores the solidarity of internationalartists with the struggle of the Palestinian people, and the gift willserve as the nucleus for a museum of contemporary art works donated byinternational  artists, in solidarity with the people of Palestine,that the Sakakini will try to establish in Ramallah.

The exhibit featured mainly prints and etchings that showcased the mostimportant phases of Marwan’s career: faces, people standing, face-landscapes,marionettes, etc.. The donation also featured a number of unique prints(1/1) and  watercolors. The exhibit’s poster was also a rare limitededition signed serigraphy.

Marwan was born in1934 in Damascus, and has studied fine arts in Berlin. He  participatedat the Venice Biennial, the Dokumenta, the Paris FIAC, and held numeroussolo exhibitions throughout the world, among them at Paris’ BibliothéqueNationale and Institut du Monde Arabe, and Amman’s Darat el Funoun. Heis considered one of the leading visual artists of    Germany,and many of his works can be found in European museums.
