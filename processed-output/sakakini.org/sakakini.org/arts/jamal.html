Jamal Badran



The Artist:
Badran (1909-1999) studied Islamic art at the Hamzaoui School in Cairo from 1922 to 1927. After his return from Egypt to Palestine he began working on the first major 20th century renovation of the Aqsa and Dome of the Rock mosques in Jerusalem from 1927 to 1928. He was sent by the British Mandate Government ruling Palestine at the time, to the UK to study at the Central College of Arts and Crafts from 1934 to 1937. After his return he taught Islamic art in many colleges. After the Nakba, he was forced to flee to Syria. He went to work for the UNESCO in Libya from 1952 to 1962 as an expert in art education. Badran returned to Palestine later that year and lived in Ramallah were he ran a workshop. He received a UNESCO award in 1994 for his work on glass. Badran died in Amman, Jordan.
