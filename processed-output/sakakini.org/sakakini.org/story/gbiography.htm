Gharib Asqalany


KSCC Photo

Biography:

Gharib Asqalany’s real name is Ibrahim el Zant. His pseudonym(meaning stranger from Asqalan), is a reference to his 1947 birth in MajdalAsqalan, & his family’s migration from it in 1948 to the Gaza strip.Asqalan is now emptied of all its original Palestinian inhabitants, &renamed Ashqelon.

Asqalany obtained a BSc. in Agronomy from Alexandria Universityin 1969, a BA in Islamic Studies from Cairo University in 1983, & workedin Syria for four years as an agricultural engineer. He was forbidden bythe Israelis from working in Agronomy in Gaza, and taught instead, for20 years in Gaza secondary schools. He now works at the Palestinian ministryof Culture in Gaza City. He writes a weekly column for the daily AlHayat al Jadida.

He was awarded the Short Story Award in 1977 by BethlehemUniversity, and by the Palestinian Writers’ Union in 1990. He was amongthe selection of Palestinian writers participating in the 1997 PalestinianCultural Spring/ Belles Etrangéres manifestations in France.
