IsmailShammout

Ismaïl Shammout   is considered tobe a founder of modern Palestinian visual arts, holding the first stand-alonepainting exhibit in Gaza City in 1953. He is a prolific and consistentpainter of realist scenes of the Palestinian tragedy and struggle: TheNakba, the exodus of refugees, civilians’ massacres, the rise of resistanceand the Fedayeen, as well as idyllic depictions of Palestinian rurallife. Many of his works are widely reproduced in Palestinian publications,and became domestic icons of sorts.  Born in 1930 in Lydda, his familysought refuge in Khan Yunis after the Nakba. He studied at Cairo’s finearts college and at Rome’s fine arts academy.  He was appointed in1965 as head of the PLO’s fine arts department, and  obtained a specialPalestine Award for visual arts in 1997. He has exhibited widely throughoutthe world, and now lives and works in Jordan, where he has been experimentingwith computer art. He has written extensively on Palestinian art.
