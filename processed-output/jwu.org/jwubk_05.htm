THE ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE OF JWU

The current organizational structure of JWU is the result of years of minor adjustments and changes to the original structure. Thus, the current structure does not properly reflect the distribution of functions and responsibilities among divisions and employees within the organization. JWU management recognizes these defects and sets its goals to achieve improvements in clearly defining the levels within the structure, structuring the lower levels, proper distribution of functions among organizational sections, separating customer services division and creating an Administrative Division that will report directly to the General Manager and bringing together existing functions such as the secretarial and computer services, as well as personnel and public relations.

THE ADMINISTRATION

Administrative Affairs are run by a team of aides attached tothe General Manager's office. Presently, three sections reportdirectly to the Manager:

the Computer Section is running two Local Area Networks ( LANs) operating 24 hours a day. The capacity of these networks is4.5 GB. They support 25 concurrent users working on accounting,engineering, invoicing, storing, personnel, word processing, transportand technical services. The hardware and software in this sectionare upgraded constantly to keep up with modern software programs,thus improving the efficiency and the performance at JWU.

The Secretarial and General Services Section handles most of theday-to-day secretarial activities in JWU. All of the word processing,typing and filing takes place in this section. Consumers records,contracts, national and international aid and all other kindsof files are kept and maintained in this section.

Personnel and Public Relations Officer reports also to the GeneralManager and handles contacts with employees and with nationaland international organizations.

THE TECHNICAL DIVISION

The Technical Division in the Undertaking consists of two sections;the network and the Meter Sections. The Network Section amongother things handles water distribution, maintenance of lines,laying of new consumers connections, answering emergency callsfor network repairs, projects design, monitoring and supervision.

The Meter Section is responsible for installing new meters, dismantlingand maintaining used meters, recording the reading of meters,distributing water bills and collecting payments from consumersin addition to dealing with consumers complaints.

THEMECHANICAL DIVISION

The primary responsibilities of the Mechanical Division are toensure the smooth running of all the wells, pumps and pumpingstations and fix as quickly as possible any malfunction in thepumps and motors to ensure continuity of water distribution. Allmechanical and electrical designs, contract documents tendering,maintenance for pumps equipment and vehicles are done by the mechanicalsection, or under its full supervision.

THE ACCOUNTING DIVISION

The Accounting Division handles the accounting system in the Undertaking,the warehouses, inventory, book keeping, prepare financial statements,reports, payroll, end- of- year balance sheet and final accounts,in addition to receiving payments from consumers who wish to payat the Undertaking.

PROPOSEDORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURES FOR JWU

The proposed organizational structures were developed taking intoconsideration the areas of concern and shortcomings identifiedin the current organizational structure as well as the potentialrequirements and future developments expected to take place onboth the long and short term.

ShortTerm Organizational Structure

The proposed short term organizational structure was developedin a manner which provides solutions to the areas of concern andshortcomings identified in the current structure. The major changesmade with regard to this structure include:

  • The establishment of an Administrative Division
  • The establishment of a Personnel and Public Relations Sectionand the introduction of jobs related to this section.
  • The introduction of the position of Purchasing Officer inthe Administrative Division.
  • The introduction of the positions of Executive Secretary andDevelopment Coordinator reporting to the General Manager.
  • The assignment of the responsibility of general maintenanceand transportation to the Mechanical Division through the establishmentof the Maintenance and Transport Section.
  • The separation of the functions of the Network (previouslyTechnical) Division into a Planning and Projects Section and aNetwork Operation and Maintenance Section.
  • The separation of the section previously named the Water MetersSection from the Technical Division and changing its name to theCustomers Services Division.

LongTerm Organizational Structure

The proposed long term organizational structure Figure 4 takes into consideration all changes made on the short term aswell as potential requirements and development at JWU. The majorchanges made in the long term (as compared to the short term)include:

  • The introduction of the responsibility of sewerage and treatedwater networks to the overall responsibilities of the JWU throughthe establishment of the related sections in both the Mechanicaland Network Divisions.
  • The introduction of the function of Quality Control to thefunctions of the JWU through the establishment of the QualityControl Division including the Inspection and Testing Sectionand the Quality Assurance Section.
  • The establishment of the Supplies Section in the AdministrativeDivision which includes the Purchasing and Inventory functions.
  • The introduction of the function of Cost Accounting to theFinancial Division (previously Accounting Division) through theestablishment of the Cost Accounting and Financial Studies Section.
  • The establishment of the Geographic Information and the LeakDetection Units within the Network Division.
  • The possible elimination of the Inspection and CollectionDivision since door to door fee collection may not be requiredin the future. The fee collectors will thus be transferred toother jobs while the Unit Supervisor may be transferred to thejob of Inspector for the Customer Services Division.
  • The establishment of the posts of Assistant to the GeneralManager for Administrative, Financial and Quality Control andAssistant to General Manager for Technical, Customer Servicesand Mechanical Affairs.

The number of Mechanics and Guards at the Ein Samya and otherPumping Stations are expected to be reduced in the near futureonce remote control is installed in these stations. These employeeswill subsequently be transferred to other jobs at JWU, or theircontracts terminated.
