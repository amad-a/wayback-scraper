National and International Cooperation

For the last 28 years, JWU built up an excellent reputation locally and internationally. JWU was mentioned in the World Bank Report (Volume V) that was published in September 1993 more than once as the most efficient and best run water authority in the West Bank and Gaza. Jerusalem Water Undertaking with its limited capabilities and human resources, plays a major role in participating in major activities whether initiated by JWU or by the communities it serves. Training

Jerusalem Water Undertaking realizes the importance of improvingthe capacity of the water sector employees in general and itshuman resources in specific. Keeping up with modern methods thatmeet international standards necessitates having appropriate trainingprogram in place, to meet the training requirements of the Undertaking.JWU sees itself in the near future as a regional training centerin the Water and Waste Water fields. International organizationssuch as the World Bank, GTZ and CDG and UNDP are working withthe Undertaking to develop a training concept and facilities thatwill benefit the Undertaking and the region as a whole.

CDG planned a training workshop that was held in Bethlehem inMarch of 1995 for which JWU was a facilitator. JWU professionalsand other organizations in the West Bank and Gaza were invitedto come up with a training program in the water and waste waterfields. The outcome of the workshop was a comprehensive reporton training in the fields of water supply and waste water management.Problems and training needs have been identified and a trainingprogram was developed.

GTZ on the other hand signed an agreement with JWU in which improvingthe management and staff skills is one of the results expectedafter three years.

JWU conducted a training program for staff from 6 West Bank villages.This training session, was funded by the UNDP and it requiredthe participation of more than 20 management and staff membersfrom JWU to conduct training lectures after work hours and onthe job training for 6 weeks. The participants of this trainingsession benefited greatly and UNDP was extremely pleased withthe results of this training. A training booklet for techniciansand accountants was published by JWU. It included the lectures,the training materials, diagrams, specifications and methods tobe followed in managing the small scale water utilities. Anothergroup from two villages also sponsored by the UNDP started trainingat the Undertaking in October 1995.

Additionally, JWU is constantly providing on the job trainingfor graduates of educational and vocational institutions in theWest Bank. These graduates gain the knowledge provided by themany years of experience the employees of JWU posses and go intothe work market prepared in their fields.

JWU is also very keen on providing training for its own staff.During the last few years a number of its employees were sentabroad for administrative and technical training and many participatedin courses sponsored by local universities and continuing educationinstitutions, to enhance their knowledge and expand their knowhow.

JWU initiated and implemented a rotating employment program for2-6 months periods to absorb some of the community un employedand provide training to the graduates of education and vocationalinstitutions.

Studies

Due to its leadership position in the water aspects, JWU was involvedin many studies in the last few years. These studies were eitherconducted exclusively by JWU management and staff, or with thehelp of local and international experts. The following is a sampleof studies and a brief description of each one:

  • Institutional Development Study.

This study was initiated by the Jerusalem Water Undertaking (JWU)- Ramallah District with the aim of developing its institutionalsystems and plans through the revision of its organizational andsalary structures as well as the introduction of new personnelpolicies and systems including job and employee ranking, performanceappraisal and retirement plans.

Although the need for this study has been realized by JWU managementfor years, the unstable situation in the West Bank since 1987,did not make it possible to initiate and implement it until now.The study was initiated by the JWU General Manager with the approvalof the Board of Directors. It was conducted by JWU employees underthe direct supervision of the General Manager and with the finalapproval of the Board of Directors. Various aspects of this study,especially the job evaluation process, were carried out throughthe Job Description Committee which includes two members of theBoard, the General Manager, an employee representative and thecommittee coordinator.

  • Water and SewerAIntegration Study.

This study was conducted by JWU General Manager and El Bireh MunicipalityChief Engineer with support from the UNDP. The main issue thatthis study dealt with, is the existence of an efficient and wellorganized framework for the management of water and sewerage inPalestine. This is an essential factor in the effort to conservewater resources and control usage and demand. The final revisionof this study is available at the offices of the Undertaking.

JWU staff and local experts contributed and participated inthree studies ( Water Resources Management, Water Saving and WaterCost ) that were funded and sponsored by THE INSTITUE MEDITERRANEANDEL'AU STUDIES ( IME ). Copies of these studies are availableat the offices of the Undertaking:-

  • Water Resource Management and Water Saving Study.

This study dealt with the current water resources in the PalestinianTerritory (PT) (e.g. the inland region of the West Bankand the coastal region of the Gaza Strip) and the ways and meansto manage these resources and saving and rationing the use ofwater. The main focus of the study was JWU area of supply andthe study dealt with the following issues:

1. The unaccounted for water percentage and components were analyzed.

2. The management of supply and demand was focused upon

3. The ways and means to reduce leakage and improve the efficiencyof water supply in Ramallah area.

4. A list of solutions and priority action plan was proposed

  • Water Cost Study

This study intended to examine the water production cost at theJWU in order to reduce the cost of production and distributionas well as increase the efficiency of all technical, financialand administrative aspects of JWU. The study also intended toaccomplish the following purposes:

a. To identify deficiencies in the present accounting and financialsystem and suggests ways and means to improve it, in order toserve the management in investment and managerial decision making.

b. To identify the inefficient areas of operations using standardfinancial ratios and water efficiency indicators.

c. To introduce a cost accounting system for the JWU to measurethe cost of production, to plan projects activities, and to reducecost.

d. To formulate a framework to articulate financial, cost andbudgeting system using a unified chart of accounts.

e. To state the specific components of fixed and variable costitems to be used as a base to establish an annual flexible budgetformula.

f. To identify new investment projects which may decrease thewater cost and increase the efficiently of operations .

The following Studies were conducted by the General Managerof JWU and were distributed to the interested national and internationalbodies working in the water sector in Palestine:

  • Cost Recovery in Public Utilities, 1995

This study was conducted by the General Manager of JWU with theobjective of pointing out the main ways and means that will leadto improving the financial self sufficiency of Palestinian publicservice utilities with particular reference and emphasis on thoseoperating in the water sector.

  • Water and Peace in the Region, September, 1995

This paper focuses on the water resources in the region with specificdetails about the Palestinian water rights and why the Palestinianmust insist on their water rights and not on water quantitiesor allocations. It also presents the outcome of the Jordanian-Israeliwater agreement.

  • An Institutional Framework for Water within the PalestinianNational Authority , March 1994

This study focuses on proposing the proper institutional frameworkwhich will suit the requirements and conditions of Palestine,since the existence of an efficient and well organized frameworkfor management of water and sewerage institutions in Palestineis an essential factor in the effort to conserve water and controlusage demand.
