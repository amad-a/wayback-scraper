WATER SUPPLY DEVELOPMENT PROJECT

Jerusalem Water Undertaking (JWU), Ramallah/AL Bireh- District

GTZ Project No. 93.2257.9

 

PROJECT HISTORY ANDBACKGROUND

 

In order to improve the existing water supply situation in theRamallah and Al-Bireh District where available sources of water were far fromfulfilling the minimum needs of the district. s population, Jerusalem WaterUndertaking (JWU) submitted a request to the German Government to fund drillingof new and rehabilitation of existing deep wells. In response, a GTZ projectappraisal mission was sent to Ramallah in May 1993. In the course of themission JWU and GTZ realised that a comprehensive development project includingtechnical assistance should be considered without ignoring that drilling andrehabilitation of wells as requested is of utmost importance.

 

After a joint planning workshop held in Ramallah in June1993 to prepare the project proposal, which included the scope of the project,expected results and required resources, the proposal was submitted by GTZ tothe German Ministry for Economic Co-operation and Development (BMZ) and theapproval for project implementation along with the necessary funds (about 7.3millions Deutsch Marks) was received by end of 1993.

 

The first phase of the project was concluded by end of 1997and it was agreed to extend it to a second three-year phase. The German fundingfor the second phase amounts to DM 4.5 millions of which DM 1.5 millions wasallocated for well construction.

 

The Water Supply Development Project currently implementedby Jerusalem Water Undertaking with GTZ support is an ambitious attempt towardsfundamental institutional changes and infrastructure improvement which takesinto consideration the implications of the potential integration of wastewaterservices in Ramallah and Al-Bireh District into the scope of service of JWU.

 

PROJECT CONCEPT ANDOBJECTIVES

 

In line with the results of the planning workshop for thesecond phase (September 1997), the project shall contribute to improve theliving and environmental health situation of the population of Ramallah andAl-Bireh District. Therefore, the objective of the project is to meet moreefficiently the drinking water requirements of the District, to reduce thedependency on purchased water and to prepare JWU to play a major role inintegrating water supply and wastewater services in Ramallah and Al-BirehDistrict.

 

To achieve the a.m. objectives, the following areas ofintervention were identified:

 

Þ    Water quantity produced by JWU is to beincreased on a sustainable basis.

Þ    Unaccounted for water is to be reducedthrough cost effective measures.

Þ    Provision of services is to be managedon cost effective basis.

Þ    Performance of staff at all levels isto be enhanced.

Þ    Population awareness concerning waterand wastewater issues is to be enhanced.

Þ    Implications of integrating water andwastewater services are to be elaborated.

 

The German contribution to the project consists of technicalassistance, long term advisory and short term consulting services as well ascapital investment for the

Construction of new and rehabilitation of existing deepwells.

 

 

PROGRESS ANDPROSPECTS

 

Þ   Drillingnew deep wells and rehabilitation of existing wells:

The implementation of the project has been initiated sinceMay 1994. The first six months of the project duration was totally devoted todrilling the substitute well Ein Samia No. 2a. To overcome the water shortagein the Ramallah/Al-Bireh District, the well was initially connected to thewater supply system on a temporary basis mid 1995 and presently, is in fulloperation with a capacity of 165 m³/hour, well above the expected capacity of120 m³/hour.

 

Afterward, a GTZ specialist identified two potential wellsites in December 1995, one of which is located in Ein Samia and the other oneis in the Ein Senia area. Like Ein Senia No.5, identified by an IsraeliConsultant and the West Bank Water Department, Ein Senia site No.6 was drilledand unfortunately with no success. On the other hand, Ein Samia site No.5 iscurrently being drilled and developed with promising results. Also, Ein Samiawell No. 3 was rehabilitated and its capacity was increased from 136 m³/hour to185 m³/hour. Also, in order to get a broader picture of the main geologicalfaults and fractures in the region, a stereoscopic analysis and evaluation ofaerial photographs of the Ramallah/Al-Bireh District was carried out.

 

  Þ Protectionof Water Resources

Based on a recent preliminary environmental assessment ofJWU well fields in Ein Samia area and some locations of distributionfacilities, the need for an action plan for the protection and preservation ofJWU water resources has been pointed out as of utmost urgency. JWU and GTZ haveinitiated a preliminary study on the a.m. issue and the results are due in fewweeks.

 

Þ   Remotecontrol and communication system

In it. s efforts to optimise and economise well and pump operations JWUmanaged to introduce a modern remote control and communication system forthe wells, booster pumps, main pumping stations and reservoirs. Development, programmingand installation were made . in-house. by JWU at a fractionof the cost of a . turn-key. installation. Another initiative for theintroduction of data loggers for meter reading is underway.

 

Þ   Leakagecontrol and pressure management

Although the unaccounted-for water is at 22 %, JWUundertakes continuous efforts to monitor and reduce the water losses. As partof active leakage control measures, the project introduced computerised leakagecontrol and pressure management models in order to cut down the water losses ona systematic basis. A case study on water losses carried out at JWU showedclearly that a major part of the water losses occur due to faulty andinaccurate meters. In response, JWU replaced the old meter test bench by amodern one, which enables the meter section to test the water meters moreefficiently and regularly. The new test bench was purchased from the projectfunds. With the availability of such installation, it is hoped that other watersupply departments in the West Bank can make use of it.

 

Þ    Institutionalcapacity building

Although, JWU. s performance and absorption capacity isbetter than other water departments in Palestine, the need to prepare JWU tomeet the next millennium challenges amongst which is the possible integrationof wastewater services into the scope of its service, has never been debated.Therefore, JWU and with the support of GTZ has undertaken a comprehensiveorganisation development process. The ongoing process, best described as aprocess of forming future strategies and objectives that should be translatedinto jobs people do on regular basis, is progressing well. Presently JWU isprogressively implementing a new organisation structure, working on theintroduction of management-by-results system and a new compensation scheme thatrewards actual performance and seniority, and appreciates extra efforts.

 

Þ   Training

JWU plays a major role in training of water sector staff in Palestine.Training contracts were awarded to JWU by UNDP in the past years. Alsoand as part of the German financed water sector training programme for Palestine,JWU was nominated as an official local partner for the Carl Duisberg Gesellschaft,CDG. In order to prepare and manage these training courses, a Palestinian. water training board. was established with CDG and GTZ representedin this board. The second phase of the successful programme is being run by thePalestinian Engineering Association as the official CDG Partner.

 

Based on its training experience, JWU recognised thattraining in the water sector should be more co-ordinated and organised.Therefore, a comprehensive national strategy for training in the Palestinianwater sector was developed by a GTZ specialist in co-operation with JWU and thePalestinian Water Authority, PWA. However, implementing steps have yet to betaken by PWA.

 

Arranged by GTZ, JWU General Manager and some Key Staff hasparticipated in a number of international, regional and local workshops andfairs

 

Þ   Publicawareness campaigns

JWU played a major role in establishing and managing the publicawareness campaigns initiated by UNDP/Water resources action programme (WRAP)in 1994. However, since end of 1995 this programme did not continue. JWU andin line with the relevant results of the second planning workshop, has ideasto set up it. s own awareness programme in the future.

 

Þ   Wastewatermanagement

Based on the Palestinian national strategy that advocatesthe establishment of regional water and sanitation utilities, JWU as a regionalautonomous water supply utility in the Ramallah -Al-Bireh District will have avital role in managing wastewater services in the District. This role stemsfrom its need to protect and safeguard its water resources and from its overallproven management capacity.

 

The need for action in the field of wastewater managementwas highlighted in the 1993 project-planning workshop and JWU assumedresponsibility to co-ordinate and facilitate activities in this field. Aregional steering committee was established in which the main municipalitiesand villages and representatives of External Support Agencies were represented.Also, initiated by JWU and UNDP, in co-ordination with GTZ and under theauspices of PWA, a Sewerage Masterplan Study for the Ramallah and Al-BirehDistrict (funded by FINIDA) was prepared. The study clearly called for majorinvestment in the sewerage infrastructure as well as for institutionalintegration of water supply and wastewater services.

 

In response, the Finnish government committeditself to fund a Bridging project for the possible integration of wastewaterservices in the District. The project is due to start shortly. GTZ is playing amajor role in such move and the a.m. organisation development process is anintegral part of the efforts towards preparing JWU to play a major role in theintegration process. Moreover, should the Bridging project become a reality,GTZ would work with JWU and all other concerned parties on other implicationsand/or relevant issues of the integration.

 

Þ   JWU. srole in water sector development in Palestine

Due to it. s unique history and legal status as an autonomous watersupply utility and it. s performance, JWU serves now as a model utilityfor the establishment of similar utilities in whole of Palestine. Its activerole has a substantial impact on water sector development in Palestine.
