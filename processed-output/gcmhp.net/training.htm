Contact person: AbdelHamid Afana
 

Philosophy:

TheTraining and Education Department’s philosophy is to develop advocatesin mental health who are concerned with mobilizing the social servicesin the community to assist individuals in feeling that they are membersof the community and not merely members in the community. Their role hasbeen changed from that of providing the care to facilitating care; thus,enabling people to solve their problems.
 
When established in 1990, the primarystaff at the Gaza Community Mental Health Programme (GCMHP) recognizedthe absence of training institutes that could adequately prepare mentalhealth professionals in Gaza. To overcome this problem, the Programme embarkedon ambitious in-service training activities forthe staff at GCMHP and attracted trainers from prestigious training institutesfrom around the world. The trainers provided in-house training to the GCMHPstaff so as to enable them to offer quality services to the Palestinianpopulation. Several selected members of the staff went abroad to furthertheir professional skills and returned with an increased capacity to assistin the training of local staff. The capacity of the Palestinian staff toconduct their own culturally sensitive training has also increased. In1995, a one-year training of trainer’s course was conducted forfive mental health professionals from GCMHP. The course aimed to improvethe local trainers' training skills, enabling them to properly train others.
 
In addition to training GCMHP staff,the Programme has increasingly been called upon to train other Palestinianhealth care providers in various mental health skills. For example, in1993 the Programme successfully completed a three-monthcomprehensive course in community mental health nursing. Twelvenurses from the Palestinian Health System attended the course. In 1994,the department conducted a one-year course in communitymental health . Twelve social workers, psychologists, nurses, anddoctors from different governmental and non-governmental organizationscompleted the course. These promising results encouraged the training Departmentto organize another course in 1995 for another fifteen professionals. Uponthe completion of the course, the Palestinian Ministry of Education employedall students as mental health professionals in health institutions andas school counselors. In 1996, the department organized a sixmonth training course for a women community leader for twelve communitywomen leaders completed the course. Currently, they are running their owncommunity women center in the southern part of Gaza. Twelve more studentsparticipated another one-year women community leaders course. Thegraduates opened their own community women center in Beach Camp in GazaCity.
To improve the communication skillsand to enable kindergarten teachers to instruct children and their parents,the training department organized two six-month trainingcourses for child care providers in 1997. Forty participants, selectedfrom different kindergartens in the Gaza Strip, attended these courses.
In 1994, the department developedplans to introduce a Post-graduate Diploma inCommunity Mental Health for mental health workers of any disciplinein an attempt to address this problem and compensate for the total lackof psychiatric training in Palestine. Training aimed to ensure that candidateshave adequate knowledge of state of the art theories, principles, and treatmentand prevention strategies used in addressing mental health concerns. Thetraining is based on a spirit of the candidate’s active participation andself-learning. The diploma will be the first step towards the formationof an Arab Institute of Community Mental Health Sciences that will attract international students and researchers.
The curriculum for the courses taughtby the GCMHP are culturally sensitive and are based on the needs of thestudents and the community. The staff of GCMHP is responsible for conductinglectures as well as practical training placements.
In-ServiceTraining activities:
1. Journal clubs and case conferences
Aim:
To utilize the expertise of GCMHPstaff by ensuring that they share their knowledge and experience with theircolleagues. The clinical staff meets for journal clubs and case conferencesthat are held alternately. These activities are carried out in each clinicunder the supervision of the team leaders. A seminar or study day heldmonthly analyzes certain social phenomena.
2. In-service training courses
Aim:

The Training and Education departmentinvited Harriet Barli to give this course that aims to provide the potentialGCMHP trainers with skills related to training others in the field of mentalhealth.
