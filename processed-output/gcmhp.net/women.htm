About the Women's EmpowermentProject (WEP):
 
The Women's Empowerment Project (WEP)forms part of the Gaza Community Mental Health Programme. The ultimategoal of the WEP , is to improve the quality of life of women victims ofviolence, their families and community, through strengthening of localrehabilitation, education and training facilities. The ongoing deteriorationof the socio-economic and political situation in the Gaza Strip has leadto an increase in violence, specially against women and children. Thisalso lead to an increased demand by women for the WEP services. The WEPhas responded by expanding its range of viable services. At the presenttime the WEP offers facilities allowing for women to be trained in vocationaland income generating skills, while at the same time providing psychologicaland legal counseling, and education in self development, women's health,social and legal issues. The WEP has three center, in Gaza, Rafah and BeachCamp. The further plans include the replication of these centers in themiddle camps area due to their present lack of services.
Using a Training of Trainers approachwill help to develop a pool of  female community leaders, workingas educators and mentors within the community.
Currently, training courses are beingdeveloped for women who were unable to complete their secondary schooling,and networks are being established with local policewomen to cooperatein a training course which will increase their skills and awareness indealing with vulnerable sectors of the community.
Since its establishment, the projecthas focused on the community approach. Workshops, public meetings and supportgroups are organized beside lectures from within or outside the center.The project philosophy and believes that a woman school not be consideredjust as separate unit, but should be dealt with the family as a whole.
Being conscious of the importanceof gender awareness within the framework of training of trainers and education,the project also realized that this issue is not exclusive to women, soefforts will be made to regularly monitor the impact of these courses onmale members of families, and by extension, the broader community.
In 1997 the WEP established a resourceunit and started to collect information in order to establish a data baseconcerning violence against women in the community. As the involvementof women in the development process is essential, these information collectedby the resource center will be helpful in designing future research.
