Women's EmpowermentProject
Stories of Success

According to needs assessment  surveys conducted by the CommunityDevelopment Leaders in both Beach camp and Rafah areas, the economicalproblem was the first to be identified by women.
The WEP main goal is to empower women victims through education andwork by providing different services both to them or to their family members.
 

 
Fifi, 24-year-old, single,secondary school graduate. She was born into a  large family of 2brothers, and 4 sisters.  Nobody gave her any  attention. Theoldest brother was the main power source and control in the family. Hecontrolled everyone including Fifi herself, by beatings and insults. Fifitried to commit suicide several times not only because of that brotherbut also because of her other educated sisters insults . They succeededto have higher education while she was forced to leave school because ofher mother’s illness. Even  she was forced to leave her fiancee after3 years  of engagement.
On 1 July 1997, Fifi  joined the WEP. Among the services providedto her were, vocational training, psycho-social counseling, family counseling,legal and health education.

After finishing her training at the project, Fifi started a small projectwhich included flower arrangement, ceramics and glass painting. She isworking now  towards marketing her products by herself.

Sawsan, 34-year-old, widow,secondary school graduate. She has three children. After 2 years at theIslamic University she was forced to leave because of economical reasons.Later  she found  work  and met a man who became  herhusband  and who was  killed  during the Intifada by Palestinianactivists.  After this his family threw  her and  her children out of  house, but by the help of some people and police she returnedback to their home.  They only gave her a small room at the entranceof the house.  Sawsan Joined the WEP on 1  July 1997 and finishedher training on 31 December 1997. Among the services provided to Sawsan  at the WEP were vocational training, psycho-social counseling, health andlegal education.

Sawsan started a small knitting business  and  began marketingher products by herself.

Nuha, 35-year-old, married and has 8 children. Nuha was forced to marry her cousin who has polio whichmade him unable to work. Her problems started prior to her marriage. Shelived with her family  in Egypt where her father was wanted becauseof accusations against  him of killing somebody. The father escapedto Jordan and the mother returned back to Gaza with her five children,one of  whom was Nuha. After marriage her mother left Gaza with herchildren to Iraq  while her father stayed in Jordan because of fears of revenge. Nuha lost her family economical and emotional support.
She joined the WEP  on 1 July 1997 and finished her training atthe end of December 1997. Her project is crystal and pottery decoration.She  markets her products by herself.
 
Aida, 22-year-old, Married,she has a 3-year- child. After she finished  her preparatory educationshe was forced to marry her cousin. Her suffering started the first dayof marriage. She lived with his  big family in a tiny room. They usedher like a servant and if she refused to obey she was beaten and insultedby her husband. Once he hit her with a stick and caused a problem withher eyes.
Her  husband does  not work  because he  suffersfrom a skin disease.
Aida joined the WEP on 1 January 1997 and finished her training on30 June 1997. Among the services provided to Aida at the WEP were vocationaltraining, psychosocial counseling , health and legal education.
Her project is knitting and ceramics. Her husband  markets herproducts.

Kareema, 42-year-old, married,secondary school graduate. Kareema was one of the women  activist during the Intifada. She joined the Palestinian Women’s Union and was arrestedfor 6 months. She participated in different political activities and wasinjured 6 times by rubber bullets. Despite her family rejection to politicalparticipation, Kareema continued.
Kareema married at the age of 35 and has no children.
On  1 January 1996, Kareema joined the WEP and  finishedher training at the end of June 1996. Among the services provided at theWEP, Kareema received vocational training, psychosocial counseling, healthand legal education and  child care providers training.

Her project is establishing a child care in  the area  whereshe is living .
 
Suad, 44-year-old, widow.She has 9 children. She was born into a poor family. Her father died whenshe was 4 years old  and later on  her mother remarried and she became neglected.
When Suad finished her preparatory education, her family organizedher marriage. She lived a happy life with her husband who was killed bythe IDF during the Intifada.

Suad joined the WEP on 1 January 1997 and finished her training at theend of June 1997.  At the end of her training a sewing machine wasgiven to her as a grant  as well as  advanced training coursein fashion design. Services to her provided by WEP were psychosocial counselingsessions, health and legal education.
 
Ebtisam, 30-year-old,single.   She has a diploma in social science but she did notsucceed in finding  a work and  was not encouraged by her familyto do so because her parents are old and sick and they need somebody tolook after them. Although  her brothers are married and living inother houses they still control  her and her  young sister.
Ebtisam joined the WEP in January 1997 and finished her training byJune 1997. At the end of her
 training she  received  a sewing machine as a grant and started her project at home. At the same time she was selected to trainwomen prisoners as part of the rehabilitation programme.
 
Ferial, 22-year-old, divorce,a secondary school graduate. Ferial was born into  a poor family fromEl Nusierat refugee camp. After finishing her secondary school educationshe was forced to marry her cousin. She tried to commit suicide many times,and was beaten severely by her family to force her to marry. After fewmonths of marriage Ferial’s  psychological problems started and wasdivorced. After her divorce her family treated her badly.
Ferial joined the WEP at the beginning of 1997 and finished her trainingby the end of June 1997. The services provided by the Wep were vocationaltraining, psycho-social counseling, health and legal education.
Ferial project is glass painting, pottery decoration and she is responsible  for marketing her products.

Nisrin, 20-year-old, single,secondary school graduate.  Ten years ago her father left his wifeand his 8 children to marry another woman. After the death of Nisrin’s mother they found themselves alone without even food. They live in a garage without a kitchen or even bathroom. Nisrin joined the WEP at thebeginning of 1997 and finished her training in June 1997.  She receiveda sewing machine as a grant and started her own business at home. Lateron she joined the child care providers training course organized by WEPand she works  now at Rafah project kindergarten.
 
Ebtisam, 35-year-old,widow. She has 4 children.  Ebtisam was born into a poor family. Herfather used to beat her and her other  8 sisters and brothers severely.When Ebtisam finished her preparatory school education she was forced tomarry  a man who was 20 years older. Ebtisam was his second wife.She moved with him to Saudia Arabia where her suffering  continued.Her husband died since 6 years and she returned back home with her children.
Ebtisam joined the WEP at the beginning of July 1997 and finished hertraining at the end of 1997. Her project is a hair dressing and beautysalon in a small area of her home.
