Establishment

 

The establishment of the Rashad Shawa Cultural Center and its development:

    The Rashad Shawa Cultural Center in Gaza is the first Cultural Center to be built in Palestine.

    The commission for the design of the center was initiated by the late Rashad Shawa (1909-1988), Mayor of Gaza, in his capacity as president and founder of the Benevolent society for the Gaza Strip which acted as patron to the project.

    The building was designed by the Syrian born British architect sa’ad Mohaffel who supervised the construction and completion of the building to international standards. The Cultural Center was nominated for the Agakhan Award in 1992.

    The Center was designed to cater for the cultural needs of the population of Gaza, who suffered total isolation from the world community and endured an occupation designed to erode their cultural heritage and national identity. The objectives of building the center were to fill  this vacuume and to provide access for all Palestinians to have a platform from which it could interact with the world community for all forms of cultural exchange.

    The center has facilities for seminars, lectures, conferences, art exhibitions as well as theatre performances and the cinema. Its library, “ The Diana Tamari Sabbagh library” is designed to serve students and professionals, the library has a  lending section with over 100,000 books, videos and CD from disks.

    The cultural center started operating in 1992 and is today the focus of most, if not all, cultural and social activities in Gaza. As such, it needs the support and patronage, in order to continue fulfilling the role it set out to play in rebuilding sense of identity with dignity and restoring pride in the cultural heritage of all Palestinians.
