Bethlehem Peace Centre

On July 1, 2000 the Bethlehem Peace Centre started its activities on Manger Square and is now open to the public. 

The Bethlehem Peace Centre is a cultural centre with the aim to promote and enhance peace, democracy, religious tolerance and diversity. The centre will promote Palestinian culture and cater for the cultural needs of the citizens of Bethlehem and visitors. It is centrally located on Manger Square, the heart of the little town of Bethlehem, and as such is the natural meeting point for the citizens of Bethlehem and the thousands of tourists who come to visit here year after year. While the centre is certainly meant as a focal point for the local community, it is also meant as a springboard for international exchange and the promotion of Palestinian cultural heritage from here to everywhere. 

At the entrance floor, a book shop, and a tourist information office are happy to assist the visitors who want to know more on Bethlehem and Palestine. A restaurant will soon open its doors to visitors. The restaurant will serve quick meals and also arrange dinners and receptions. 

On the first floor you find an Auditorium that is used for concerts, theatre and film performances as well as other gatherings. Three exhibition halls are open for different temporary exhibitions, performances and meetings. The children's' room is equipped with a small library of children's' books, playing equipment and art materials. Public toilets are available to the visitors of the Bethlehem Peace Centre one flight down. This is also the office floor for the Centre where plans are underway for a municipal library and archive. Garages are available for visitors, although space is limited. 

In the basement ancient mosaics and a centuries old well have been discovered. Regrettably these historical remains cannot yet be shown to the public. 

The Centre is planning to create a membership Friends of the Centre as well as a monthly newsletter for those who wish to become involved on a regular basis. 

In close cooperation with Bethlehem Municipality, BPC is working towards a full schedule and will soon offer the citizens and visitors a wide range of events and activities.

The Swedish International Cooperation Agency, Sida has financed the rehabilitation of Manger Square and the construction of Bethlehem Peace Centre as part of the Swedish development cooperation in Palestine . The idea of the rehabilitation of Manger Square was developed by Sida and the municipality of Bethlehem in 1996. Agreement between the Municipality of Bethlehem and Sida on the construction of the Peace Centre was signed on November the 4th. The architect Snorre Lindquist designed the center.

Construction of the Peace Centre started by a Palestinian construction firm on August the 31st. Byzantine mosaics were discovered in the basement on November the 20th. Construction work was delayed by 3 months due to the discovery of the mosaics. The rehabilitation of Manger Square was completed on the 8th of February 1999. Bethlehem Peace Centre was completed on November the 28th after 1 year and 1 month. Grand opening of the Bethlehem Peace Centre was on December the 8th 1999.
