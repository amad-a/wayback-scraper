Friday March 15th, 2002

Press Release

 

          The Israeli army continued and intensified its attacks against Bethlehem, the town of the Nativity.  At dawn of Thursday March 14th, the Israeli tanks moved towards Pope Paul IV intersection in Bethlehem and opened their heavy machine gun fire and artillery at the facing area that comprises a highly populated neighbourhood, the Bethlehem University, an orphanage, the Holy Family hospital and St. Joseph School.

          Despite the Vatican flag flying over the Holy Family hospital, one tank artillery hit the Church inside the premises and penetrated through its historical walls.  Another one hit the wall of Ephpheta Institute opening a big hole in it. The Maternity Hospital with newly born babies was not spared by the random shooting.  The shining statue of Virgin Mary rising over the Church was severely damaged by the heavy Israeli gun fire.  This statue is a precious historical and religious monument constructed in the year 1885 similar to the one of the Sisters of Mercy in Paris.  

          We consider this attack being targeted at the Christian holy places  and hospitals a strong violation to Geneva Convention and disrespect to all cultural, historical and religious charts.   It is an Israeli despisement of the religious feelings of our Christian community and a clear challenge to the simple humanitarian and religious values.

          All churches and all religious institutions in the world are called upon to condemn this aggression and to intervene with the Government of Israel to guarantee the protection of our religious and cultural heritage and to stop this savage attack on our Palestinian people.

 

                                                                                            Hanna J. Nasser

Mayor of Bethlehem
