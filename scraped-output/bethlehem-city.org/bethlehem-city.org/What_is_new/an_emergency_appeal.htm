An Emergency Appeal

             Most of the municipal, civil administrative resources, documents and systems accumulated throughout long years of service, were destroyed during the Israeli incursion to Bethlehem.  At the moment, our municipal council is forced to attend to its constituents of 30,000 inhabitants with practically nothing.  Bethlehem municipality, its administrative, technical, health, sanitation and financial offices operate in a state of total devastation.

      

             The Israeli occupation authority with a determination to paralyze any civil service in the city, purposely invaded on April 2nd, 2002 the City Hall, its compound and adjacent Engineering offices.  Heavily armed and under no justifiable reason, they intentionally vandalized, destroyed and disrupted all of its contents, fixtures, and even structure.  The intensity of the damage estimated at US$ 485,591 is a caption of the physical damage itself excluding the amount of work and rehabilitation that is necessary to rebuild the whole operational system to a functioning stage.

             The incursion into the city that lasted for 38 days caused a total standstill to all aspects of life in the city.  Almost all civil, medical, legal, security and social services were paralyzed and hence has deteriorated the living condition of Bethlehem city residents as well as that of the whole district.  Families had to endure a state of total curfew, discontinued power, and communication lines, fear and anxiety 24 hours a day.  Some of the residential and commercial complexes within a diameter of 1000 meters circulating the Nativity Church have been damaged to a level beyond renovation and rehabilitation, and require complete restorations.

             The economic crisis that prevailed since September 28th 2000, the total isolation and closure of Bethlehem to outside tourism and working opportunities, has drained all personal economic resources of the local residents to be able to accommodate for their living needs let alone to meet with municipal service charges and taxes that are the primary source of revenue and operations at the Municipality.  Amidst this economic recession, the recurring incursions into the city and discontinued relations with Palestinian civic forums and authorities due to closures and isolating boundaries, the Municipality cannot meet with the drastic pressure for rehabilitation and restoration to several parts of the city under such acute lack of resources and facilities.

             The Municipality being the primary representative and provider for the city�s most basic services, is obliged more than ever before to meet up with such requirements otherwise the whole social structure could dwindle.  However, the Municipality and at its own current resources, cannot in any term nor time, provide simultaneously for its daily chores as well as to attend to the rehabilitation and restoration  necessary.  The dilemma at this stage is that neither of them can afford postponement or delay.

             As the Mayor of Bethlehem, I urge you to reach out with a helping hand in reinstating the Municipality back to its operating status prior to the incursion and hence provide the Municipal council with the appropriate monetary and in kind support necessary to assume its daily responsibility and to attend to the requirements and needs of its citizens.  Attached to this appeal are the detail cost estimates as conducted by our staff of engineers and specialists in evaluation of the losses and damages inflicted upon the City Hall.

             The damages inflicted on a civic institution such as the Municipality of Bethlehem, the city of peace, a cradle for civilization is by far an atrocity and a violation of civic rights that requires immediate attention, prevention and show of solidarity.

Hanna J. Nasser
Mayor of Bethlehem
