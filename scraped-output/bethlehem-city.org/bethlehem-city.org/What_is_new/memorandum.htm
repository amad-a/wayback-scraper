March 6th, 2003

Memorandum

     RE: RACHEL�S TOMB AREA IN BETHLEHEM

1.

Throughout history, Rachel�s Tomb has always been a part of the town of Bethlehem. It lies within the northern municipal borders of Bethlehem and is considered one of its famous religious and historical places.

2.

Following the beginning of the peace process in Madrid in 1993, the Israeli government and our Palestinian leadership signed the famous Oslo II agreement in 1995 in which both parties agreed on the arrangements regarding Rachel�s Tomb, annex 1 � article V, section 7 clearly states:

a. While the Tomb, as well as the main road leading from Jerusalem to the Tomb, as indicated, will be under the security responsibility of Israel, the free movement of Palestinians on the main road will continue.

b. For the purpose of protecting the Tomb, three Israeli guard posts may be located in the Tomb, the roof of the Waqf building and the parking lot.

c. The present situation and existing practices in the Tomb shall be preserved.

3.

In contradiction with the Oslo agreement and disregarding all international conventions, the Israeli authorities constructed on March 1996 a concrete wall in front of the Tomb closing the western side of the road and forcing the traffic at the main entrance of Bethlehem to a narrow lane less than 6 metres. Even this bottle- neck was later on closed by the Israeli forces.

4.

The Israeli authorities continued carelessly in implementation of their plans to change the features of Rachel�s Tomb area and to impose the policy of the accomplished fact in Bethlehem. On July 1999, they constructed a 120 metre x 5 metre slabed passage built on the sidewalk alongside the Tomb area.

5.

The disrespection of the holy places contained in the Tomb site (the Bilal Bin Rabah mosque and the Islamic cemetery) has deprived our people of their freedom of worship and denied them access to the cemetery to bury their deceased.

6.

On September 11, 2002, while the whole world�s attention was attracted to the first anniversary of the horrible New York and Washington attacks, the Israeli security cabinet endorsed a decision to set up a "security envelope" around Jerusalem to include Rachel�s Tomb area in Bethlehem and keep it under permanent Israeli control.

7.

The pretext is insuring secure arrival of Jews to the holy site. The real reason however is grabbing the compound and the northern entrance of Bethlehem, to annex them to Israel in order to implement the "Metropolitan Jerusalem Plan".

8.

A census was executed unlawfully on December 5th, 2002 during which the Israeli authorities registered the names and the identity card numbers of the Bethlehem citizens in that area.

9.

The Israeli occupying forces issued on February 9, 2003 a military order (Order No. 03/14/T) in which they announced seizing of Palestinian land and property at Rachel�s Tomb area for military purposes.

10.

A map was attached to the order showing a huge concrete wall the Israeli government intends to construct in the middle of the main road from the military check point until the Tomb area, to surround the holy site and the neighboring buildings and lands.

11.

Depriving our citizens from their properties and heritage and eventually causing all their work and businesses to shut down is certainly a flagrant violation to all concluded agreements not to mention to simple human rights.

12.

The construction of the separation wall will virtually transform Rachel�s Tomb area into a ghetto. Around 500 Bethlehem citizens will accordingly be required to obtain a permission to enter or leave their own houses and lands. No visitors will be allowed. No freedom of movement is given.

13.

The wall will affect the whole northern part of Bethlehem which accommodates factories, souvenir shops, restaurants, churches, Islamic Waqf building, the sole baby hospital in town and many houses inhabited by around 4000 people. It will prevent Bethlehem from the only remaining zone for its natural future growth. Furthermore, the isolation, harassments and pressure anticipated by the execution of the above tyrannical order, is feared to drive more and more of our citizens to emigrate.

14.

In addition to cutting interconnection between Bethlehem and the rest of the Palestinian cities, in particular with Jerusalem, the separation wall, this massive obstruction at the main entrance of Bethlehem will decimate tourism our major source of revenue and will strangle the whole economy of our town resulting in a dramatic increase to the already high unemployment and poverty.

15.

The ironic thing is that this wall will be constructed in the name of security for the few Jewish worshippers who visit Rachel�s Tomb. It should be mentioned that the holy site has never witnessed any violent act or attack against worshippers. Throughout history Palestinians have safeguarded the Tomb and guaranteed free worshipping in it.

16.

More ironically, this wall was not judged necessary during 28 years of Israeli occupation to Bethlehem and 7 years of the first Intifada.

17.

The Tomb after all, has been a place for heavy Israeli military presence since 1993. It is the Israelis who assume responsibility for the security at the site. The erection of the wall is therefore completely unjustified.

18.

While initiatives are being launched to get the Israelis and Palestinians back to the negotiating table, the above oppressive practices evidently do not help in building bridges of trust nor do they serve the cause of peace in our region.

19.

This is an urgent and loud appeal to all people of good will in the world to oppose this order, to interfere and to prevent the execution of the wall.

20.

Please stop this aggression and spare our Holy Town this catastrophic threat. Bethlehem needs neither ghettos nor new Berlin Walls. It needs respect of its rights and lands. It needs opening of its borders and enlarging its main entrance rather than closure and confiscation of land.

                                                                    Hanna J. Nasser

Mayor of Bethlehm
