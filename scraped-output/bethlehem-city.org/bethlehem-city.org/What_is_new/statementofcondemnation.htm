Statement of condemnation
of the Israeli Government decision to
annex Rachel�s Tomb

The Israeli government announced that the security cabinet decided in its session held on Wednesday September 11th 2002 to endorse setting up a  �security envelope� around Jerusalem to include Rachel�s Tomb situated in the northern outskirt of Bethlehem and to keep it under total Israeli control.  The decision stated that the cabinet has authorized a number of military commanders and the Mayor of West Jerusalem who attended the session to discuss the way of building a new road which the Prime Minister approved its construction to link Jerusalem with Rachel�s Tomb area for the sake of ensuring secure arrival of Jews to the Holy Site.

The Municipal Council of Bethlehem took this serious tyrannical decision with disapproval and denunciation.  The council deemed that the occupation has no right to separate Rachel�s Tomb area which is located within the town�s municipal borders and constitutes 25% of its approved master plan from Bethlehem.  Furthermore it represents a vital residential area inhabited by 3000 citizens. It is considered the most important development area and the sole remaining zone for the town�s demographic and economical growth.  It contains essential residential, tourist, industrial and health utilities in addition to Islamic and Christian religious places.  It is the only tourist passage for the town.  This unjust decision is regarded as outrageous aggression on the municipal borders of Bethlehem, on the town�s northern lands, on Bilal Ben Rabah Mosque and on the Islamic Cemetery.  It is an encroachment on international legitimacy and a new flagrant violation to the concluded agreements between the Israelis and the Palestinians and to all international conventions.

Bethlehem Municipality as it strongly condemns this decision of the Israeli government, it calls upon both the Christian and the Islamic worlds and the various international bodies to interfere and pressure the Israeli government to cancel its oppressive decision which blows up the peace process and stability in the region rather than serving them.

Bethlehem Municipal Council
