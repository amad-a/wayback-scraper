Bethlehem Chamber of Commerce & Industry

Efforts to establish a chamber of commerce in Bethlehem began during the British mandate. During the Jordanian Rule and on March 30, 1952 a preliminary committee was formed to set the laws and regulations for the establishment of a Chamber of Commerce in Bethlehem. On April, 6 1952 the preliminary committee called for a meeting whereby the first Council for the chamber was elected by the merchants and traders of the three towns, Bethlehem, Beit Jala, and Beit Sahour. In 1965 elections for a new council took place. The responsibility for the newly elected members during the Occupation was limited, being confined to the collection of membership fees and to certain specific transactions and formalities.

In 1971 the Chairman of the Council resigned and the late Mayor Mr. Elias Freij, was elected as Chairman, a position which he accepted in addition to the responsibilities of his office as Mayor of Bethlehem. In October 1998 the Minister of Trade and Economy in the Palestinian Authority nominated a new council and approved the appointment of Mr. Munther Bandak as Chairman. The council consisted of twelve members five of whom were new members. In October 1999 the Minister of Trade and Economics appointed a new council of ten new members chaired by Dr. Sameer Hazboun.

The main current objectives of the Chamber are:

To promote and protect the interests of the merchants, the industrialists and the business people in the Bethlehem District.

To provide information and help to the public on a day to day basis.

To enlighten its members as to Import and Export regulations, Customs laws, and provide legal and technical advice pertinent to business and trade matters.

To issue a journal periodically whereby the merchants and traders in Bethlehem area would be briefed about the activities and achievements of the Chamber.

To emphasize the importance of the traditional crafts of Bethlehem such as olive wood and mother-of-pearl industry.

To organize training courses in Business Management in order to raise the standard, and update the knowledge of merchants and traders in the area.

To establish contacts with chambers of Commerce in different countries of the world. 
The Bethlehem Chamber of Commerce will in the near future move to a new location in the southern part of the town, better suited for the work of the Chamber and equipped with up-to-date facilities.
