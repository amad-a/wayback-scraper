DISTRIBUTION NETWORK

The original network was laid by Ramallah El-Bireh Water Co. in the early 1950s. The pipes used, were not according to standard specifications, and pipes above 2 inch in diameter were second hand. The laying of pipes was usually done for individual consumers and not for entire streets or areas. Most pipes were laid above ground and in privately owned land thus, making the pipes exposed to damage and breakage. In some cases, the consumers were allowed to purchase and install their own pipes. All this, resulted in a confused network with high losses and costly upkeep and maintenance.

JWU - Distribution Network

Additionally, JWU took over the inoperative and obsolete networks of Der Dibwan, Biteen and Kafr Malik which further impeded proper performance of water supply and created more difficulties for JWU.

At present, JWU uses galvanized steel pipes 3/4 - 2 inches in diameter, threaded at both ends and are joined by threaded joints in accordance with the ISO medium, BS 1387 medium DIN 2440 standards. It also uses 3 - 16 inches in diameter pipes, with cement lining and coated by two layers of asphalt and fiber glass or by plastic wrapping to protect the pipes against corrosion and increase the service lifetime. These pipes are joined together by welding as per IS 530, BS 534 standards.

Due to lack of adequate funds, JWU was not able to expand its distribution network to cover un-built sites or newly planned zones in anticipation of connecting future buildings. JWU can only connect pre-paying consumers taking into consideration the possibility of future development in the particular area in as far as what size of pipes to use when connecting the area.

PLANNED ACTIONS

1. Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs). Incorrect, laying of pipes, installation of manholes, and maintenance of meters have been reported. JWU cannot ensure the proper carrying out of technical activities without the existence of SOPs. The development of these standard procedures should be assigned to consultants and to a JWU engineer who will be instructed to work very closely with the technical personnel who conduct these activities. All new recruits should be trained on these SOPs and upon the introduction of the SOPs all existing personnel should be retrained based upon the SOPs.

2. Following the implementation of the SOPs, JWU should consider implementing some form of reporting procedures following maintenance and other technical activities. Reports should include indications of labor and time required to execute the activity. These reports can be subsequently used to estimate the cost of these activities (to be used in the pricing of the activities) and for performance evaluation of the employees.

3. Maintenance Programs for distribution network and pumps. The implementation of Preventive Maintenance (PM) on the distribution network and pumping stations will probably greatly reduce leakage and break down of pumps. Maintenance cards should be prepared and a computer system developed to keep up with the scheduled maintenance activities.

This program will also provide in the future reports on the performance history of each item maintained. The implementation of GIS and a Leak Detection Unit (acting as an inspection team) will support the implementation of PM.

4. Classification of contractors. Information on many of the contractors with which JWU deals will be gathered and rated. Prior to inviting any contractor to participate in a tender, it is recommended that the contractor be required to fill out a special form.

5. Establish a system to test and license pipe fitters. In order to regulate this profession and keep up the work standards at the premises of the public.

6. Coordinate with the Engineers Association. To establish clear guidelines when planning new buildings and to indicate clearly the locations of the interior pipes, tanks and possible location of the meters taking into account, the Undertaking's rules and regulations and safety factors.

7. Establishment of the Geographic Information System ( GIS ).

This system will include Computer Hardware, Software, Plotter, Scanner Digitizing Equipment and Employee's Training, etc. The mapping system in JWU is primitive since maps are updated manually. There is a need for a system to store and retrieve maps as needed and JWU is actively seeking funding for this system.
