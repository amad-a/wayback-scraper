January 20th, 2003

 

APPEAL  FOR  URGENT  AID

          A new year has commenced and the heavy clouds of destitution and frustration hanging over Bethlehem hang lower and darker.

            The town of the Nativity has been whimpering under a grave economical burden for the last twenty months and the situation is rapidly deteriorating.  With the continuous tight closure that prohibits freedom of movement and with the recurring Israeli incursions into Bethlehem, the town today suffers from a state of economical suffocation, devastation to both public and private property and a standstill to most aspects of life.  Curfew, however, is aggravating the repercussions of this tragedy on Bethlehem as its 28,000 citizens are forced to endure prolonged periods of house arrest.

            The Israeli aggressive practices and collective punishments have crippled trade, agriculture, education, services and transformed the vast majority of our workers to jobless.  Unemployment in Bethlehem soared to 65%.  Tourism, once a major source of revenue to our people, is dead and the Church of Nativity, the oldest church on earth is desolated.  Suffice it to say that more than 70 % of the Bethlehemites live today under the poverty line.  All of their savings were drained.  The cost of basic food for children like milk and bread is out of most families reach.

            This situation reflected dramatically on Bethlehem Municipality whose meager budget depends basically on the taxes and fees collected from these families.  The Municipality is facing a dire financial crisis that has affected our capability to provide essential municipal services.  As we struggle to help our citizens survive at these critical times, the deficit at the municipality in light of lack of resources is quickly exceeding 1 million dollars threatening the very existence of this 131 year old reputed national institution.

            On behalf of our municipal council and all people of our Holy Town, I appeal to you in the strongest possible terms to extend emergent monetary aid to enable us withstand this intolerable situation.  Your support is badly needed to alleviate our peopleï¿½s suffering at this time of urgent need.  

            I believe Bethlehem, the birthplace of the prince of peace and a cradle for civilization deserves immediate attention, solidarity and support from the whole international community.
