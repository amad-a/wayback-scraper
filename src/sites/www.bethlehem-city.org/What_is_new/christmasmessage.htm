Date: December 23rd 2002  

Christmas Message

By the Mayor of Bethlehem Hanna J. Nasser

 

Christmas is approaching and once again Bethlehem, the capital of Christmas is denied the joy and peace this Holy occasion carries with it.

For us in Bethlehem, Christmas has an extremely important and incomparable significance for it was in our little town that our Lord & Savior Jesus Christ was born and it was in the sky of Bethlehem that the angels proclaimed his eternal message of peace:

� Glory to God in the highest and on Earth peace, good will towards men�

The Town of the Nativity today is burdened with big suffering. With the tense political situation prevailing in the Holy Land, the Israeli forces mounted four incursions into the town during this year.  The result was loss of beloved ones, damage to public and private property, misery and disruption of normal life. The town is under curfew since November 22nd.

Like the Holy Family who did not find a place overnight, there are people in Bethlehem today who have no place to spend the nights, after their homes were destroyed by the shelling.  The town is besieged and its people are confined in what is similar to a large prison.  Bethlehem has paid a painful heavy price for the recent Israeli Security Cabinet�s decision to annex Rachel Tomb area to Jerusalem boundaries.  More than 3000 acres of Bethlehem land have been either confiscated or access to them was denied to create the so-called security envelope. Economically the situation is desperate with an unprecedented high rate of employment.  The Israeli aggression has seriously deteriorated the living conditions of our people and drained all of their savings.  It has stolen the joy from the hearts and the smile from our children�s lips.

At this difficult time, we look at Christmas as an occasion to draw the world�s attention to the suffering and hardships faced by so many in Bethlehem: the aggrieved, the injured, the oppressed, the unemployed, the homeless, the desperate the traumatized children and to extend a hand of support to them at this time of urgent need.

Christmas is the time for love and reconciliation.  It renews hope in our hearts.  Hope that repression and injustice would stop.  Hope that this Holy Land and its people would enjoy justice and peace one day.  We pray for the prince of peace to bestow his grace upon the land he has blessed.

From the shadow of the Church of Nativity, I urge all Christians, despite this difficult situation to have the courage and to visit Bethlehem to celebrate the true splendor of Christmas. Bethlehem is the cradle of Christianity.  It deserves all remembrance, care and support.  As descendants of the poor Christmas shepherds, we shall always keep expecting to receive many new wise men in Bethlehem, bearing precious gifts but above all justice, freedom and peace that our people have been kept waiting for too long.

I wish you all a Merry Christmas and a peaceful blessed new year.

 

 Hanna  J.  Nasser

      Mayor of  Bethlehem
