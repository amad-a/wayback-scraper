  February 19th, 2003 

 

PRESS  RELEASE

New Getto in Bethlehem

 

In a serious development to the tyrannical decision of the Israeli Security Cabinet of Sept 11th, 2002 to keep Rachel�s Tomb area under permanent Israeli control, the Israeli military authorities have delivered on February 16th, 2003 to the landlords in that area an order issued by the Israeli Defence Force (Order No. 03/14/T) in which they announce taking possession  of their properties for military purposes.

          According to the plan attached to the order, an 8-10 meter high concrete wall will be constructed in the middle of the main road at the entrance of Bethlehem from the military check-point until the Tomb area surrounding the Holy Site with the neighboring buildings and lands. See Map

 :

          The ironic thing is that this wall will be constructed in the name of security for the Jewish worshippers who visit Rachel�s Tomb.  Throughout history, Palestinians have safeguarded the Tomb and guaranteed free and safe worshiping of Jews in it.  After all, the Tomb has been a place for heavy Israeli military presence since 1993.  It is the Israelis who assume responsibility for security at the site which makes the construction of the wall completely unjustified.

          The construction of this wall will virtually transform Rachel�s Tomb area into a ghetto requiring permission for more than 500 residents to enter or leave their own houses and lands while no visitors are allowed in, violating the simple human rights and in contradiction with the Oslo Accord article 7 a (1) on the subject which clearly states �the free movement of Palestinians on the main road will continue.�  

It will deprive these residents from their properties and cause their work and businesses to shutdown.  I am genuinely afraid that the whole northern part of Bethlehem that contains factories, souvenir shops, restaurants, churches, Islamic Waqf building, a baby hospital and houses inhabited by around 4000 people will be segregated from the town and kept under isolation and intolerable pressure, eventually driving more and more to emigrate.

          The disastrous effect however is that this wall will strangle the economy of Bethlehem and cut interconnection between our town and the rest of the Palestinian cities, in particular with Jerusalem. With a huge obstruction built at the middle of the main and the only road between Jerusalem and Bethlehem, tourism which is the major source of revenue for our citizens will certainly drop dramatically causing the already high unemployment to increase.

          I believe such oppressive practices do not help in building bridges of trust nor do they serve the cause of peace in our region.

          It behooves all people of good will to oppoe them, prevent their execution and to spare our Holy Town this catastrophic threat.

 

 

                                                                     Hanna J. Nasser

                                                                  Mayor of Bethlehm
