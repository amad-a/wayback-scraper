Conference on the Role of the European Commission in
the Middle East Peace Process
(Brussels: 12,13 November 1998)

Dr. Eyad El-Sarraj participated in the conference on "The Role of the European Commission in the Middle East Peace Process" organized by the European Socialists Party on 12 and 13 November.  He talked about the responsibility of EC countries to actively support the Middle East peace process, especially in view of the Israeli Government efforts to abort peace.  Dr. El-Sarraj stressed European liability stating that Europe tortured, killed, and exiled Jews; then, it helped them build a national homeland in Palestine at the expense of the original inhabitants of the land.  Thus Europe has a moral responsibility to help the victim's victim.  In addition, this supports European geographical, economic, and refugee policies.

In addition, Dr. El-Sarraj stressed the importance of cultural exchange between Europe on the one hand and Palestine and Arab countries on the other.  He also said that if Europe's only concern is the Jews it should save Israel from its current government.  Dr. El-Sarraj also expressed his surprise at the European position in light of the vast financial resources that it is investing in the region.  He wondered if Europe was lacking leadership, unity, or will?

Dr. El-Sarraj also inquired as to how Europe could support a country such as Israel, in which the High Court sanctions and legalizes torturing Palestinians.  How can Europe finance some PA security branches that continuously violate human rights?

With regard to the "Wye Plantation Agreement", Dr. El-Sarraj expressed his concern that the implementation of the agreement will be a prelude to establishing a police state controlled by the military.  He also voiced his reservation about CIA involvement in the region in light of its long history of ties with dictatorial military regimes.
