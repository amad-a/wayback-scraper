Dr. Eyad El-Sarraj participates in a “Health and Human Rights” conference in Sweden

On May 15 and 16, Dr. Eyad El-Sarraj participated in an International Conference on “Health and Human Rights” in Stockholm, Sweden. The conference was organized by several Scandinavian human rights organizations including Amnesty International, Physicians for Human Rights, and others caring for the rights of refugees, asylum seeker, and torture survivors. 250 human rights activists from Scandinavia and Europe participated.

Dr. El-Sarraj gave a paper on “Palestinian Human Rights and Mental Health” pointing to the fiftieth anniversary of the State of Israel and the Palestinian “Nakba” (catastrophe) and uprooting. He stressed that the cycle of violence in the Middle East started in Europe with the persecution of Jews, who directed their hatred and frustration on a new victim (the Palestinian people). Moreover, he talked about the systematic Israeli policy of humiliation, torture, and destruction that has stripped children of feelings of safety and security and, thus, continuing the cycle of violence between the two peoples as well as within each individual community. In addition, Dr. El-Sarraj indicated that the Israelis tortured about two hundred thousand Palestinians; and that, today, thirty thousand still suffer the effects of torture and pose a problem to society.

In his presentation, Dr. El-Sarraj called upon people working in the field of rehabilitating torture survivors to raise their voice to stop all forms of torture and violence before it occurs. He also encouraged the conference participants to call upon Israel to stop its policy of settlement building, house demolition, torture, and oppression.

Dr. El-Sarraj stated that peace is the only solution to the Israeli-Arab conflict. However, peace must be based on justice, the guarantee of Palestinian rights, and the establishment of a Palestinian State.

In addition to participating in the conference, Dr. El-Sarraj took part in a rally for solidarity with Palestinians organized by the Palestinian Solidarity Association in Sweden in the main square in Stockholm.
