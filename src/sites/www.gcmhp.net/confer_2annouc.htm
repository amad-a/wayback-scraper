The International Association for the Study of Forced Migration (IASFM) will convene the sixth International Research and Advisory Panel Conference on Forced Migration (IRAP) in Gaza from 13-16 December 1998. As the fifth IRAP in Eldoret, Kenya, the conference is being hosted in a focal area of forced migration. It will take place at Rashad El-Shawa Cultural Center in Gaza, Palestine.

The International Association for the Study of Forced Migration (IASFM) is an independent and self-governing community of scholars and practitioners who are concerned about understanding forced migration and about improving the formulation of policies and administration of programs dealing with refugees and other displaced persons. We are also an open collegial community, receptive to meeting with and learning from others.

This International Association evolved during the 1990s. The precursor organization was the International Research and Advisory Panel on Refugees and Other Displaced Persons (IRAP), which was created to advise the Refugee Studies Programme at Oxford and the Journal of Refugee Studies. Participants at the first IRAP meeting in January 1990 tried to define a research agenda for refugee studies, and we discussed forming an "independent international think-tank on refugee policy and practice" that could utilize and merge the insights and perspectives of both scholars and practitioners.

Succeeding IRAP meetings in 1991, 1992, and 1994 all combined general analyses and state-of-the-art papers with reporting on developments in the world and field. However, each meeting was larger; the panel meeting had become a conference; and the subject matter had expanded from refugees to encompass the larger arena of forced migration. By 1994 the body of scholars and practitioners who had been attending IRAP meetings had formed a self-sustaining community and continuity and believed that it was time to delink ourselves from our Oxford hosts and become an independent organization.

The International Association was voted into existence at that 1994 meeting. Its primary mandate is to organize IRAP conferences, the meeting place for our community. In addition to sponsoring formal meetings (or co-sponsoring them with local hosts), the Association also wants to establish and strengthen international and interactive networks of scholars and practitioners who are involved in the global issues of forced migration. We are truly international in scope, membership, and leadership, and we invite people from around the world to join us in our search for understanding and for ways to improve the lives of forced migrants and affected hosts.
We are not simply an academic organization. Our deliberations and exchanges also shape global debates, policies, and programs. We are committed to coming to areas of the world where forced migration is a pressing concern, interacting with people in different world regions, and learning from (and teaching) each other. The first IRAP sponsored by the IASFM was in Kenya in 1996, and the next will be this December in Gaza. We invite you to join us there.

In response to our first announcement and call for papers, the IASFM program committee received more than 100 abstracts of presentations for the 6th IRAP from researchers all over the world. The committee is currently setting up the detailed program. A preliminary draft is given below.

General Assembly of the IASFM 
 
 
If you are interested as scholar, fieldworker, person involved in policies or public relations with regard to forced migration, or as researcher, you are cordially invited to join the 6th IRAP and to participate in the conference's discussions.

The host of the 6th IRAP, the Gaza Community Mental Health Programme, is a Palestinian non-governmental institution, established in 1990. GCMHP aims to offer comprehensive community-based mental health services. Research and training constitute an important part of GCMHP. Numerous research projects have been completed and many are still being carried out. The GCMHP hosted several international conferences such as the Third International Conference on "Health and Human Rights" in October 1997. For details, please visit http://www.gcmhp.net.

The 6th IRAP will take place at Rashad El-Shawa Cultural Center. All foreign participants will be accommodated in local hotels in Gaza. Regular transportation for participants from Tel Aviv airport to Gaza and back to Tel Aviv including local transportation will be guaranteed. For those who reserve a full package, food will be available to them in the conference hall and at dinner. Local tours to refugee camps and archeological sites will be organized daily for those interested to get first hand information about the living conditions of Palestinians, and understand more about the Palestinian society. Social evenings will be organized for conference participants including music and Palestinian folklore.

Please note that December will be winter in Gaza; so participants should also bring warm clothes with them. The temperatures usually range from 10 - 15 degrees Celsius at that time. Gaza is very safe and quiet place, people are friendly, and we hope participants will enjoy a very pleasant stay and will get to know more about the Palestinian society and culture.
