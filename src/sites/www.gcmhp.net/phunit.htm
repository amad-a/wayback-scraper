PHYSIOTHERAPY FOR EXPOLITICAL PRISONERS
 
 
As part of the Project for the Rehabilitation of Victims of Human Rights Violations, the GCMHP broke new ground in 1998 by pioneering the junction of mental health and physiotherapy.  The GCMHP Physiotherapy Unit manages cases of ex-political prisoners with physical disabilities due to torture and conducts public awareness campaigns about the physical consequences of torture.  Integrated into the three GCMHP clinics, the physiotherapist and assistant physiotherapist provide manual treatment (massage, hot-pack, and cold-pack), ultrasound treatment, and paraffin oil baths to patients with stiffness or weakness of the extremities, pain in the lower back or neck, or tendon problems.  Understanding the interdependence of the physical and mental effects of torture, the GCMHP hopes that the treatment of patientsâ€™ physical suffering will facilitate the treatment of their psychological suffering.
