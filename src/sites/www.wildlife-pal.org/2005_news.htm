Palestine Wildlife Society.

Activities of the year 2005.

 

I. Summer and winter Environmental Awareness Centers.

1.      Holding the 5th winter camp in Jericho in cooperation with the Directorate of Education of Jericho from 10-13/Jan /05. The activities included a tour of the Botanic Garden, and visits to Hisham Palace and Qruntol Mountain. About 30 students, girls and boys, participated in this activity.

 

2.      Holding the 6th summer camp in Hebron district in cooperation with the Palestinian Child for Arts in Hebron. The activities lasted for one week and they included lectures about environmental awareness, the concept of ‘school clubs’ for the protection of nature, the importance of water in nature, and garbage and its affect on our environment. The camp carried out field visits to Hebron municipality; water springs in the city, Solomon’s Pools, Artas village, and the old city in Bethlehem including the Nativity Church. Many environmental activities were carried out by the students, mainly about garbage and water who recognized the natural places and their antiquities in Palestine. Some environmental films were shown about birds and biodiversity. About 30 students participated in these activities.

 

3.      Holding the 7th summer camp in Bethlehem district. The activities included lectures about environmental awareness, the concepts and activities of the school clubs for the protection of nature, and the importance of water in nature. The participants visited water well in Duheisha Camp, and Beit Jala municipality where they exchanged ideas about garbage with its Mayor. They also carried out some field activities such as environmental surveying of garbage and water. Some environmental films were shown about birds and biodiversity.

 

4.      Holding the 8th summer camp in Ramallah district. About 33 students participated in its activities that belonged to the program of the school id the friend of the environment. The camp was held in Alqiqab garden in Al Bireh, and the activities included lectures about garbage, biodiversity, and the concepts of nature protection and water. A visit to the Al Bireh water purification center was carried out to understand its workings, as well as a field trip to Beit Ello station. Some environmental films about birds and biodiversity were shown.

 

II. Field visits to the Botanic Garden" Jericho Wildlife Monitoring Station". 

1.      A delegation of about 35 educational and environmental health managers from the Ministry of Education from all the districts of Palestine visited the Botanic Garden "Jericho Wildlife Monitoring Station" on April 20th to understand the benefits of the center. The researchers Anton Khaliliaillah and Samer Emmeiya performed the process of ringing the birds and talked about the importance of the nature of Palestine for birds and animals living in the area.

2.      A field and scientific visit was made to the Botanic Garden "Jericho Wildlife Monitoring Station", with school principals and educational supervisors from Bethlehem and Hebron directorates of education. They toured the garden and recognized its benefits. They also watched the process of ringing some birds and listened to a detailed lecture about the importance of Palestine for migratory birds.

3.      Some school students (members of the school clubs from Bethlehem district) did voluntary work in the Botanic Garden "Jericho Wildlife Monitoring Station"by clearing rubbish.

4.      A field trip to Jericho was made by girls from the environmental school clubs at the Aida primary school. They visited Qruntol Mountain and the Botanic Garden.

5.      Some international delegations were received and welcomed to the Botanic Garden "Jericho Wildlife Monitoring Station". The first delegation was from the American Consulate (USAID), the second from the American group “Tree of life”, and the third delegation was from the Japanese Embassy. They were introduced to the activities of the center.

6.      Many photos of the migratory and soaring birds were also taken.

VIII. Field Environmental Researches.

1.      A follow-up of the work and activities of the Botanic Garden was made. The ringing of the birds continued and a survey of the visiting winter birds was also made.

2.      Our field researcher Anton Khaliliyah was able to catch and examine a bird named the Iranian hide. This bird has only been caught twice before in Palestine; once in the1920s and again in the 1970s.

3.      Ringing and monitoring the visiting winter birds continued. Many technical photos were taken and we were able to register some rare birds such as the Reed Bunting and the Spectacled Speckled? Warbler in the Botanic Garden "Jericho Wildlife Monitoring Station".

4.      A Wildlife survey together with the monitoring process started for the spring migratory birds, on 15/2/05.

5.      Create the data Base for the Flora and Fauna of Palestine.

6.      The volunteer Samer Emmaya took part in the bird ringing in Jericho in the spring time.

7.      Field visits and tours were made to Saint Saba area, Arab Rashaida, Wadi Foukin, Beit Ello, and Umsafa and Nabi Saleh forests. An environmental survey was made together with the monitoring process of some Important Bird Areas. Our researchers were able to register, watch and photograph some rare birds for the first time.

8.      An environmental survey was made for some Raptors birds nesting in Qruntol Mountain, Wadi Quilt and Wadi Nueima in Jericho.

9.      Our researchers carried out some ringing of birds in Al Bireh district, while visiting the Environmental Quality Authority in Ramallah. They also presented some detailed explanations about this process and the importance of these birds.

10.  The Egyptian Mongoose was released in the Beit Sahour area after being handed to us by the Chemical Drugs Company employees in Beit Jala.

11.  As part of the campaign against illegal hunting, our executive manager and the police department in Bethlehem district confiscated a number of endangered wild birds, such as the Shocker and their eggs. They also rescued and released some soaring birds. They informed some traders that it was forbidden to buy and sell such birds.

12.  A visit to the Jerusalem Wilderness was made (Wadi Daraja area and "Ras naqab ElHumar "east of Bethlehem and Hebron), where we monitored some migratory birds.

13.  A 275 bird species was recorded in Jericho Wildlife Monitoring Station during the survey.

 

IX. Establishing and Forming Committees.

1.      A meeting was held on 11/4/05 in our offices with 65 hunters, and we discussed with them the importance of organizing the hunting in order to maintain our natural wealth. We also referred to the endangered species so that they could be saved and kept safe in their nests. They formed a committee and interested people could apply for membership.

2.      Following the Cucumber exhibition held in Beit Sahour last summer, we formed a committee of farmers from Beit Sahour to develop agriculture in the town.

3.      The meetings with the Environmental Quality Authority in Ramalla continued. PWLS is the main member of this committee.

 

X. Festivals and Environmental Ceremonies.  

1.      Celebrating Tree Day in cooperation with the Ministry of Agriculture and the Municipalities of Beit Sahour and Bethlehem on 26/4/05 under the patronage of the Minister of Agriculture. The ceremony was held in the park of the city during which some trees were planted.

2.      The first spring exhibition was held in Jericho on 30/3/05 in cooperation with the Directorate of Education in Jericho district. During the ceremony some entertaining activities were presented by the students of the school clubs, such as the Dabka (traditional dancing), Palestinian folklore and songs.

3.      Celebrating the second spring exhibition in Bethlehem area  on 21/3/05  in Finiq Centre hall in Duheisha Camp in cooperation with the Ministry of Education in Bethlehem district. The ceremony included several activities such as some songs and the Dabka. All these activities were presented by the students of the school clubs in the districts.

4.      The second Cucumber Exhibition was held in Beit Sahour. PWLS and the Municipality of Beit Sahour organized this celebration in cooperation with the Agricultural Relief Committees and the Centre of Maintaining the Cultural Heritage. The ceremony included some Dabka, and other activities presented by the boy scouts and girl guides in the city. Workshops for the children and for the women were carried out and a final report of the ceremony was presented by Bethlehem TV.

5.      Some voluntary works were organized in the International Clearing up Day on 26/9/05 in cooperation with the municipality of Beit Sahour to clear rubbish in Beit Sahour and Jericho. The Minister of Tourism Mr. Ziad AlBandak attended this activity.

6.      Celebrating the Environmental Tourism in the World on 17/9/05 in cooperation with the Ministry of Tourism in Jericho. The celebration focused on the task of clearing rubbish in some areas in Jericho. A clearing campaign was launched on 18/9/05. The ceremony was held under the patronage of the Minister of Tourism and Antiquities Mr. Al-Bandak.
