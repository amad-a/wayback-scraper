The Story of the Stolen Country

Once upon a time in a land called Palestine (originally the land of Canaan), the Palestinian people (Christians, Muslims, and a few Jews) dwelled happily. One day, out of nowhere, outside intruders started to flock down claiming their right to the land. Soon, these ravenous intruders started appropriating the land by force, until the aboriginal people of Palestine found themselves denied the right to live on their own soil.

Shunned by their neighbours and by the world, the extent of misery that the Palestinians have had to endure in their lives cannot be expressed in words. If the pain the Palestinians have had to undergo could be represented by tears, it would be enough to inundate the whole planet. Until today, both muslim and christian Palestinians cannot reclaim what is theirs--the few Palestinian jews, betrayed their people and sided with the intruders. With the enormous aid supplied to the intruders by the world, the chances of the Palestinians liberating their land seem bleak, but perhaps one should keep some hope. Who knows, maybe one day the scale will tip in the other direction and Palestine will flourish again. Although tyranny and injustice appear to be perpetual at the present, "there is an end to all evil".

Many lies and fabricated stories have been devised in order to justify the intruders' position, but the truth cannot be circumvented; it is as bright as sunlight for those who really want to see it.

If someone knocks on your door, pulls a gun on you and tells you that "God" promised him/her your house, and forces you to get out of your OWN HOME, how would you feel?
