Summary of Activities :1st July 1997- 31st December 1997
 
 

Out-service training

A. Mental Health nursing course:

This course started in the beginning of April 1997, an agreement GCMHP and the faculty of education was reached to train 15 male and female nurses who have BA. In nursing and work in clinics and hospitals in the Gaza Strip. The course lasted for 6 months with average of 175 hours.

B. Kinder Garden Teachers Course

This course was conducted for teachers working in kinder garten. The number of participants was 15 women for 3 months. Members of HRT conducted lectures in this course.

C. Counseling Course
In cooperation with Beir Zeit University, an 8 months a training course for counselors started on December 1997. The number of participants was 20. A member of HRT conducted lectures in this course.

Changes in the project frame work.

There were no changes in the project frame work, however political situation in the country is getting worse due to difficulties and restrictions in the implementation of peace accord. The Israeli government still dictating the Palestinian Authority to detain those who oppose peace agreement? As a result hundreds of Palestinians still captured by Palestinian Authority. The question of Palestinian prisoners in the Israeli prisons is still one of the most difficult problem being discussed by Israeli and Palestinian delegations.

 Institutional support provided
 

Fourteen visits have been delivered to different institutions for the purpose of cooperation and networking. As a result some torture victims have been referred for rehabilitation in GCMHP clinics, but to lack of well organized referral system in our data base system, the number of those cases were not documented. This issue well is tackled in the new data information system, which will be ready and implemented in 1998.

We will be able to evaluate the effects of those meetings on general public through a new data base program, which is in process of preparation for this purpose.

Media activities

10 different articles have been published in newspaper interviews. Issues tackled were related to certain phenomena in Palestinian society (e.g., torture and its effects, domestic violence and crime in Palestinian society, and rehabilitation of human rights victims.
