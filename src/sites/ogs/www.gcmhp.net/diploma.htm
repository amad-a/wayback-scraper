Postgraduate Diploma in Community Mental Health

 
1. Broad Aims and objectives of the Diploma
The Postgraduate Diploma In Community Mental Health is a two-year course that is jointly run by the Islamic University of Gaza and Gaza Community Mental Health Programme. Candidates who successfully complete the course will be awarded a Diploma in Applied Community Mental Health. It combines both theory and clinical aspects of community mental health and provides candidates with a broad knowledge base. The curriculum is culturally sensitive, community oriented, and critically analyzes the concepts of human rights and mental health. Seven International Universities are contributing to the diploma: Edinburgh University-UK, Flinders University-Australia, Oslo University-Norway, University of California at San Francisco-USA, Tel Aviv University-Israel, Tunis University-Tunisia, Utrecht University-the Netherlands.
  • It is designed to encourage the candidates to explore theoretical concepts in community mental health and human rights in addition to their practical applications. Wide exposure to clinical experience enables them to discover their particular interest in mental health, while learning about different areas and how to integrate them in clinical practice and research.
  •  It is an attempt to break away from the medical model and its tendency to focus on microscopic aspects of mental health. Instead, it hopes to place the study of mental health within the wider social, political, environmental and biological context. The course further seeks ways to shift psychiatric therapy away from traditional Freudian concentration on the individual and his unconscious motivational conflicts to move closer towards an emphasis on the social interpersonal care and the role of a person’s environment.
  • The course is placed firmly within the philosophy of the community-based approach that emphasizes the shift from segregated and self-contained mental health care systems within institutions to community-based therapy and community involvement in health related issues. It also emphasizes the change in the role of mental health professionals from being providers of mental health care to facilitators of care; thus enabling individuals to solve their problems using the resources of the family and community institutions such as schools, mosques, etc.
  • The course aims to equip students with real skills, help the transition from theoretical class-based study to skilled and practical application within the community.
  • No course in mental health would be complete without analysis of health human rights and the role that they play within the community. The course therefore hopes to expose students to notions of human rights and to highlight the links between these concepts.
  • Candidates are expected to combine learning from their clinical work with information obtained from the Diploma. They will have access to the GCMHP library and computers in addition to the Islamic University library. Key reference articles will be copied and distributed prior to lectures. A general reading list will be available at the end of each module.
2. General structure of the Diploma
2.1 The Curriculum
The curriculum is culturally sensitive and community oriented. It avoids the danger of imposing an inflexible categorical structure on particular problems when such categories may result in fallacies: a danger that has been evident when western methods of assessment and treatment were used to train local mental health workers with minimum consideration to local cultural problems. The curriculum is based on the principles of collaborative adult learning with an interest in emancipatory knowledge that allows the students to avoid any conflict or separation between theory and practice.
The Diploma is full time, and students will be recruited into training posts. These posts will require that students participate in the study schedule of the diploma as well as selected training activities of the GCMHP. At the commencement their first year, the students will be allocated to one of the GCMHP teams. They will also be assigned a clinical supervisor who will be responsible for their schedule when they are not attending classes. Each student will be required to undertake tasks allocated to them and they will be required to complete a predetermined number of hours of community/clinical work per academic year.
They will be taught by international teachers at intervals of between one and three weeks to teach these modules in collaboration with local trainers. The local trainers will be available to supplement the training in between visits. Each module taught in the first year has a specific international and local trainer selected according to professional background and expertise. The second year is a community/clinical year and the students will have a series of supervised placements in a variety of mental health settings.

There will be a variety of teaching methods used throughout the diploma including: formal lectures, seminars, tutorials, student presentations, journal clubs, role-play, video work and supervised community and clinical practice. There will be a similar range of assessment tools plus a final student evaluation.

Each year is divided into two semesters of 16 weeks each, and a total of ten core modules will be taught in the first year. Presently these modules are:

Core modules

Child adolescent mental health

This module aims to give the participants general knowledge of normal child development, the normal pathological development, and the different theories applied to development. It also makes the participants conscious of social and family impact on child health and development, and of notions of children's rights.
Social Psychology
The Social Psychology module highlights the main topics related to social perception and cognition, attitude to change, helping behavior, social influence, group dynamics, and the environment and social behavior.
Clinical Psychiatry
This module clarifies the main psychiatric disorders with the emphasis on adult psychiatry. The participants will be able to recognize the different therapeutic approaches and their indications. The classifications of mental disorders and the etiology and mental state examination will be clarified.
Communication and Counseling
This module aims to make the participants aware of their own interpersonal behavior, develop an understanding of successful communication techniques, the barriers to communication and introduce the participants to different counseling approaches.
Culture and Mental Health
This module gives participants a clear picture about the Palestinian culture, religion, and their utilization in mental health care. The participants will be able to define the healthy aspects of the Palestinian culture and how to utilize them in mental health. In addition, they will be able to understand the psychology of oppression and racism and its influences. They will also consider human rights issues within the field of mental health, with reference to Palestinian history.
Trauma, Stress and Coping
This module aims to give students a general background about the basic individual and collective reactions to trauma and the indications of emotional disturbances. In addition, it will analyze the role of professionals in dealing with survivors of severe trauma and relevant human rights issues.
Clinical Psychology
The clinical Psychology module highlights the elements of psychotherapeutic linguistics and the recent theoretical development such as object relation, self-psychology and social system theories. In addition, it will highlight the role of the therapist in-groups.
Research Methodology
This module aims to orient the participants with different methods of research. It will cover the design of surveys in addition to other useful research procedures and material, such as content analysis, personal documents, and archival measures. It will give the participants an idea of the sampling, statistics, and reports.
Community Mental Health
Through this module, the participants will become aware of aspects of community mental health. They will develop their knowledge in prevention, treatment, and rehabilitation of mental health problems in the community. It will discuss gender and mental health as well as social factors of health as well as the politics of communities and health rights within the community.
Mental Health Education
Through this module, the participants will be able to identify the different approaches to community health education, the barriers to health education and health promotion, the politics of health promotion, and the health belief model. The module will explore ways of educating people on their right to be involved in decisions related to their future and their role in determining those futures.
2.2 The Community/Clinical Year
This year focuses on providing participants with wide clinical practice. It is divided
into two modules. The students will do four three-month rotations for the year. Each rotation will have a different focus and will include: Children's issues, Women's issues, General Outpatient Settings, and Hospital or Inpatient setting. There will be four groups of students, each consisting of three, with each group being assigned to one of the four settings.
3. Selection criteria of candidates
The admission process focuses on selecting competent, self-motivated, responsible learners who are qualified for Postgraduate Studies. Selection is based on review and assessment of the individual’s application and supporting documentation. An initial intake of 30 students is planned. Entry criteria are:
a. Specific
- BA in Psychology, Sociology, Medicine, or Nursing. Any other degree in Social Sciences or its equivalent will be considered.
- Good written and spoken English.
- Entrance exam.
- Personal interview based on the applicant’s motivation, maturity, communication, and problem solving skills.
b. Preferred
1-2 years working experience in their fields, mental health, or a related areas.
4. The Teaching Staff
GCMHP will provide the bulk of the local teaching staff and support services, including administration and material resources. Guest lecturers, who design each module, will be brought in from the implementing universities at relevant points throughout the course to teach, supervise, and consult.
5. Strategic Plan
The Long-term plan is to move towards the formation of an Arab Institute of Community Mental Health that will attract international students and researchers. Well-qualified local professionals who will take over the international teacher’s teaching responsibilities will run the institute. In order to prepare the local trainers for this task, continuous education towards Ph.D. and Masters degrees in mental health by distance learning will be organized. This would enable Palestinian trainers to achieve international academic requirements.
