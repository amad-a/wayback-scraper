An Epidemiological Study on the Prevalence of Stress Related Psychiatric Disorders Among Palestinians in the Gaza Strip" ( Dr. Samir Qouta, Dr. Mustafa El Masri, and Dr. Eyad El-Sarraj)
The Research Department of the Gaza Community Mental Health Programme, in conjunction with TPO, Holland, conducted a study in order to explore the prevalence of psychological disorders among the Palestinian people. The sample consisted of 615 people with ages ranging from 16-60. 53% were female, 47% male. The sample was representative of camps, towns, and resettlement areas. The main results of the study were: 1) 19.5% of the entire population suffered from PTSD. Refugees (24.7%) suffered more than citizens (11.7%). 2) 17.6%of the sample suffered from depression, while 13.2% suffered from anxiety. 3) The researchers found the arithmetic mean of psychosis in the Palestinian communityat 4.47 and the standard deviation at 5.37.
