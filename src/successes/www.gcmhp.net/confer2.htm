6th International Refugee Advisory Panel (IRAP) Conference on Forced Migration
 

The International Association for the Study of Forced Migration (IASFM) will organize the 6th IRAP conference on forced migration in coordination with the Gaza Community Mental Health Programme.  The conference will be held in Gaza on 13-16 December 1998.  For information information on themes and registration, see the "Announcement and Call for Papers" below, and visit the IASFM homepage at: http://www.uni-bamberg.de/~ba6ef3/iasfm.htm

Announcement and Call for Papers
6th INTERNATIONAL RESEARCH AND ADVISORY PANEL CONFERENCE ON FORCED MIGRATION
13-16 December 1998
Gaza Community Mental Health Programme, Gaza Strip

 

The International Association for the Study of Forced Migration (IASFM) will convene the sixth International Research and Advisory Panel Conference on Forced Migration (IRAP) in Gaza from 13-16 December 1998. As was the case with the fifth IRAP in Eldoret, Kenya, the conference is being hosted in a focal area of forced migration during the Middle East Peace Talks. It will take place at Rashad El-Shawa Cultural Center in Gaza, Palestine.
Since the first meeting in January 1990, IRAP has developed into the largest and most wide-ranging international conference devoted solely to forced migration issues in the world. Held every two years, the aim of the conference is to provide an interdisciplinary forum for intellectual exchange and communication among academic researchers, refugee professionals and policy makers, and to stimulate debate on research priorities and policy issues. Discussion at the 6th IRAP conference will concentrate on a number of key themes, on which offers of research or discussion papers are urgently sought:
  • Current issues in forced migration: changes in the right to asylum and the right to remain.
  • Host responses: opportunities for participation and integration.
  • Repatriation, reconstruction and social reconciliation.
Within each theme, regional case studies from Palestine or other focal areas of the worldwide problem of forced migration are sought. Also aspects like gender, age (i.e. unaccompanied minors), ethnicity and sexual preference should be presented and integrated into the discussions. After an opening session on 13 December, in which two international experts (one researcher, and one policy-maker) discuss the international developments regarding the right to asylum and the discussion on a right to remain, each of the following three days will be wholly or partly devoted to one of the themes listed above. In each case, plenary sessions to be addressed by invited keynote speakers will be followed by parallel workshops providing a forum for discussion of submitted papers, or allowing for round-table discussion on particular issues or regions. In addition to these formal sessions there will be a series of specialist workshops on issues such as regional activities, teaching, public policy, etc., at the initiative of conference participants. A range of optional excursions will also be available at the end of the conference.
A meeting of the assembly of IASFM members will take place discussing the reports from the board, the IASFM policy plan, future activities and rules of the association. At this meeting, members will elect the officers for the next term.
The host of the 6th IRAP, the Gaza Community Mental Health Programme, is a Palestinian non-governmental institution, established in 1990. GCMHP aims to offer comprehensive community-based mental health services. Research and training constitute an important part of GCMHP. Numerous research projects have been completed and many are still being carried out. The GCMHP hosted several international conferences such as the Third International Conference on "Health and Human Rights" in October 1997. For details, please visit http://www.gcmhp.net.
