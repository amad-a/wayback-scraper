Third International Conference
"Health and Human Rights"


"Letters to President Arafat"
 

Your Excellency, President Arafat

October 15, 1997

Dear President Arafat,

We, the participants of the Third International Conference on Health and Human Rights organised by the Gaza Community Mental Health Programme, were pleased that you were able to open the conference through your representative, Mr. Suliman Shorafa. This conforms with your earlier commitments to human rights as stated in the Declaration of Independence of the State of Palestine and your reaffirmation of this at your meeting with Amnesty International in July 1994, following your arrival in Palestine.

We believe that the Palestinian commitment to human rights was, during the years of your exile, a powerful weapon that assisted in demonstrating the wrongfulness of the Israeli occupation and in securing your arrival and that of the Palestine Liberation Organisation.

The Third Conference has clearly shown the intimate and profound link that exists between health and human rights, and the adverse consequences to health of human rights violations. Assured by this evidence we wish to respectfully request from you the following:

1.To reaffirm your commitment to human rights and at the earliest possible opportunity introduce into law measures that protect and promote democracy and human rights.

2. To resist all pressure to violate or diminish the rule of law and the rights of the Palestinian people.

3. To release all prisoners detained for their political, religious or other beliefs, or speedily bring them to fair trial.

4. To acknowledge the essential role that non-governmental organisations play in a democratic civil society and the importance of ensuring respect for their role in Palestine.

Signed by the participants

October 15, 1997

Dear Prime Minister,

The Gaza Community Mental Health Programme is currently holding its Third International Conference. The theme of this year's conference, Health and Human Rights, has attracted over 400 health professionals, scholars, and social activists. Unfortunately, a number of invited guests were unable to attend the conference because of intransigence on the part of Israeli officials. You could send no stronger signal of your sentiments toward the human rights of the Palestinian people than to block simple travel plans from one area to another for people dedicated to the promotion of human rights and improved health conditions around the world.

During our conference, participants have amply demonstrated the close relationship that exists between health and human rights. Having made this link we feel obliged to raise a number of concerns about ongoing human rights abuses perpetrated by your government. We respectfully submit to you the following requests.

1. All cruel, inhuman, and degrading treatment and torture of prisoners must cease in accordance with Article V of the Universal Declaration of Human Rights.

2. All political prisoners must be released immediately.

3. The severe closure policy begun on March 30, 1993 must cease as it damages the Palestinian economy and frequently endangers the lives of Palestinian patients in need of medical attention.

4. House demolitions and the ongoing expropriation of Palestinian land in the Gaza Strip West Bank and East Jerusalem are grave human rights violations and must cease immediately.

5. The existence of the Israeli settlements is a serious violation of human rights and international law. All settlement building and expansion must cease immediately.

6. Meaningful negotiations must begin at once. Further delay only risks plunging the region back into war and bloodshed. Such a result would be the gravest violation of human rights of all.

Sincerely,

GCMHP and conference participants
